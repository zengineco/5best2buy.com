About Worth The Hunt

Published June 2026 · Last updated 29 June 2026

Worth The Hunt is a curated guide to the food the big platforms can't or won't sell you — small makers, regional specialties, and genuinely hard-to-find things — with an honest pointer to where you can actually buy each one.

I started it for a simple reason: the best food in the world is made by people too small to out-shout the giants. A two-person hot sauce operation. A five-generation family sugarhouse. A worker-owned salami co-op. They make extraordinary things and then disappear under a million sponsored listings. This site is a map back to them.

The one rule that matters

The list is never for sale. No maker pays to be featured, ever. Plenty of the makers here are tiny operations we sought out and fell for on our own — we feature them simply because they're the best at what they do. That's the whole point. If a list can be bought, it's worthless. Ours can't be.

What we're actually up against

It isn't any single store. It's consolidation — the slow machine that buys up the good small brands or quietly pushes them off the shelf. Every shelf here is a small act against that: a way to send your money to the person who actually made the thing, instead of the conglomerate that bought the company that bought them.

How we keep the lights on

We use affiliate links where a maker offers one. If you buy through such a link, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. It never changes who makes a list, it never costs the maker anything, and — to be perfectly clear — many of our favorite makers earn us nothing at all. (See our Affiliate Disclosure.)

That's it. Find the good stuff. Support the people making it.

Who's behind this

My name is Vince Gonzalez, and I build and run Worth The Hunt out of Punta Gorda, Florida — every shelf, every recipe, every ingredient note is mine. I cook the recipes myself and only publish the ones I'd actually make again; Recipe No.01, the potica, is my grandmother's. If you have a question, a correction, or a maker I should know about, write me directly at vince@5best2buy.com. A real person reads every one.
