Straight Answers
Fresh Ricotta FAQ
What makes fresh ricotta different from the supermarket tub?

Real ricotta is hand-dipped whole-milk curd — sweet and delicate and slightly grainy, meant to be eaten fresh. Supermarket tubs are often stabilized with gums to extend shelf life, and they taste gummy and flat by comparison. Once you have had the real thing, the difference is unmistakable.

Do you use ricotta or bechamel in lasagna?

It is regional. Southern-Italian and Italian-American lasagna uses ricotta between the layers. Classic Emilia-Romagna lasagne alla Bolognese uses a bechamel white sauce instead. Both are correct — they are simply different traditions, not a right-and-wrong.

How do I store fresh ricotta?

Fresh ricotta is highly perishable — keep it cold and use it within a few days of opening. Drain off any excess liquid that collects. It does not freeze well without losing its delicate texture, so buy what you will use soon.

What else can I do with ricotta?

Beyond lasagna and stuffed shells: spread it on toast with a drizzle of honey, fold it into pancakes, fill cannoli, or dollop it on pizza. And if you make it yourself, save the whey for baking.
