Straight Answers
Remoulade & German Condiments FAQ
What is German remoulade?

German Remoulade is a creamy, mayonnaise-based sauce flecked with herbs, capers, mustard, and finely chopped pickle — milder and less spicy than the Cajun kind. It's the standard partner for fried fish (Backfisch), fish sandwiches, Frikadellen (German meat patties), and fries. Kühne's herb version is one of the most common brands on German tables.

What's curry ketchup, and what's currywurst?

Curry ketchup is a spiced tomato ketchup seasoned with curry powder — sweeter and more aromatic than plain ketchup. Currywurst is the beloved German street food where sliced bratwurst is smothered in curry ketchup and dusted with more curry powder, usually with fries or a roll. Hela is the classic curry-ketchup brand; it comes in mild to hot.

How is Bavarian sweet mustard different from regular mustard?

Bavarian sweet mustard (süßer Senf) is coarse-ground and sweetened, often with sugar or apple, giving it a mild, malty-sweet flavor rather than the sharp bite of yellow or Dijon. It's the traditional and near-mandatory partner to Weisswurst, the white Bavarian veal sausage. Händlmaier is the benchmark brand. Once you've had it with a soft pretzel, regular mustard feels one-note.

Which German condiment brands are actually independent?

Kühne, Hela (Hamburg), Händlmaier, and Zeisner (Bremen) are all independent family companies, which is why these shops carry them. Some big-name German condiments are owned by multinationals — Thomy, for instance, is a Nestlé brand — so if voting with your dollars matters to you, the family names above are the ones to reach for.
