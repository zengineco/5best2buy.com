Hemp Seeds FAQ
Will hemp seeds get you high or fail a drug test?

No. Hulled hemp hearts come from industrial hemp and contain only trace THC, far too little to have any effect. Reputable brands test to keep THC negligible. Eating hemp hearts is a food, not a drug - it's the seed, not the flower.

What's the difference between hemp seeds and hemp hearts?

'Hemp hearts' are hemp seeds with the crunchy outer hull removed, leaving the soft, nutty inner kernel - that's what most people eat and cook with. Whole hemp seeds keep the fibrous shell, adding fiber but a tougher texture. Most bags labeled hemp hearts or 'hulled hemp seed' are the soft kernels.

Are American-grown hemp seeds new?

As a legal domestic crop, fairly - the 2018 Farm Bill removed hemp from the controlled-substances list, so US farms could grow it at scale again, reviving a crop Kentucky and others grew for generations. Before that, most hemp food came from Canada. Buying US-grown supports that returning domestic industry.

How do I use hemp hearts, and how do I store them?

Eat them raw - sprinkle over yogurt, oatmeal, salads, and smoothies, or blend into dressings; they don't need cooking and have a mild, nutty taste. They're oil-rich, so keep them sealed in the fridge or freezer to prevent rancidity, where they'll last many months.
