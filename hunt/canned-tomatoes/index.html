Canned Tomatoes Worth the Hunt

The single biggest upgrade to any pizza or red sauce isn't technique — it's the tomato. Real San Marzano tomatoes, grown in volcanic soil near Naples and certified DOP, are sweet, low-acid, meaty, and thin-skinned. The catch: most cans labeled "San Marzano" in American stores are imitations, so certification and traceability are everything. Below are the genuinely certified imports and the California-grown organics that rival them — the cans that make the sauce.

Straight Answers
Canned Tomatoes FAQ
How do I know if San Marzano tomatoes are real?

Genuine San Marzano carries DOP certification, which ties it to a protected growing area around Naples and a licensed consortium. Look for the DOP seal, a consortium certification number, and often a lot or batch number printed on the can. Vague wording like "San Marzano style" or "San Marzano variety" grown outside Italy is the tell that it is an imitation.

Whole peeled, crushed, or paste — which should I buy?

Whole peeled are the most versatile and usually the best fruit; you can crush them by hand for a rustic sauce or blend them smooth. Crushed saves a step but hides the quality of the tomato underneath. Paste is concentrated tomato cooked down for depth and body, used a spoonful at a time to build a base rather than as the sauce itself.

Are California-grown tomatoes as good as imported San Marzano?

Often, yes. California's climate grows excellent processing tomatoes, and top organic single-source packers pick at peak ripeness and can within hours. Many chefs now prefer a great California can over a mediocre or faked import. Provenance and how fresh the fruit was when canned matter more than the country on the label.

Should I buy tomatoes packed in juice or in purée?

Tomatoes packed in their own juice let you taste the actual fruit and give you control over the final texture, which is why most cooks prefer them. Purée or added tomato concentrate can mask a lesser tomato and pushes the flavor toward cooked-down and jammy before you have even started. For a bright, fresh sauce, reach for the ones packed in juice.
