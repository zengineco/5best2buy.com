5 Craft Tofu & Tempeh Makers Worth the Hunt

Most grocery tofu is a watery brick from an industrial line. Craft tofu and tempeh — made fresh in small batches from organic or non-GMO soybeans, the traditional way — is a genuinely better food: firmer, nuttier, with actual flavor. The makers below ship it cold or frozen to your door. Here's where to get the real thing.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small, uses organic or non-GMO soy and traditional methods, genuinely cold-ships, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The tofu shelf is industrial bricks; these are the small shops making it fresh and shipping it to you.
No.1 · The Best Place to Start

Hodo Foods

Oakland, CA · organic tofu & fresh yuba

Hodo is an organic, non-GMO maker handcrafting tofu and yuba in Oakland — in fact the only US maker of fresh organic yuba (tofu skin), a genuine delicacy. They ship nationwide weekly, and the quality is restaurant-grade. The best place to start if you want to taste what tofu can actually be.

Why it isn't on AmazonFresh, handcrafted organic tofu and yuba shipped weekly is perishable craft. The freshness and the rare yuba are exactly what a warehouse can't do.

No.2 · Frozen, Shipped Anywhere

Soya Maya

Greensburg, IN · farm-grown organic tempeh

Soya Maya makes small-batch tempeh from organic soybeans grown on their own Indiana farm, and — crucially — ships it frozen nationwide, which solves the perishability problem most makers can't. Real, traditionally-fermented tempeh from the people who grew the beans. A great pick if you want farm-to-ferment tempeh delivered anywhere.

Why it isn't on AmazonTempeh fermented from a farm's own organic soybeans and frozen for shipping is a deliberate, integrated operation. The whole chain is theirs.

See it at Soya Maya →
No.3 · Small Vermont Family

Rhapsody Natural Foods

Cabot, VT · small-batch organic tempeh

Rhapsody is a family-run Vermont maker doing small-batch organic tempeh the traditional way, with an honest, published cold-shipping map. It's a genuinely tiny operation with deep roots in real fermentation. A wonderful pick for organic tempeh from a small family who's been doing it a long time.

Why it isn't on AmazonSmall-batch organic tempeh from one Vermont family is a hands-on, perishable craft. The smallness and the tradition are the value.

See it at Rhapsody →
No.4 · Made to Order

BOStempeh

Somersworth, NH · USDA-organic, 3 ingredients

BOStempeh makes USDA-certified-organic tempeh to order from just three ingredients, shipped with ice packs — clean, fresh, and genuinely small. Made-to-order means it arrives at peak, not after sitting in a warehouse. A great pick for the cleanest, freshest tempeh you can get mailed to you.

Why it isn't on AmazonMade-to-order, three-ingredient organic tempeh shipped on ice is the opposite of a shelf product. The made-to-order freshness is the entire point.

See it at BOStempeh →
No.5 · A Chicago Institution

Phoenix Bean

Chicago, IL · non-GMO, Illinois soy

Phoenix Bean (also known as Jenny's Tofu) is a beloved Chicago maker using non-GMO Illinois-grown soybeans — the kind of small local institution that supplies the city's best restaurants. Their fried and frozen items ship further afield. A great pick for genuinely good tofu with a real local-origin story.

Why it isn't on AmazonA small Chicago tofu maker using local soybeans is a regional institution, not a national brand. The local soy and the freshness are what make it.
