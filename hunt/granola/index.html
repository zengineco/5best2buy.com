5 Small-Batch Granola Makers Worth the Hunt

Most grocery granola is dessert pretending to be breakfast — as much sugar as a candy bar, plus seed oils to make it cling. Small-batch granola is a different food: real oats, real nuts, sweetened lightly and honestly, baked in small trays so it actually tastes toasted rather than candied. The makers below keep the sugar in check and the ingredient lists short. Here's where to buy them.

Straight Answers
Granola FAQ
How much sugar should good granola actually have?

Most grocery granola carries as much sugar as a candy bar, often well into double digits of grams per serving. A well-made small-batch granola keeps it honest, lightly sweetened so the oats and nuts do the work; Nana Joes lands around three grams a serving, and Struesli adds no sugar at all. Read the label per serving, not per package, and watch that the serving size is a realistic amount rather than a token quarter cup.

Why does the oil in granola matter?

Many mass-market granolas use cheap seed oils to make the clusters cling and cut cost, which is part of why the ingredient list runs long. Small makers often choose a fat with more character and a cleaner profile instead; Jamie's Farm bakes with ghee, and Marge uses olive oil, both of which add real flavor rather than just binding. If you are avoiding seed oils, the oil line on the label is the first thing to check.

What makes small-batch granola taste different from the boxed kind?

It is baked in small trays at lower volume, so it toasts evenly and tastes roasted rather than candied, and the ingredient list is short and recognizable. Bigger operations bake in bulk and lean on sugar and oil to paper over uneven baking. The result in the bowl is more distinct clusters, real nut and oat flavor, and a crunch that comes from the bake rather than from a sugar shell.

How should you store granola to keep it crunchy?

Keep it in an airtight container at room temperature, away from heat and humidity, both of which soften the clusters and can turn the nuts and oils stale. Because these are real-ingredient granolas without heavy preservatives, buy amounts you will finish within a few weeks rather than stockpiling. If a batch loses its crunch, a short spell in a low oven crisps it back up before it goes into the bowl.
