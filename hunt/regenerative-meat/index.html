5 Regenerative Meat Companies Worth the Hunt

Most supermarket meat comes from a feedlot and a supply chain built to hide itself. The ranches and farms below are the opposite — pasture-raised, grass-fed, and managed to heal the land instead of strip it, and they'll ship it frozen to your door. You pay more, and you're paying for animals raised right on farms that will tell you exactly how. Here's where to order.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every operation here is independent, raises or sources its animals humanely and regeneratively, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The thing we're up against is the feedlot system and the consolidation behind it. Nothing here is endangered; this is pasture-raised beef, pork, poultry, and managed game.
No.1 · The Transparency Leader

White Oak Pastures

Bluffton, GA · multi-species regenerative farm

White Oak Pastures is the Georgia farm that brought a dying town back to life by doing regenerative agriculture at real scale — multi-species, zero-waste, with published data showing their beef can be carbon-negative. It's one of the most transparent, animal-welfare-serious operations in the country, and the meat is excellent. If you want to vote with your dollar for how farming should work, start here.

Why it isn't on AmazonA radically transparent, multi-species regenerative farm is a whole philosophy, not a SKU. The farm itself is what you're buying.

No.2 · The Wildest-Raised Beef

Alderspring Ranch

May, ID · certified organic, 100% grass-fed

Alderspring is a small Idaho family ranch that does something almost no one else does — they herd their cattle on horseback through wild mountain range all summer, which is about as far from a feedlot as beef can get. It's certified organic, 100% grass-fed, and the care shows in the meat. The strongest small-ranch story on this list.

Why it isn't on AmazonHerding organic cattle through wild range by hand produces a small amount of exceptional beef. That's the opposite of a commodity supply chain.

No.3 · Many Small Farms at Once

Grass Roots Farmers' Cooperative

Clarksville, AR · co-op of 40+ regenerative farms

Grass Roots pools more than 40 small American regenerative farms into one co-op, so a single order supports a whole network of family farms doing it right — pasture-raised, no antibiotics, no added hormones, full transparency. It's the easiest way to back many small farms at once instead of one big operation. A great default for a regular meat order you can feel good about.

Why it isn't on AmazonA cooperative of small family farms exists specifically to route around the consolidated meat industry. It's built to be the alternative to the big platforms, not to live on them.

No.4 · The Family Farm

Seven Sons Farms

Roanoke, IN · family pastured beef, pork & poultry

Seven Sons is a seven-brother Indiana family farm doing 100% grass-finished beef plus pastured pork and chicken, with a loyal subscriber base built on doing things the slow, right way. It's a genuinely family-run operation you can get to know, and the quality reflects people who care about their animals and their land. A solid all-around pick for a household.

Why it isn't on AmazonA family farm raising grass-finished animals on its own pasture sells what its land can produce, not what a warehouse demands. The family is the brand.

No.5 · The Bison & Elk Pick

Force of Nature

Austin, TX · regenerative ancestral meats

Force of Nature focuses on regenerative-sourced meats with the widest range here — including bison and elk blends — backed by a real Land to Market regenerative-sourcing program. It's the most adventurous pick if you want to cook with meats beyond the usual, from a company taking regenerative sourcing seriously. Good for trying something new without guessing about how it was raised.
