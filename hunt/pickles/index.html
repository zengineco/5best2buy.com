Straight Answers
Pickles & Ferments FAQ
What's the difference between fermented pickles and vinegar (quick) pickles?

Quick or "fresh-pack" pickles are cucumbers steeped in a hot vinegar brine and often pasteurized, which makes them crisp and sour but biologically dead. Fermented pickles are submerged in a salt brine and left for days or weeks, where naturally present bacteria convert sugars into lactic acid — the sourness comes from that fermentation, not added vinegar. The fermented kind carries live probiotics; the vinegar kind does not.

How can I tell if pickles are truly fermented and alive?

Look for words like raw, live, unpasteurized, or "keep refrigerated," and check that they are sold cold rather than off a warm shelf. The ingredient list should be short — vegetables, salt, water, spices — with no vinegar and no preservatives. Live ferments are naturally cloudy and may bubble slightly when opened, which is a good sign, not spoilage.

Do live ferments have to be refrigerated?

Yes — raw, live pickles, kraut, and kimchi are perishable and must be kept cold, which is why the makers here cold-ship them to your door. Refrigeration slows the fermentation so the product stays at the flavor and texture you want rather than souring further. Left warm, they keep fermenting, can build pressure in the jar, and eventually turn mushy or overly sour.

Why is the brine cloudy, and should I keep the vegetables submerged?

Cloudy brine is normal for a live ferment — it comes from the lactic-acid bacteria doing their work and is a sign the cultures are active. Keep the vegetables pressed down under the brine, because the liquid protects them from air and preserves both crunch and safety. If some kraut or kimchi rises above the surface, push it back under or spoon a little brine over it before resealing.
