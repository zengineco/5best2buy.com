5 Small-Batch Ferment Makers Worth the Hunt

Most grocery pickles and kraut are pasteurized vinegar brine — dead, no probiotics, no real fermentation. True ferments are raw, alive, and made the slow way, usually with nothing but vegetables and salt. The makers below ship raw, live ferments cold to your door. Here's where to get the genuinely fermented stuff.

How this list works. Every maker here is small, ferments raw and live (no vinegar shortcut), and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The pickle aisle is pasteurized vinegar; these are the people doing real, probiotic, traditional fermentation and cold-shipping it alive.
No.1 · Farm-to-Ferment Organic

Oregon Brineworks

Hood River, OR · certified organic, vinegar-free

Oregon Brineworks makes certified-organic, vinegar-free raw ferments — pickles, kraut, kimchi, beet kvass — from vegetables grown on their own organic farm. It's farm-to-ferment in the fullest sense, raw and alive with probiotics. A great place to start if you want genuinely organic, genuinely fermented vegetables.

Why it isn't on AmazonCertified-organic raw ferments from a farm's own vegetables are perishable, living food. The farm and the live cultures are the product.

No.2 · Never Fermented in Plastic

OlyKraut

Olympia, WA · B-Corp, woman-owned, organic

OlyKraut is a woman-owned, B-Corp maker doing raw organic sauerkraut and ferments — and they're proud that they never ferment in plastic, which is rarer than it should be. It's about as clean and values-driven as this category gets. A great pick if the how-it's-made matters as much as the taste.

Why it isn't on AmazonRaw organic kraut fermented in glass by a B-Corp is a deliberate, careful operation. The values are baked into how it's made.

San Francisco, CA · vegan, organic, wild-fermented

Volcano Kimchi is a founder-led San Francisco maker doing vegan, organic, wild-fermented kimchi with no added sugar — a genuinely distinctive take using traditional Korean method. It's bright, funky, and alive in a way jarred kimchi never is. A wonderful pick for real, small-batch kimchi.

Why it isn't on AmazonWild-fermented vegan kimchi made in small batches is a hands-on traditional craft. The wild fermentation and the founder's hand are what make it.

Finger Lakes, NY · raw, organic, since 2013

Food and Ferments is a small Finger Lakes maker doing raw, never-heated ferments — classic kraut, garlic dill pickles, traditional kimchi, beet kvass — from organic ingredients since 2013. It's the real, traditional, raw thing, shipped quickly. A great pick for honest, alive ferments from a small New York operation.
