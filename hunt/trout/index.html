Straight Answers
Trout & Freshwater Fish FAQ
Is farmed trout safe and sustainable?

US rainbow trout is one of the most sustainable farmed fish — Monterey Bay Seafood Watch rates it a 'Best Choice.' It's raised in clean flow-through freshwater on a low-impact diet. Sunburst's spring-water, antibiotic-free approach is the gold standard.

What does lake whitefish taste like?

Mild, slightly sweet, and delicate with medium-firm flesh — a Great Lakes staple that's excellent pan-fried, smoked, or in chowder. Its roe (whitefish caviar) is a regional delicacy. It rarely travels far from the lakes, which is why shipping direct matters.

Why can't I find freshwater fish at the grocery store?

National seafood distribution is built around ocean fish and a few farmed species; regional freshwater fish from small Great Lakes and river operations don't fit that supply chain. Buying direct from the families who catch it is essentially the only way to get it.

How should I cook trout?

Whole trout loves a hot pan or grill — stuff the cavity with lemon and herbs and cook skin-on until crisp. Fillets take just a few minutes a side. Because these are so fresh, keep it simple: butter, salt, lemon, and don't overcook past the point where it flakes.
