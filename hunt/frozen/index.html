Straight Answers
Regional Frozen FAQ
How does regional food survive shipping across the country?

These makers freeze the food solid and pack it in an insulated box with dry ice or gel packs, usually shipping early in the week by expedited carrier so it arrives while still frozen. Dry ice can keep a well-packed box cold for a couple of days, which is why most makers avoid shipping into a weekend. When it lands, you move it straight to the freezer.

Why does freezing preserve quality instead of ruining it?

Freezing food quickly at the peak of freshness locks in flavor and texture by keeping ice crystals small, so the boudin, dumplings, or chile taste close to the day they were made. That is very different from food that sat in a case for weeks before freezing. For items like flash-frozen Hatch chile or sealed soup dumplings, freezing is genuinely the best way to move them, not a compromise.

What do I do when a dry-ice box arrives?

Open it in a ventilated space and do not handle the dry ice with bare hands, since it can burn skin. Check that the food is still frozen or at least very cold and firm; most makers pack enough coolant for the transit time. Get everything into your freezer promptly, and follow the maker's guidance on any item that arrived partially thawed.

Can I refreeze regional frozen food if it thawed a little?

As a general rule, food that still has ice crystals or is at refrigerator temperature can be refrozen, though the texture may soften slightly. Food that has fully thawed and sat warm should be cooked and eaten rather than refrozen. When in doubt, follow the specific maker's instructions, since a raw boudin and a cooked dumpling have different margins.
