Straight Answers
Hermit Crab Food FAQ
What do hermit crabs eat?

Land hermit crabs are omnivorous scavengers. A good diet is varied: some commercial hermit crab food plus fresh fruits and vegetables, a protein source (like bloodworms or unseasoned meat/fish), leaf litter, and a constant calcium source such as cuttlebone. They also need both fresh dechlorinated water and marine salt water.

Are commercial hermit crab foods safe?

Read the label. Many older pet-store hermit crab foods contain preservatives like ethoxyquin and copper sulfate that experienced keepers avoid. Choose preservative-free foods, and treat any commercial food as one part of a varied whole-food diet rather than the entire menu.

Do hermit crabs need calcium?

Yes. Hermit crabs need a steady calcium source to build their exoskeleton and molt successfully. Cuttlebone, crushed oyster shell, or a calcium supplement should always be available in the enclosure, alongside their food.

Do hermit crabs need salt water?

Yes — they need access to both dechlorinated fresh water and marine (salt) water at all times. Use an aquarium marine salt mix made for hermit crabs or saltwater fish, never table salt. Both water dishes are essential for their health and shell selection.
