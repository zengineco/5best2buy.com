Straight Answers
Goat Feed FAQ
What do goats eat?

Goats are browsers — the base of their diet is hay, pasture, and browse (leaves, shrubs, weeds). Supplement with a free-choice loose goat mineral and, for milkers or growing kids, a measured amount of goat grain. Never rely on grain as the main food.

Why do goats need special minerals?

Goats need copper — a lot of it relative to other livestock — and a goat-specific loose mineral supplies it. Copper deficiency causes poor coats, health, and fertility. Loose minerals are absorbed better than a hard block, so most keepers offer them free-choice.

Can goats eat sheep feed or sheep minerals?

No. Sheep feed and minerals are deliberately low in copper because copper is toxic to sheep — but that same low copper causes deficiency and disease in goats. Always use goat-specific (or goat-safe) minerals, never sheep products.

How much grain should a goat get?

As little as possible for the goat's needs. Goats are built for forage; too much grain causes bloat, and in males, dangerous urinary calculi. Milkers, pregnant does, and growing kids get measured grain, but hay and browse should always be the foundation.
