Straight Answers
Pizza Flour FAQ
What is Tipo 00 flour and why is it used for pizza?

Tipo 00 is an Italian grading for how finely the wheat is milled, the 00 being the finest, talcum-fine grind. That fineness plus a gluten structure tuned for extensibility lets the dough stretch thin without tearing, hold a long cold ferment, and blister into a leopard-spotted, chewy crust under high heat. It is milled for one job. That is why a real 00 behaves completely differently in your hands than ordinary flour.

Is pizza flour the same as bread flour?

No, and this is a common and costly mix-up. The 00 grade is about grind fineness, not protein, and a pizza 00 is tuned for a thin, extensible, fast-charring crust. Bread flour is a coarser grind chosen for strength and a tall rise. Use bread flour for pizza and you get bread that happens to be flat. Some mills here make both; this shelf is specifically their pizza 00, a different product from their bread flour.

Can you make good pizza in a home oven with 00 flour?

Yes, though 00 is happiest at very high heat. A traditional Neapolitan bake wants a screaming-hot oven, ideally a pizza steel or stone preheated as hot as your oven goes, to get the quick char that 00 is built for. In a standard home oven the flour still gives you a more extensible, tender dough than all-purpose. A long, cool ferment, which a good 00 holds well, does much of the flavor work regardless of oven.

Does American-milled 00 work as well as imported Italian flour?

A finely milled American 00 loses nothing to imports but the ocean freight, and buying direct keeps it fresher than a bag that crossed the Atlantic and sat. The mills here grind to genuine 00 fineness from American wheat, some from heritage varieties for a different character. Beware the grocery bag simply labeled pizza flour, which is often just repackaged all-purpose. Look for a real Type 00 grade.
