5 Artisan Pasta Makers Worth the Hunt

Supermarket pasta is extruded fast through Teflon dies and flash-dried, which leaves it slick and bland. Artisan pasta is pushed through bronze dies that give it a rough, sauce-grabbing surface, then dried slowly at low temperature — often from heritage or organic grains. The difference in the bowl is real. The makers below are small or import direct. Here's where to get pasta worth cooking.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small or imports direct, uses bronze dies and real grain, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The pasta aisle is fast-extruded commodity wheat; these are the people doing it the slow, textured, old-world way.
No.1 · The Small-Maker Standard

Sfoglini

Coxsackie, NY · bronze-die, organic semolina

Sfoglini is a small New York maker doing bronze-die, organic-semolina pasta, including cult collaborations and heritage-grain runs in shapes you won't find elsewhere. It's the gold standard for a small US pasta maker that actually ships nationally, and the texture is a genuine upgrade. A great place to start with artisan pasta.

Why it isn't on AmazonBronze-die, slow-dried, organic pasta in small runs is craft made by people who care about texture. The method is the whole difference.

No.2 · Hundreds of Cuts

Pappardelle's Pasta

Denver, CO · family-run, 200+ artisan cuts

Pappardelle's is a family-run Denver maker with an enormous range of artisan cuts — more than 200 — including heritage, ancient-grain, and gluten-free lines. The variety is genuinely fun, and it's all made with real care. A great pick if you want to explore shapes and flavors way beyond the grocery aisle.

No.3 · Ancient Sicilian Grains

Bona Furtuna

Sicily · organic heirloom-wheat pasta

Bona Furtuna imports pasta made from organic ancient Sicilian grains — varieties like Russello and Perciasacchi — grown on a regenerative estate. It's the heritage-grain, single-origin pick, with a real farm and a real story behind it. A great choice if you want to taste what old-world grains actually bring to pasta.

Why it isn't on AmazonPasta from organic ancient grains grown on one regenerative estate is tied to specific land and seed. The heritage grain is exactly what a commodity box leaves out.

No.4 · The Farmers Make the Pasta

Guardian Grains

North Dakota · regenerative, stone-milled

Guardian Grains makes farm-direct pasta from regeneratively grown, stone-milled heritage whole grains — the most anti-Amazon farm story of the bunch, where the people growing the grain are the people making the pasta. It's hearty, whole-grain, and genuinely traceable to the field. A wonderful pick if you want to support regenerative farming directly.

Why it isn't on AmazonStone-milled, regeneratively grown pasta made by the farmers themselves is farm-direct in the fullest sense. The whole chain is the value.

No.5 · The Small-Batch Purist

Della Terra Pasta

Oklahoma City, OK · bronze-die, slow-dried

Della Terra is a genuinely tiny old-world maker using bronze dies and slow drying, founded by a chef committed to doing it the traditional, painstaking way. It's the small-batch purist pick — pasta as craft, made by hand in small quantities. Worth seeking out for the texture alone.
