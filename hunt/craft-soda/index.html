5 Craft Sodas & Mixers Worth the Hunt

Big soda is high-fructose corn syrup and lab flavoring. Craft soda and real mixers are the opposite — actual botanicals, real cane sugar or none at all, made in small batches by people who care what goes in the glass. The makers below cover everything from prebiotic sparkling tonics to booze-free cocktails to genuinely good fruit mixers. Here's where to get them.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small, builds drinks from real ingredients, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The soda aisle is owned by a couple of giants selling corn syrup; these are the people making something you'd actually want to drink, by hand.
No.1 · The Everyday Upgrade

wildwonder

Oakland, CA · botanical prebiotic tonics

wildwonder makes botanical, prebiotic sparkling tonics rooted in the founder's Asian heritage — real botanicals, gut-friendly, and only lightly sweetened with cane sugar. They're genuinely refreshing in a way most "healthy" sodas aren't, and the flavors are interesting. A great everyday upgrade from soda that actually tastes good.

Hudson Valley, NY · booze-free craft cocktails

Curious Elixirs makes booze-free craft cocktails — small-batch, built with real botanicals and adaptogens — for when you want something complex and grown-up without the alcohol. They're far more interesting than the usual mocktail, with real bitter, herbal depth. The pick for a serious non-alcoholic drink.

No.3 · The Sophisticated Soda

Casamara Club

Detroit, MI · botanical "leisure sodas"

Casamara Club makes small-batch botanical "leisure sodas" — non-alcoholic amaro-style sodas that are bitter, bright, and genuinely sophisticated. It's arguably the purest craft-soda pick here: low-key, beautifully made, the kind of thing you serve someone to surprise them. A standout for anyone bored of sweet soda.

Why it isn't on AmazonBotanical amaro-style sodas in small batches are a niche craft. The bitter-sophisticated profile is exactly what mass soda won't make.

Brooklyn, NY · fresh ginger, unfiltered

Bruce Cost Ginger Ale is made with fresh ginger — actual ginger, unfiltered, so you can see it settle — and real cane sugar, nothing from concentrate. It's got a genuine ginger bite that mass ginger ale completely lacks. A revelation if you thought you didn't like ginger ale, and a great non-alcoholic option with real character.

Greater Boston · craft soda & cocktail mixer

Splash it into soda water for an honest fruit soda, or into a cocktail. It's the simple, real-ingredient pick for anyone who reads labels. Refreshingly uncomplicated.
