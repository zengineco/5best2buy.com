Straight Answers
Craft Soda & Mixers FAQ
What makes a soda craft rather than just small-brand?

The dividing line is the ingredient list. Craft soda is built from real botanicals, fruit, and cane sugar (or nothing) rather than high-fructose corn syrup and lab flavoring, and it is made in small batches by people making recipe decisions by taste. Some are barely sweet, some are bitter and amaro-style, some are prebiotic tonics — but the common thread is that you can pronounce what is in the can. Brand size matters less than what is in the glass.

Are prebiotic sodas actually good for your gut, or is that marketing?

Prebiotic sodas add plant fibers like inulin or chicory root that feed the bacteria already in your gut, which is a real mechanism, not pure marketing. That said, a can is not medicine, and too much added fiber at once can cause bloating for some people. Treat them as a genuinely better-for-you swap for sugary soda rather than a supplement. The bigger honest win is far less sugar than a conventional soda.

What is the difference between a craft soda and a mixer?

A craft soda is finished — pour it over ice and drink it. A mixer, like a fruit syrup or a tonic base, is concentrated and meant to be diluted with soda water or spirits, so it stretches further and lets you control the sweetness and fizz. Many mixers double as a soda if you just add sparkling water. If you like tinkering with your own drinks, a real mixer is the more versatile buy.

Do non-alcoholic craft cocktails and leisure sodas contain any alcohol?

These are made without spirits, so they are non-alcoholic drinks, not de-alcoholized wine or beer. What they borrow from cocktails is the complexity — real bitters, botanicals, and adaptogens that give the bitter, herbal depth an ordinary soda lacks — without the alcohol. They are built for someone who wants a grown-up drink with dinner and a clear head after. Always check the specific label if a zero-alcohol guarantee matters to you.
