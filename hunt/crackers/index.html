5 Artisan Cracker Makers Worth the Hunt

The cracker is the one part of a good cheese board people usually cheap out on, which is a shame, because a real cracker matters as much as the cheese it carries. The bakers below make crackers and crisps the slow way — sourdough, seeds, whole grains, actual butter — instead of the shelf-stable, additive-heavy versions that dominate a grocery aisle. Most are small enough that they ship the boxes themselves. Here's where to buy them.

Straight Answers
Artisan Cracker FAQ
What makes an artisan cracker better than a grocery-aisle box?

The difference is in the ingredient list and the method. Artisan crackers are built from real things, sourdough, whole grains, seeds, actual butter, and baked slowly in small batches, where mass-market crackers lean on refined flour, palm oil, and preservatives so they can sit on a shelf for a year. That means real flavor and structure that holds up under a topping instead of a bland, uniform vehicle. On a cheese board, the cracker matters as much as the cheese.

Which crackers are best for a cheese board?

Look for a sturdy cracker with a fairly plain profile that supports cheese rather than fighting it, then add one or two with more character for variety. A whole-grain or seeded cracker like Potter's or Cult Crackers carries a soft cheese well; a buttery oatcake like Effie's stands up to a strong blue or aged cheddar. Flavored crisps such as Maine Crisp's fig and thyme bring their own accent for pairing with milder cheeses.

Are there good gluten-free artisan crackers?

Yes, and several here are genuinely good rather than a compromise. Top Seedz and Cult Crackers make organic seed crackers that hold structure without wheat, and Maine Crisp uses buckwheat for a nutty depth that most gluten-free crackers lack. The key is a cracker built around seeds or an alternative grain from the start, not one engineered to imitate a wheat cracker, which is where the crumbly reputation comes from.

How should you store artisan crackers to keep them crisp?

Because these are baked without the additives that keep supermarket crackers indefinitely crisp, keep them in an airtight container or their resealable box, away from humidity, which is the enemy of a good cracker. If a box softens, a few minutes in a low oven usually brings the snap back. Buy what you will eat within a reasonable window rather than stockpiling, since freshness is part of what you are paying for.
