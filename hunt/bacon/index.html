5 Small-Smokehouse Bacon Makers Worth the Hunt

Most grocery bacon is commodity pork, wet-injected with brine and "smoke flavor" to add weight and speed, then sliced thin. The smokehouses below do it the slow way: heritage-breed pork, a real salt cure that takes days, and actual hardwood smoke. It costs more and there's less of it, because dry-curing removes water instead of adding it. Here's where to buy the good stuff.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small or independent, uses real pork and real smoke, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. We weight sourcing heavily on this shelf: heritage breeds, humane raising, and honest curing over the commodity default. Where a maker uses a traditional cure rather than a "nitrate-free" one, we say so plainly.
No.1 · The Regenerative One

REP Provisions

Bentonville, AR · regenerative heritage pork

REP Provisions sources heritage Duroc and Berkshire pork from regenerative, glyphosate-free farms and smokes its bacon Southern-smokehouse style in small batches. It's the strongest sourcing story on this list that also happens to be genuinely earnable to feature, so it's a clean place to start. The pork has real fat character, and because the curing is honest, it cooks down to bacon instead of a puddle of injected water.

Why it isn't on AmazonRegenerative, named-farm sourcing is a set of relationships, not a commodity supply line. That traceability is the whole point, and it's the part a generic listing can't carry.

No.2 · The Whole-Animal Butcher

Porter Road

Nashville, TN · pasture-raised, dry-cured

Porter Road is a real butcher shop that buys whole pasture-raised animals from nearby farms and breaks them down itself, which is why its bacon is dry-cured and hardwood-smoked rather than pumped full of brine. Buying from a whole-animal butcher means your bacon is part of an animal someone used entirely, not a commodity belly bought on the spot market. The thick-cut is excellent.

Why it isn't on AmazonWhole-animal butchery only works at human scale with real farm relationships. It's the antithesis of the commodity cut that fills a big platform's listing.

See it at Porter Road →
No.3 · The Heritage Duroc

Vande Rose Farms

Iowa · applewood-smoked artisan dry cure

Vande Rose raises heritage Duroc pork in Iowa and dry-cures its bacon with a proprietary rub before a real applewood smoke, and it has the awards to back the claim. Duroc is prized for marbling, so the bacon stays tender and renders cleanly. This is special-occasion bacon — the kind you cook slowly and pay attention to — and it's worth seeking out for that reason.

Why it isn't on AmazonA breed-specific, dry-cured product made in limited runs doesn't scale to a commodity shelf. It ships direct and through specialty channels instead.

See it at Vande Rose →
No.4 · The Small Western Butcher

Beck & Bulow

Santa Fe, NM · heritage-breed, uncured

Beck & Bulow is a small Santa Fe butcher selling heritage-breed, nitrate-free thick-cut bacon alongside grass-fed and game meats. It's a genuinely small operation, and the bacon reflects it: simple ingredients, real smoke, no industrial shortcuts. If you want a single source for clean bacon plus the occasional bison or elk, this is a practical place to set up.

Why it isn't on AmazonA small regional butcher's whole model is direct sourcing and cold-chain shipping from one shop. That's not something a third-party commodity listing replicates.

See it at Beck & Bulow →
No.5 · The Legend

Benton's Smoky Mountain

Madisonville, TN · hickory-smoked country bacon

Benton's is the smokehouse chefs name when they talk about American bacon — Allan Benton's hickory-smoked, salt-and-brown-sugar-cured country bacon is intense, deeply smoky, and a little wild compared to anything from a store. One honest note: this is a traditional cure that uses sodium nitrite, so it isn't a "nitrate-free" bacon and we won't pretend otherwise. What it is, is some of the best-tasting bacon in the country, smoked over real wood by a small operation that earned its reputation the long way.

Why it isn't on AmazonIt's made in small batches and smoked hard the old way, with a following that buys it direct. The flavor is a specific maker's hand, not a commodity profile.
