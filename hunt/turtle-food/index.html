Turtle & Tortoise Food Worth the Hunt

Feed the wrong thing (or a cheap all-purpose pellet) and you get shell deformities, organ disease, and a short life. The makers below are the zoo-standard, species-appropriate diets that experienced keepers and reptile vets trust — plus grazing gels that make feeding a picky tortoise easy. Straight Answers
Turtle & Tortoise Food FAQ
What do pet turtles eat?

It depends on the species. Aquatic turtles (like red-eared sliders) are omnivores that need a complete pellet plus protein and leafy greens. Tortoises are herbivores that need grasses, weeds, leafy greens, and a grass-based pellet to fill nutritional gaps.

Can tortoises eat lettuce?

Skip iceberg — it's basically water with no nutrition. Dark leafy greens, grasses, and edible weeds are good, and a complete grassland pellet like Mazuri or Zoo Med fills the gaps a produce-only diet leaves.

Are Mazuri and Zoo Med good turtle foods?

Yes. Both are nutritionally complete, widely used by zoos and experienced keepers, and formulated for specific needs (aquatic turtle vs. tortoise, standard vs. low-starch). They're reliable staples, not commodity filler.

How much should I feed my turtle?

For aquatic turtles, a portion roughly the size of their head a few times a week, plus greens. For tortoises, offer daily greens and grasses with a measured amount of pellets. Overfeeding leads to shell and organ problems.
