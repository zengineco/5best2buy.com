Straight Answers
Rice Noodle FAQ
What kind of rice noodle do I need for pho?

You want the flat rice stick called banh pho — a medium-width dried flat noodle. Soak it, then blanch it briefly just before serving so it stays tender, not mushy.

What are the main types of rice noodle?

Flat rice sticks come in several widths for pho and pad see ew; thin round rice vermicelli (bun) is used for noodle bowls and spring rolls; and wide fresh rice noodles are used for chow fun. Width and shape matter to the dish.

Fresh or dried rice noodles?

Dried are shelf-stable and what most home cooks use — soak them in warm water then briefly cook. Fresh rice noodles are more tender and used quickly, but they are perishable and harder to find outside Asian markets.

Are rice noodles gluten-free?

Pure rice noodles are naturally gluten-free — but check the label, since some blends add wheat or tapioca. Look for rice as the main or only ingredient.
