Straight Answers
Chicken Feed FAQ
What is the best feed for backyard laying hens?

A complete layer feed with about 16-17% protein and added calcium for strong eggshells. Certified-organic, soy-free options are the healthiest choice for many keepers, and the quality shows in the birds and the eggs.

What is the difference between starter, grower, and layer feed?

Starter feed is high-protein for chicks, grower feed is for growing pullets, and layer feed adds the extra calcium laying hens need for eggshells. Feed the stage your birds are in — layer feed's calcium is too high for chicks.

Is organic, soy-free chicken feed worth it?

For many backyard keepers, yes. It avoids GMO grains and soy, which some people prefer for themselves and their birds, and healthier hens tend to lay better eggs. It costs more than commodity feed, but it's a small flock and a real quality difference.

Do backyard chickens need grit and oyster shell?

Yes — offer both free-choice alongside feed. Grit helps chickens grind their food in the gizzard, and crushed oyster shell gives laying hens extra calcium for strong eggshells. Birds take what they need.
