5 Artisan Cheeses Worth the Hunt

Grocery "artisan" cheese is usually a factory wheel with a rustic label. The real thing comes from small creameries — often a single farm with its own animals — and a lot of them now ship cold, straight to your door. The makers below are among the best in the country, and a couple are working farmsteads where the cows are visible from the make room. Here's where to order them.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is a small creamery or a serious independent monger, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. What fills the grocery cheese case isn't one store; it's consolidation that rewards bland, shelf-stable wheels over living, farm-based cheese. These are the people keeping the real thing alive.

The West Coast Benchmark

Cowgirl Creamery

Point Reyes, CA · organic artisan
$$★★★★★🚛 Ground only

Cowgirl Creamery helped define West Coast artisan cheese — organic, soft-ripened cheeses like the Mt Tam and Red Hawk that are benchmarks of the style. They ship nationwide and have spent decades treating dairy as something to do thoughtfully rather than cheaply. A great pick when you want soft, bloomy, special-occasion cheese with a real point of view.

Straight Answers
Artisan Cheese FAQ
What actually makes a cheese "artisan" or "farmstead"?

Artisan means the cheese is made in small batches largely by hand, but the word is unregulated, which is why grocery "artisan" wheels are often factory-made. Farmstead is stricter: it means the cheese is made on the same farm that raises the animals whose milk it uses, so the herd is genuinely part of the product. Several makers here are true farmsteads where the animals are visible from the make room.

Is raw-milk cheese safe, and what does it taste like?

Raw-milk cheese is legal to sell in the US when it has been aged at least 60 days, which is why so much traditional cheese falls in that aged category. Skipping pasteurization preserves the native bacteria and enzymes in the milk, giving raw-milk cheese more complex, place-specific flavor. If you are pregnant or immune-compromised, ask a cheesemonger, since soft fresh cheeses are the ones to be cautious with.

How do I store artisan cheese once it arrives?

Keep it in the warmest part of the fridge, ideally a drawer, wrapped in cheese paper or loosely in wax paper rather than tight plastic, which suffocates a living rind and traps moisture. Re-wrap in fresh paper each time you cut it. A little surface mold on a hard aged cheese can be trimmed away; discard soft cheeses that mold unexpectedly.

How should I serve it, and how far ahead?

Take cheese out of the fridge about an hour before serving so it comes to room temperature — cold mutes aroma and flavor, and a good wheel tastes noticeably better warmed to the room. Serve a range of styles, a bloomy, an aged, a blue, and cut each cheese with its own knife so flavors don't cross. If you are unsure where to start, an assembled selection from a monger is the easiest way to taste breadth.
