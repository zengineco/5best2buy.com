Straight Answers
Fresh Truffles (Seasonal) FAQ
When can I actually buy fresh truffles?

It's tightly seasonal: Oregon black run roughly December-March, Oregon white November-January, and European winter truffles October-March. Summer truffles fill part of the gap. Outside these windows you'll see 'sold out' — that's the season, not the business closing. Buy when your target truffle is in season and order for overnight delivery.

Why are fresh truffles so expensive?

They can't be farmed at scale the way mushrooms can — most are wild-foraged (often with trained dogs), they're intensely perishable, and they must be air-shipped overnight to arrive usable. Scarcity plus speed plus a short season is the whole price story. A little goes a long way, which softens the blow.

How do I use and store a fresh truffle?

Use it fast — fresh truffles fade within a week or so. Store it in a sealed jar in the fridge, often nestled with eggs or rice, which absorb the aroma (bonus truffled eggs). Shave it raw over hot pasta, risotto, or eggs at the very end; heat destroys the aroma, so never cook it hard.

Are Oregon truffles as good as European ones?

They're different, not lesser. Oregon white truffles are prized for a bright, garlicky-fruity aroma and cost far less than Alba whites; Oregon blacks are earthier. Many chefs love them precisely because they're domestic, fresher on arrival (less transit), and a fraction of the import price. Try both if you can.
