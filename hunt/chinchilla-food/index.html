Best Chinchilla Food Worth the Hunt

Chinchillas evolved in the high, dry Andes on tough, fibrous plants — which means their digestive system is exquisitely sensitive to sugar, fat, and moisture. The colorful "chinchilla mix" full of dried fruit and seeds is genuinely dangerous to them: they pick out the sweet bits, skip the nutrition, and end up with bloat and worse. What a chinchilla actually needs is dull and simple — unlimited fresh timothy hay and a plain, uniform timothy-based pellet. The makers below do exactly that, done right.

Straight Answers
Chinchilla Food FAQ
What do chinchillas eat?

Mostly grass hay — unlimited timothy hay is the foundation of the diet — plus a small measured amount of a plain, quality timothy-based pellet. Treats should be rare and simple; their desert-adapted gut is easily upset by sugar and fat.

How much pellet food should a chinchilla eat per day?

About one to two tablespoons of pellets per day is plenty. Hay, on the other hand, should always be available in unlimited amounts — it does most of the nutritional and dental work.

What is the best hay for chinchillas?

Timothy hay for adult chinchillas — high fiber, low calcium. Alfalfa is richer and suits young, pregnant, or nursing chinchillas, but is too high in calcium for everyday adult feeding.

Are colorful chinchilla food mixes bad?

Yes — mixes with dried fruit, seeds, and colored bits let a chinchilla pick out the sugary pieces and skip the nutrition, and the sugar and fat can cause serious digestive problems. A plain, uniform timothy pellet plus hay is far healthier.
