Straight Answers
Snake Food FAQ
What do pet snakes eat?

Nearly all pet snakes are obligate carnivores that eat whole prey — most commonly frozen-thawed rodents (mice or rats) sized to the snake. Whole prey provides complete nutrition, so a properly sized feeder rodent is a full meal; there is no pellet or kibble substitute for the vast majority of snakes.

Should I feed my snake frozen or live prey?

Frozen-thawed is strongly recommended. A live rodent can bite and seriously injure or even kill a snake, especially if left unattended. Frozen-thawed prey is safer, more convenient, storable, and — from a quality supplier — humanely produced. Always thaw fully and warm to about 100°F before offering.

What size prey should I feed my snake?

Feed prey roughly as wide as the widest part of your snake's body. A correctly sized meal leaves a small, gentle bump — not a huge bulge. As the snake grows you move up the size chart: pinky, fuzzy, hopper, adult mouse, then rat pups and up for larger species.

How often should I feed a snake?

It depends on age and species. Young, growing snakes typically eat every 5–7 days; adults often eat every 1–2 weeks or longer, especially large constrictors. Feeding too often causes obesity, which is one of the most common health problems in captive snakes.
