Straight Answers
Horse Feed FAQ
What should horses eat?

Forage first. A horse should eat roughly 1.5–2% of its body weight in hay or pasture per day — grass and hay are the foundation. Concentrates (grain or balancers) only fill the gaps forage can't, and every horse needs constant fresh water and free-choice salt.

Do horses need grain?

Many easy keepers don't. Grain and concentrates are for hard keepers, performance horses, broodmares, and growing youngstock — or when forage alone can't meet requirements. A horse on good forage that's holding weight usually needs only a ration balancer, not grain.

What is a ration balancer?

A concentrated vitamin, mineral, and protein pellet fed in small amounts (often 1–2 lbs a day). It fills the nutritional gaps in an all-forage diet without the sugar, starch, and calories of a full grain feed — ideal for easy keepers and horses on good hay or pasture.

What is the best feed for an easy keeper or metabolic horse?

A low-NSC (low sugar and starch) feed or a ration balancer, paired with quality forage — ideally tested hay. Horses with metabolic issues (EMS, IR, PPID) or that gain weight easily should avoid high-grain, high-sugar feeds; look for feeds specifically labeled low-NSC.
