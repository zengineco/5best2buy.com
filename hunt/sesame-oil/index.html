Straight Answers
Toasted Sesame Oil FAQ
Toasted sesame oil vs plain sesame oil — what's the difference?

Toasted (dark) sesame oil is pressed from roasted seeds — deep amber, intensely nutty, used as a finishing seasoning. Plain (light) sesame oil is from raw seeds, pale and neutral, and can take higher heat for cooking.

Can I cook or fry with toasted sesame oil?

No — its smoke point is low and its flavor is delicate, so heat wastes it and can turn it bitter. Add it off the heat at the end, a few drops to finish soups, noodles, dressings, and marinades.

Why is most good sesame oil imported?

The sesame seed is grown mostly in Asia and Africa, and the deepest-roasted styles come from Japanese and Korean makers. Genuine US mills that press and bottle it are rare, which is why this shelf is short and honest.

How do I store toasted sesame oil?

It's prone to going rancid — keep it tightly closed away from light and heat, and refrigerate it after opening for the longest, freshest life.
