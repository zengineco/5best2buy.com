5 Raw Honey Makers Worth the Hunt

A lot of supermarket honey is a blend from no particular place, gently heated and filtered until it's just generic sweetness, and a fair amount of it has been cut with cheaper syrup. Raw honey from a real apiary is a different thing: unheated, unfiltered, and it tastes like the specific flowers the bees worked — orange blossom, tupelo, buckwheat, wildflower. The makers below keep their own hives or buy from named small beekeepers, and most will tell you exactly where a jar came from. Here's where to buy them.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here keeps bees or sources from named small apiaries, sells the honey raw, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. What pushes real honey off the shelf is a commodity market full of blended, heated, sometimes adulterated jars sold on price alone. These are the people doing it the slow way.
No.1 · The Single-Flower Standard

Basic Honey

Fourth-generation family apiary · monofloral

Basic Honey is a fourth-generation family operation that bottles award-winning single-flower honey — orange blossom, gallberry — hand-harvested and never processed past the point of staying raw. Single-varietal jars taste markedly different from one another, which is the whole reason to buy honey this way instead of a blend, and a good first order is whichever flower sounds best to you. For a fourth-generation operation, the care shows jar to jar.

Why it isn't on AmazonMonofloral, unprocessed honey from one family's hives is a seasonal, small-lot product. It doesn't fold into the anonymous commodity honey a big platform stocks by the pallet.

No.2 · The Varietal Range

Bloom Honey

Raw single-floral varietals

Bloom Honey sells raw, single-floral honey across a real range — wildflower, orange blossom, the dark and malty buckwheat — kept unfiltered so it still carries the character of its source. If you want to taste how much the flower changes the honey, buying two or three of theirs side by side is the easiest way to do it. They also keep things simple: raw honey and beeswax, nothing dressed up.

Why it isn't on AmazonRaw varietal honey is defined by where and when it was gathered. That provenance is the product, and it's exactly what a generic commodity listing strips away.

See it at Bloom Honey →
No.3 · The Tupelo One

Smiley Honey

Gulf County, FL · own hives & extraction

Smiley is a family apiary on the Gulf Coast that runs its own hives, extraction, and bottling, and they're one of the few sources for real tupelo honey — a genuinely rare American varietal harvested from river swamps for only a couple of weeks a year. True tupelo resists crystallizing and has a clean, almost floral finish, and the price reflects how little of it exists. Their never-heated comb honey is worth trying too.

Why it isn't on AmazonTupelo has a tiny, weather-dependent harvest window and limited supply. It's the opposite of an always-in-stock commodity, so it stays close to the apiary that gathers it.

See it at Smiley Honey →
No.4 · The Small Family Apiary

East Hill Honey Co.

Gulf Coast · family-owned since 2011

East Hill is a small family apiary selling raw wildflower honey alongside curated single-origin jars like tupelo, sourwood, and orange blossom. It's the kind of operation where the honey is gathered, settled, and bottled without a processing line in between, so what you get tastes like a specific season in a specific place. A jar of their wildflower is a good everyday honey; the varietals are where it gets interesting.

Why it isn't on AmazonA family apiary's output is finite and changes with the season. There's no steady commodity stream to list, which is why you buy it direct from the people who keep the bees.

See it at East Hill →
No.5 · The Regional Beekeeper

Mill Creek Apiary

New Jersey · raw & unfiltered since 2005

Mill Creek is a New Jersey apiary that has sold raw, unfiltered honey from its own and nearby small beekeepers' hives since 2005, and ships nationwide. Local raw honey like this is what people actually mean when they talk about buying honey close to home — it carries the pollen and character of a particular region rather than a national blend. Start with their wildflower and go from there.

Why it isn't on AmazonRegional raw honey is a small, place-specific harvest. It exists because a beekeeper wanted to sell their own hives' honey, not to feed a commodity supply chain.
