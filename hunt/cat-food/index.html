Fresh & Raw Cat Food Worth the Hunt

Cats are obligate carnivores — they're built to eat meat and get their water from it, not to crunch dry starch pellets all day. Dry kibble is convenient for us and quietly bad for them: too little moisture, too much carbohydrate. Fresh and raw cat food fixes both — high-protein, high-moisture, real meat and organs, gently cooked or raw. Cats who switch often drink less, weigh better, and have cleaner litter boxes. The small makers below do it right; here's where to buy them.
