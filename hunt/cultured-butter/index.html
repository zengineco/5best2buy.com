5 Cultured Butters Worth the Hunt

Most supermarket butter is sweet-cream butter made at industrial scale. Cultured, European-style butter — where the cream is fermented before churning — is a different thing entirely: tangy, complex, with a higher butterfat that makes everything you cook better. The small creameries below make it the slow way, often from grass-fed cream, and ship it cold. Here's where to get butter worth tasting on its own.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every creamery here is small, cultures its cream the slow way, genuinely ships cold, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The dairy case is industrial sweet-cream butter; these are the people fermenting and hand-churning the real European-style thing.
No.1 · The Accessible Introduction

Banner Butter

Atlanta, GA · cultured, grass-fed cream

Banner Butter makes patiently-cultured butter from hormone-free grass-fed cream in small batches, and ships it nationwide refrigerated — the rare small creamery genuinely set up for DTC. The slow culturing gives it a real tang and depth. A great, accessible introduction to what cultured butter can be.

Why it isn't on AmazonSlow-cultured grass-fed butter shipped cold is perishable craft. The culturing time and the cold chain are the whole product.

No.2 · Hand-Churned Vermont

Ploughgate Creamery

Fayston, VT · small-batch, subscription

Ploughgate is a tiny Vermont creamery hand-churning small-batch cultured butter from fresh local cream, cultured a full day before churning — and they run a butter subscription, so it arrives fresh on a schedule. It's beloved among people who take butter seriously. A wonderful pick for the real, hand-churned Vermont article.

Why it isn't on AmazonHand-churned cultured butter from one small Vermont creamery exists in tiny batches. The smallness and the fresh local cream are the point.

No.3 · One Artisan, By Name

Nordic Creamery

Westby, WI · European-style, solo buttermaker

Nordic Creamery is essentially one artisan — Al Bekkum — making European-style and cultured butters in small batches from local Wisconsin farm milk, and shipping them across the country. It's a genuine one-person craft operation with a real range of flavored and cultured butters. A great pick if you want butter from an actual buttermaker you could name.

Why it isn't on AmazonA solo artisan churning small-batch butter from local milk is the definition of small. The hand of the maker is in every batch.

See it at Nordic Creamery →
No.4 · Grass-Fed, High Butterfat

Kriemhild Dairy Farms

Hamilton, NY · farmer-owned, 100% grass-fed

Kriemhild is a farmer-owned New York operation making 100% grass-fed cultured butter with an exceptionally high butterfat (84–85%), shipped direct from their own online store. The grass-fed cream and high butterfat make it rich, yellow, and genuinely different from anything in a grocery case. A great pick for grass-fed cultured butter straight from the farm.

Why it isn't on AmazonFarmer-owned, high-butterfat grass-fed cultured butter sold direct is a farm's own product. The grass-fed cream and the directness are what you're buying.

See it at Kriemhild →
No.5 · Week-Long Cultured Farmstead

Stepladder Ranch & Creamery

Cambria, CA · farmstead, deeply cultured

Stepladder is a California farmstead making European-style cultured butter the slow way — culturing the cream most of a week into crème fraîche before churning — and shipping carefully to protect the cold chain. It's about as hands-on and farmstead as butter gets. A lovely pick for deeply-cultured, farm-made butter from the West Coast.
