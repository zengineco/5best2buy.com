5 Small-Batch Nut Butter Makers Worth the Hunt

The big jar of peanut butter is usually sugar and palm oil doing the work the nuts should be doing. Small-batch nut butter is the opposite: nuts, maybe salt, ground fresh, with the oil that separates on top because nothing's there to stop it. The makers below roast and grind in small runs, skip the palm oil, and several add a genuine mission on top of a genuinely good jar. Here's where to buy them.

Straight Answers
Nut Butter FAQ
Why does the oil separate, and how do I fix it?

That layer of oil on top is a good sign: it means the butter is just ground nuts with nothing added to hold it together. Mass-market jars stay uniform because palm oil or hydrogenated fat is blended in to stop the separation. Just stir it back in — flipping the jar upside down for a day first makes it easier — and store it in the fridge afterward to slow it down.

Should I avoid added sugar and palm oil?

If you want the butter to taste like the nut and not like candy, yes. A genuinely good jar needs nothing but nuts and maybe a pinch of salt. Palm oil is added purely for shelf stability and comes with real deforestation concerns; added sugar and hydrogenated oils are there to mimic the mass-market texture people grew up on. Read the label — a short one is the whole point.

How should I store natural nut butter and how long does it keep?

Once opened, keep natural nut butter in the refrigerator. The oils are unsaturated and will eventually go rancid at room temperature, especially in warm kitchens. Cold storage keeps it fresh for several months and also slows the oil separation. If it ever smells sharp, bitter, or like old paint, it has turned — trust your nose.

Is nut butter safe if I have a peanut allergy?

Only if it is made in a peanut-free facility. Several makers here work exclusively with tree nuts and seeds and skip peanuts entirely, but cross-contamination is the real risk, so always check the label for a dedicated allergen statement. Seed butters like sunflower are the safest route for people avoiding both peanuts and tree nuts.
