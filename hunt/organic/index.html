No.1 · Organic You Can Trace to a Farm

Frog Hollow Farm

Brentwood, CA · regenerative-organic orchard

Frog Hollow is a regenerative-organic California stone-fruit farm — one 280-acre family operation — turning its own organic peaches and apricots into fresh fruit, conserves, and dried fruit. It's organic you can trace to a single farm and family. A wonderful anchor for the organic shelf, and the fruit is genuinely exceptional.

Why it isn't on AmazonRegenerative-organic fruit from one traceable family farm is tied to that land. The single-farm provenance is exactly what commodity organic loses.

No.2 · Clean Organic Chocolate

The Good Chocolate

San Francisco, CA · organic, zero-sugar bean-to-bar

The Good Chocolate is a tiny San Francisco bean-to-bar maker doing zero-sugar dark chocolate from organic cocoa, butter, and vanilla — a remarkably short, clean, organic ingredient list. It's proof that organic and genuinely good can be the same bar. A great pick for clean, organic chocolate without the sugar.

Why it isn't on AmazonZero-sugar organic bean-to-bar chocolate from a tiny maker is a deliberate, careful craft. The clean organic ingredients are the whole product.

No.3 · Single-Garden Organic Tea

Teatulia

Denver, CO · garden in northern Bangladesh

Teatulia grows organic tea on its own single garden in northern Bangladesh and is vertically integrated from soil to cup — about as traceable as organic tea gets. Single-garden organic tea has a freshness and integrity that blended supermarket tea can't match. A great pick for genuinely organic, genuinely traceable tea.

Why it isn't on AmazonSingle-garden organic tea grown and processed by one operation is tied to that specific garden. The vertical integration is the traceability.

Burlap & Barrel sources single-origin spices direct from smallholder farmers, including a range of certified-organic offerings, with full traceability to the farm. For the organic shelf, it's the spice pick — clean, traceable, and from a genuine direct-trade B-Corp. A great way to organic-up your spice rack.

Why it isn't on AmazonSingle-origin spices traceable to the farm are a relationship business, not a commodity. The direct sourcing is what makes the organic claim real.
