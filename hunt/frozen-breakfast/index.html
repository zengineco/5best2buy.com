Straight Answers
Frozen Breakfast FAQ
Can I cook these straight from frozen?

Yes — that's the point. Biscuits and pastries bake from frozen in the oven, waffles crisp up in a toaster or oven from frozen, and breakfast burritos microwave or bake from frozen. Thawing first tends to make baked goods gummy, so follow each maker's from-frozen instructions.

What's a liège waffle versus a regular Belgian waffle?

A liège waffle is made from a dense, brioche-like dough studded with pearl sugar that caramelizes on the iron, giving a chewy, sweet, craggy waffle you can eat by hand. A Brussels waffle is lighter and crisper, made from a batter, and is the rectangular one usually served with toppings. Liège is richer; Brussels is airier.

Are these actually cleaner than grocery-store breakfast?

For these independents, generally yes — Mason Dixie and Wildgrain make from scratch with short ingredient lists and no artificial preservatives, and Burrito Kitchens uses whole-muscle meats. That's a real contrast with mass breakfast items built on fillers, gums, and preservatives for long shelf life. Read each label, but the ingredient decks here are noticeably shorter.

How should I store and reheat them?

Keep everything frozen until you're ready — get it into the freezer as soon as it arrives. Reheat from frozen per the package: oven for biscuits and pastries, toaster or oven for waffles so they crisp, microwave or oven for burritos. Avoid microwaving waffles and croissants if you want them crisp rather than soft.
