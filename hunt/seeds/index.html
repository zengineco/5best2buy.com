5 Heirloom Seed Companies Worth the Hunt

Big-box garden seeds are mostly hybrids you can't save and varieties bred for shipping, not flavor. Heirloom seed companies keep the old, open-pollinated varieties alive — the tomatoes that actually taste like something, the beans your great-grandparents grew — and you can save the seed and grow them again next year. The companies below are the real keepers of that. Here's where to get them.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every company here is independent and committed to open-pollinated, saveable, non-GMO seed, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The seed aisle at a big-box store is mostly patented hybrids; these are the people preserving genetic diversity instead, so the good old varieties don't disappear.
No.1 · The Heirloom Icon

Baker Creek (Rare Seeds)

Mansfield, MO · thousands of rare varieties

Baker Creek is the most beloved heirloom seed company in the country — thousands of rare, open-pollinated varieties from around the world, in a catalog that reads like an adventure. It's the gold standard for heirloom preservation, and their varieties (the rare tomatoes especially) are the reason a lot of people fell in love with growing food. The place to start a real garden.

Why it isn't on AmazonBaker Creek deliberately keeps its rare-seed catalog independent — preservation and the catalog itself are the mission, not marketplace reach. That independence is the point.

No.2 · The Co-op Preservationists

Southern Exposure Seed Exchange

Mineral, VA · cooperatively owned

Southern Exposure is a cooperatively owned seed company focused on open-pollinated, heirloom, and regionally adapted varieties for the Southeast and mid-Atlantic, with a deep commitment to seed-saving and the Safe Seed Pledge. It's mission-driven in the truest sense — a co-op preserving genetic diversity. A great pick for serious growers and anyone who wants regionally adapted seed.

Why it isn't on AmazonA cooperatively owned seed-preservation operation exists to do the opposite of commodity agriculture. The mission is the product.

No.3 · The Family Homestead

Seeds for Generations

Free Union, VA · family homestead, seed-saving education

Seeds for Generations is a small family homestead seed business built around heirloom varieties and teaching people to save their own seed — the knowledge as much as the seed itself. It's exactly the kind of small, mission-driven operation this whole store is for: a family passing on something that matters. A lovely place to buy from and learn from.

Why it isn't on AmazonA family homestead selling heirloom seed and seed-saving education is a personal mission at small scale. The teaching is part of what you're buying.
