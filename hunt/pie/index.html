Straight Answers
Pie FAQ
How is pie shipped without falling apart or spoiling?

Bakeries flash-freeze the finished pie, pack it in an insulated box with dry ice, and ship it one to two days. It arrives frozen solid and travels far better frozen than fresh. You thaw it, or bake it off from frozen, at home.

Do I bake it when it arrives, or is it ready?

It depends on the maker — some ship fully baked (thaw and serve or warm), others ship par-baked or unbaked to finish in your oven. The unbaked kind gives you a fresh-from-the-oven pie; the pre-baked kind is more convenient. Each shop's page says which.

How long will a shipped pie keep?

Frozen, most keep two to six months without quality loss — which is why you can order ahead for a holiday. Once thawed, treat it like any fresh pie: a few days refrigerated for fruit and custard pies.

What makes a good crust?

Real fat — usually all butter, sometimes leaf lard — cut into flour and kept cold, so it bakes up flaky rather than the mealy, shortening-and-corn-syrup texture of commodity crust. A good crust is the whole difference between a pie you finish and one you scrape the filling out of.
