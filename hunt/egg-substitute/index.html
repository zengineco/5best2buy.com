Egg Substitutes & Liquid Egg FAQ
Liquid egg or powdered replacer — which do I need?

Liquid eggs (JUST Egg, Simply Eggless) scramble, make omelets and French toast, and work where you want the egg to be the dish. Powders (Bob's Red Mill, Ener-G) are binders for baking — they do the egg's structural job in cookies, cakes, and muffins, but they won't scramble in a pan.

What are these actually made from?

JUST Egg is mung bean protein; Simply Eggless is lupin bean; Bob's Red Mill blends potato starch, tapioca, baking soda, and psyllium; Ener-G is potato and tapioca starch. All are plant-based and cholesterol-free, and each one lists its ingredients plainly rather than hiding behind 'egg replacer.'

Do they replace eggs one-for-one?

Liquid eggs run about three tablespoons per egg and scramble or bake closely. Powdered replacers mix with water to sub per egg in baking, but they don't add the richness or leavening of a real egg, so they work best in already-sturdy recipes rather than something delicate like a soufflé.

How do I store them?

Powdered replacers are shelf-stable in the pantry for a year or more. Liquid eggs are refrigerated — or frozen, for the folded style — and ship cold, so get them into the fridge on arrival and use within about a week of opening.
