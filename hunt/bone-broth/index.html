5 Small-Maker Bone Broth Makers Worth the Hunt

Most shelf-stable "bone broth" at the store is closer to seasoned stock — pressure-cooked fast, often from commodity animals, and stabilized to sit on a shelf for a year. The real thing is simmered slowly for a day or two from grass-fed bones, gels when it's cold, and usually ships frozen because it hasn't been cooked to death. The makers below source their animals carefully and treat broth like the slow food it is. Here's where to buy it.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small or regenerative, sources grass-fed or pasture-raised animals, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. We weight sourcing heavily: where the animals were raised matters as much as the simmer. We point you to the people doing both parts honestly.

Cleveland, OH · 100% grass-fed & pasture-raised

Erie Bone Broth is a small Cleveland maker simmering broth from 100% grass-fed and pasture-raised beef, bison, chicken, turkey, and even elk, with the cleanest sourcing of any program in the category. It's a genuinely regional operation doing the slow simmer properly, and the range of animals is unusual — bison and elk broth are hard to find anywhere. A strong first order for someone who wants broth they can trust ingredient by ingredient.

No.2 · The Never-Pasteurized One

BrothMasters

Frozen, slow-simmered 48 hours

BrothMasters makes grass-fed and pasture-raised broth simmered for a full 48 hours, then frozen rather than shelf-stabilized or pasteurized — which is the difference between broth that still has its gelatin and collagen intact and broth that's been cooked flat for shelf life. It ships on dry ice and arrives the way real broth should: thick enough to gel. The pick for anyone who wants the actual nutritional point of bone broth, not a seasoned liquid.

Why it isn't on AmazonNever-pasteurized frozen broth has to move through a cold chain and be eaten relatively fresh. That's the opposite of the shelf-stable cartons a commodity platform stocks.

It's broth for people who care that the animal and the soil were treated well, and it tastes clean and rich for it. The certification is the reason to choose it.
