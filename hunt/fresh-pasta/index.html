Straight Answers
Fresh Pasta FAQ
Is fresh pasta better than dried?

Not better — different. Fresh egg pasta is tender and silky, ideal for delicate sauces, filled shapes, and baked dishes. Dried semolina pasta is chewier and holds up to bold, oily sauces (a good bronze-die dried pasta is its own craft). Keep both; reach for fresh when you want luxury and speed.

How do you cook fresh pasta?

Fast — usually two to four minutes in well-salted boiling water, versus eight-plus for dried. It's done when it floats and is tender with a slight bite. Frozen filled pasta goes straight from freezer to boiling water, no thawing, plus a minute or two.

How is it shipped without spoiling?

Either fresh with cold packs for quick delivery, or flash-frozen and shipped with dry ice. Fresh-frozen keeps for months in your freezer with no loss of quality; fresh should be used within its short window or frozen when it arrives.

Can I freeze fresh pasta myself?

Yes. Freeze filled pasta in a single layer on a tray first so pieces don't stick, then bag them. Cook straight from frozen. Long fresh cuts freeze best coiled into loose nests.
