Live Sourdough Starters Worth the Hunt

A packet of commercial yeast is a single lab-grown strain, bred for speed. A live sourdough starter is a whole ecosystem — wild yeasts and bacteria that leaven slowly, build real flavor, and make the bread easier to digest. The best ones carry a lineage measured in decades or centuries, and they arrive alive, ready to feed and bake. For a richer potica dough, or the day you go down the bread rabbit hole for good, this is the craft alternative to the packet.
