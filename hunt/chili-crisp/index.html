5 Small-Batch Chili Crisps Worth the Hunt

Chili crisp went from secret weapon to grocery staple fast, and most of the new shelf versions are forgettable. The small makers below are the ones actually worth keeping in the fridge — distinct recipes, real ingredients, and the kind of crunch and heat you end up putting on everything. A couple are genuinely hard to find. Here's where to get them.

Straight Answers
Chili Crisp FAQ
What exactly is chili crisp?

Chili crisp is chili flakes fried in oil with aromatics until the solids turn crunchy, so you get crisp bits and infused oil in the same spoonful. The Sichuan-style versions lean on chili, garlic, shallot, and often numbing Sichuan peppercorn; other regions do it differently, like the brighter Nyonya-style sambal crunch. The defining trait is texture — it is meant to be spooned on, not poured.

How is craft chili crisp different from Lao Gan Ma?

Lao Gan Ma is the mass-market benchmark and it is genuinely good for the money, but it is a large-factory product with a fixed recipe. The small makers here vary the peppers, the oil, and the aromatics, and skip fillers and MSG when they choose to. You are paying for a specific point of view — a Southern peanut-oil crisp, an anchovy-deepened oil, a no-added-sugar Taipei recipe — rather than one uniform jar.

What do you put chili crisp on?

Start with a fried egg, then work outward: dumplings, noodles, rice, avocado toast, roasted vegetables, pizza, and even vanilla ice cream. Stir the crunchy solids up from the bottom before each use, since they settle under the oil. A little goes a long way, so spoon conservatively until you know a jar's heat.

Does chili crisp need to be refrigerated?

The oil and salt make most chili crisp shelf-stable for weeks at room temperature, but refrigerating it after opening keeps the aromatics and any fresh alliums from going off and preserves the crunch longer. Always use a clean, dry spoon — water or food crumbs introduced into the jar are what cause spoilage. If a jar contains anchovy or shrimp, treat it a bit more carefully and keep it cold.
