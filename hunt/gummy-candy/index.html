Straight Answers
Gummies & Chews FAQ
Are there really no big independent gummy makers?

Very few. The gummy category is dominated by large multinationals and their brands, so most 'artisan' gummies are actually made by the same big co-packers. The genuine independents tend to compete on a real difference — lower sugar, real fruit, no artificial dye — like BEHAVE does, rather than trying to out-scale the giants.

What actually is saltwater taffy?

It's a soft, chewy candy of sugar, corn syrup, butter, and flavor that's aerated by pulling — stretching and folding it repeatedly so it turns light and holds a chew. There's no seawater in it; the name is old seaside-boardwalk marketing. Fresh taffy is soft and pliable, while old taffy turns hard and sticky.

Why do some gummies use gelatin and others don't?

Traditional gummies get their bounce from gelatin, which is animal-derived, so they aren't vegetarian or vegan. Pectin (from fruit) is the usual plant-based substitute and gives a slightly different, shorter texture. Check the label if that matters to you — makers who use pectin or kosher gelatin will say so.

Does taffy or gummy candy hold up in the mail?

Both are shelf-stable and travel well, so no cold pack needed. Heat is the only real enemy: in hot weather taffy can go soft and gummies can clump together, though they usually recover once they cool down. Store them sealed at room temperature and out of the sun.
