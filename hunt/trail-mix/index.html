Trail Mix FAQ
What makes a good trail mix versus a cheap one?

Freshness and ratio. Cheap mixes load up on the least expensive filler — often stale peanuts and candy — and skimp on cashews, real dried fruit, and quality chocolate. A good mix is roasted or blended close to when it ships and balances salty, sweet, and crunchy instead of just being sweet.

Is trail mix actually healthy?

It can be, but it's calorie-dense — nuts and dried fruit pack a lot into a small handful. Mixes heavy on chocolate, yogurt coatings, and candy are closer to trail dessert. If you want the energy-food version, lean toward mixes that are mostly nuts and seeds with real dried fruit and go easy on the portion.

How long does trail mix stay fresh?

Nuts have oils that eventually go rancid, so freshness matters. Sealed, a good mix keeps a couple of months in the pantry and longer in the fridge or freezer. This is exactly why ordering from a maker who blends to order beats a bag that's been on a shelf for who-knows-how-long.

Can I get trail mix without peanuts or specific allergens?

Yes — this is a real strength of ordering from independents. Gerbs packs everything free of the top 14 allergens in a dedicated facility, and Nuts.com and Fastachi let you build or choose mixes around what you avoid. Always check each maker's allergen and cross-contact statement for your specific need.
