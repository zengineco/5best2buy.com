Straight Answers
Mail-Order Cookies FAQ
How do fresh-baked cookies survive shipping?

Small bakeries bake to order and ship fast, usually within a day or two of baking, then pack in sealed bags or tins so the cookies hold moisture in transit. Because they use real butter and no preservatives, they are not built for a month on a shelf — they are built to arrive within a few days of coming out of the oven. Most ship early in the week to avoid a package sitting over a weekend.

How long do mail-order cookies stay fresh, and can I freeze them?

Eat them within about a week of arrival for the best texture, keeping them airtight at room temperature. They freeze beautifully — seal them well and they will keep for two to three months, then thaw at room temperature or warm briefly in the oven to bring back that fresh-baked softness. This is the trick for making a shipment last past a few days.

Are vegan or allergen-friendly cookies actually as good?

The good ones are made to be great cookies first and vegan or allergen-free second, not diet substitutes. A well-made vegan cookie swaps in plant fats and binders skillfully enough that most people cannot tell, and dedicated allergen-free bakeries reformulate recipes from scratch rather than just removing an ingredient. Bakeries built around top-allergen-free baking are also the safest bet if you need a genuinely clean facility.

What is the difference between edible cookie dough and regular dough?

Edible cookie dough is made to be eaten raw, which means it uses heat-treated flour and no raw eggs — the two things that make ordinary dough unsafe uncooked. Raw flour can carry bacteria and raw egg carries a salmonella risk, so scooping straight from a normal batch is not advised. Some products are bakeable too, giving you the choice to eat it by the spoon or turn it into actual cookies.
