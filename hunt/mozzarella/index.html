Fresh Mozzarella Worth the Hunt

Fresh mozzarella is a living cheese — best within a day or two of being made, when the curd is still milky and the pull is soft. The vacuum-packed grocery kind is a pale echo, rubbery and flavorless by comparison. Real fresh mozzarella comes from a creamery that stretches the curd by hand and ships it cold, fast; water-buffalo mozzarella (di bufala) is richer and tangier still. It's a hunt — but for a Margherita, it's the whole difference.

Straight Answers
Fresh Mozzarella FAQ
What is the difference between cow and water-buffalo mozzarella?

Fior di latte is fresh mozzarella made from cow's milk — milky, mild, and springy. Mozzarella di bufala is made from water-buffalo milk, which is much higher in fat and protein, so it's richer, tangier, and more tender, with a faint gaminess. True buffalo mozzarella is rare in America because very few farms keep buffalo herds, which is why the di bufala makers here are worth seeking out.

Why does fresh mozzarella need to arrive so fast?

Fresh mozzarella is at its peak within a day or two of being stretched, while the curd is still soft and the interior weeps milk when cut. After that it firms up, dries out, and loses the delicate flavor, which is exactly what has already happened to the rubbery vacuum-packed grocery kind. That short window is why the makers here ship cold on tight schedules rather than through a warehouse.

How should I store fresh mozzarella and how long does it keep?

Keep it in its liquid or brine, refrigerated, and eat it within a few days of arrival — this is not a cheese to stockpile. Do not freeze fresh mozzarella, since freezing wrecks the delicate texture. If you bought fermented curd to stretch at home, that keeps longer and lets you make it fresh in batches as you want it.

Should I use fresh mozzarella on pizza, and how?

Fresh mozzarella makes a superb Margherita, but its high moisture can flood a pie, so tear it into pieces, and let it drain on paper towels for a bit before baking. A drier, low-moisture version melts more evenly and browns better if you want cleaner slices. For the milkiest result, add torn fresh mozzarella partway through the bake rather than at the start.
