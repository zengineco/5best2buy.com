5 Small-Batch Ice Cream Makers That Ship

Most pints in the grocery freezer are made with stabilizers, a lot of pumped-in air, and flavors built in a lab. Small-batch ice cream is denser, made with real dairy and actual ingredients, and the good news is that more independent creameries than you'd think will pack a few pints in dry ice and ship them to your door. The makers below are genuinely small, genuinely good, and confirmed to ship nationwide. Here's where to buy them.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every creamery here is small or independent, uses real dairy and real ingredients, ships DTC on dry ice, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. We left out the big national pint brands you can already get anywhere; what's here is the stuff you'd otherwise have to travel for. We point you to where they'll ship it.
No.1 · The Regenerative One

Alec's Ice Cream

California · organic, regeneratively-sourced

Alec's makes organic, small-batch ice cream from regeneratively-sourced A2/A2 dairy — the strongest sustainability story in the category, and a genuinely creamy pint to go with it. The whole company is built around the idea that better farming makes better ice cream, and it's the rare premium pint where the sourcing claims are the foundation rather than the marketing. A clean, grown-up choice for everyday indulgence.

Why it isn't on AmazonRegenerative dairy at small scale is a relationship with specific farms, shipped frozen and direct. That cold-chain, traceable model isn't something a commodity listing handles.

No.2 · The Microcreamery

Clementine's Naughty & Nice

St. Louis, MO · award-winning small batches

Clementine's is an award-winning St. Louis microcreamery making classic, boozy ("Naughty"), and vegan ("Nice") flavors in genuinely small batches, packed in dry ice so they arrive frozen. It's the best pure-ethos pick here — a real neighborhood creamery that grew on the strength of its ice cream, not a brand built for the freezer aisle. The boozy flavors are a fun thing to ship to someone.

Why it isn't on AmazonA microcreamery's pints are made in small runs and shipped frozen from one shop. That's the opposite of an always-stocked commodity pint.

See it at Clementine's →
No.3 · The Harlem Creamery

Sugar Hill Creamery

Harlem, NYC · Black & woman-owned, small-batch

Sugar Hill is a Black- and woman-owned creamery in Harlem making small-batch ice cream with a real sense of place — flavors named for the neighborhood, made by a husband-and-wife team. They ship nationwide on dry ice, so you can taste a genuinely independent New York creamery without being in New York. Buying from them is a direct line of support to a small family business doing it right.

Why it isn't on AmazonA neighborhood creamery makes small batches and ships them frozen and direct. The maker and the place are the product, and a commodity listing strips both away.

See it at Sugar Hill →
No.4 · The Boozy One

Tipsy Scoop

New York, NY · liquor-infused, up to 5% ABV

Tipsy Scoop makes liquor-infused ice cream — up to 5% ABV — in flavors like dark chocolate whiskey salted caramel, using local and seasonal ingredients, and ships to all fifty states packed deep in dry ice. It's a genuine novelty that's also genuinely good, which is rarer than it sounds. The perfect thing to ship to an adult who has everything, and a category you simply won't find in a grocery freezer.

Why it isn't on AmazonAlcohol-infused ice cream is a small, specialized product with shipping rules of its own. It sells direct from the maker, not off a commodity shelf.

See it at Tipsy Scoop →
No.5 · The Globally-Inspired One

Wanderlust Creamery

Los Angeles, CA · world-flavor small batches

Wanderlust makes small-batch ice cream inspired by flavors from around the world — Filipino ube, Thai roti, sticky rice, Vietnamese coffee — the kind of thing you'd have to travel to find, made by a creamery that treats each one with real care. They ship pints in recyclable coolers with dry ice. If you're bored of vanilla-and-chocolate, this is the most adventurous pint on the list.

Why it isn't on AmazonGlobally-inspired small-batch flavors are a creative, limited product shipped frozen from one creamery. That originality and cold-chain don't translate to a commodity listing.
