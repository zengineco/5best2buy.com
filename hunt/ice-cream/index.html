Most pints in the grocery freezer are made with stabilizers, a lot of pumped-in air, and flavors built in a lab. Small-batch ice cream is denser, made with real dairy and actual ingredients, and the good news is that more independent creameries than you'd think will pack a few pints in dry ice and ship them to your door. The makers below are genuinely small, genuinely good, and confirmed to ship nationwide. Here's where to buy them.

Straight Answers
Shipped Ice Cream FAQ
How does ice cream ship without melting?

Creameries pack pints in an insulated cooler with dry ice — frozen carbon dioxide at about minus 109 degrees Fahrenheit — which keeps everything solidly frozen for the one-to-two-day express transit. As long as the dry ice has not fully sublimated when it arrives, the pints reach you frozen. This is why shipped ice cream almost always goes out overnight or two-day, never ground.

What do I do with the dry ice when it arrives?

Handle it with gloves or a towel, never bare hands, since it can burn skin, and keep the area ventilated because it releases carbon dioxide gas. To dispose of it, just leave it in a well-ventilated spot to sublimate away on its own — do not put it down a drain or in a sealed container. If any pint is soft on arrival, refreeze it promptly and it is generally fine.

What makes small-batch ice cream different from grocery pints?

Small-batch ice cream has less overrun — the amount of air whipped in — so it is denser and richer, and it relies on real cream and actual ingredients rather than stabilizers, gums, and lab-built flavors. That density is why a small-batch pint feels heavier and tastes more intense than an airy supermarket tub. The trade-off is a shorter shelf life, which is exactly why it ships frozen and direct.

How does alcohol-infused ice cream work?

Boozy pints are infused with real liquor, typically landing up to around 5% ABV — similar to a beer — since too much alcohol keeps ice cream from freezing solid. It is treated as an adult product, so an adult signature is usually required at delivery and it ships only where local rules allow. The alcohol is cooked or blended in for flavor, not for a strong kick.
