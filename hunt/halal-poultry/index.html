Straight Answers
Halal Chicken & Poultry FAQ
What makes halal chicken different from regular chicken?

The slaughter. Halal chicken must be killed by a hand-cut to the throat with God's name invoked, alive at the time, then fully bled. Many big processors use mechanical blades with an audio prayer, which stricter certifiers don't accept. The makers here hand-slaughter each bird and name their certifier so you can judge for yourself.

Is halal chicken healthier, or is it only about ritual?

The slaughter method is a religious requirement, not a health claim by itself. But the independents here tend to pair it with cleaner raising — antibiotic-free, vegetarian or organic feed, pasture-raised — which is a real quality difference from commodity poultry. Read each maker's raising standards, not just the halal label.

Can I get a halal turkey for the holidays?

Yes, but plan ahead — certified whole halal turkeys sell out and ship frozen. Midamar carries whole halal turkeys and Halal Pastures raises pasture turkey in season. Order a couple weeks early and let it thaw in the fridge, which for a turkey means several days.

How does frozen poultry arrive safely?

Birds ship frozen, packed with dry ice or gel packs in insulated boxes, usually shipped early in the week. They should arrive frozen or ice-cold; get them into the freezer or fridge right away. If any package arrives at room temperature, contact the seller rather than using it.
