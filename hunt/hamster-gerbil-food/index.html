Straight Answers
Hamster & Gerbil Food FAQ
What should hamsters and gerbils eat?

A fortified base diet — a uniform lab block or a quality grain-and-seed mix — plus small amounts of fresh vegetables and the occasional bit of protein. Both are omnivorous rodents, so their base food should include grains, seeds, and some protein, not just one or the other.

What is the difference between hamster and gerbil food?

They're very similar — both are small omnivorous rodents needing grains, seeds, and some protein. Gerbils tolerate slightly more fat (they love seeds), while hamsters can get overweight on too much. Many quality mixes are formulated to serve both, which is why they're often sold together.

Are pellets or seed mixes better?

Uniform pellets or lab blocks prevent selective feeding — hamsters and gerbils will pick their favorite pieces out of a loose mix and leave the fortified bits, ending up malnourished. A good mix works if the animal eats all of it; many keepers offer a fortified block as the base plus a mix for enrichment.

Can hamsters eat rabbit or guinea pig food?

No. Rabbit and guinea pig foods are high-fiber herbivore diets that lack the protein and fat an omnivorous hamster or gerbil needs. Always feed a species-appropriate hamster/gerbil food — the nutritional targets are genuinely different.
