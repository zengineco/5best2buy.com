5 Small-Batch Jam Makers Worth the Hunt

Grocery jam is mostly high-fructose corn syrup with a little fruit and a lot of pectin. Real preserves are mostly fruit — sometimes from the maker's own orchard — cooked in small batches with far less sugar, so they taste like the fruit instead of just sweet. The makers below do it the real way. Here's where to get jam worth putting on good bread.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every maker here is small, cooks with real fruit and restraint on sugar, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The jam aisle is corn-syrup-and-pectin; these are the people who put up real, local, peak-season fruit the way it used to be done.
No.1 · Straight From the Orchard

Frog Hollow Farm

Brentwood, CA · organic stone-fruit orchard

Frog Hollow is an organic California stone-fruit orchard that turns its own sun-ripened peaches and apricots into preserves — about as close to orchard-direct as jam gets. When the people growing the fruit are the people making the jam, you taste it. A wonderful pick for genuinely fruit-forward preserves.

Why it isn't on AmazonPreserves made from a farm's own organic orchard fruit are tied to that orchard's harvest. The farm-to-jar directness is the product.

No.2 · The Underdog Story

Jam by Josh

Cleveland, OH · low-sugar, teen-founded

Jam by Josh is a small-batch fruit jam with about 46% less sugar than standard jam and no artificial anything — and it was founded by a teenager, which is a genuinely great underdog story. The lower sugar lets the fruit actually come through. A feel-good pick that also happens to taste like real fruit.

Why it isn't on AmazonLow-sugar small-batch jam from a tiny founder-run operation is a deliberate, hands-on product. The restraint with sugar is exactly the point.

See it at Jam by Josh →
No.3 · English-Style Preservatory

Blake Hill Preserves

Windsor, VT · low- & no-added-sugar

Blake Hill is an English-style preservatory in Vermont making award-winning low-sugar and no-added-sugar preserves with real depth and a huge range — marmalades, savory preserves for cheese, the works. It's the most polished maker here while still being genuinely small-batch. A great pick if you want preserves that punch above grocery jam in every way.

Why it isn't on AmazonEnglish-style low-sugar preserves made in small batches are a craft built on technique. The quality and restraint don't survive a commodity jar.

See it at Blake Hill →
No.4 · Built on the Local Harvest

Happy Girl Kitchen

Pacific Grove, CA · locally harvested fruit

Happy Girl Kitchen is a California cannery that preserves locally harvested fruit into award-winning low-sugar jams and marmalades — a real community-rooted operation built around the local harvest. The local-fruit sourcing gives the jam a sense of place. A lovely pick for preserves with a genuine connection to the land they came from.

Why it isn't on AmazonJam made from locally harvested fruit by a small cannery is tied to a specific place and season. The local sourcing is what gives it character.

See it at Happy Girl Kitchen →
No.5 · The Cult Seasonal Drop

Sqirl

Los Angeles, CA · seasonal jam capsules

Sqirl is the cult Los Angeles kitchen whose seasonal "jam capsule" drops are genuinely famous — preserving peak local produce in small, often limited batches. It's the deep-cut, chase-the-drop pick: jam treated as a craft worth lining up for. Worth seeking out when the right fruit is in season.

Why it isn't on AmazonSeasonal small-batch jam from a cult kitchen exists in limited drops by design. The scarcity and the peak-season fruit are exactly the appeal.
