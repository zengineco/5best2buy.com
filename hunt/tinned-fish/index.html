5 Tinned Fish Brands Worth the Hunt

Tinned fish stopped being a punchline a while ago — good conservas are one of the best things you can keep in a cupboard. The brands below are the ones worth the shelf: small canneries, sustainable catch, and real provenance instead of the watery grocery-store stuff. Several are MSC-certified or B Corps. Put any of them on good bread with butter and you'll get it immediately. Nothing on this list is endangered or overfished.

Published June 2026 · Updated 29 Jun 2026

How this list works. Every brand here is small or specialist, sources responsibly, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. It also clears a hard rule: no bluefin, no shark, no endangered eel — ever. We point you to the canneries doing it right.
No.1 · The One That Started It

Fishwife

Portland, OR · hand-packed, responsibly sourced

Fishwife is the brand that pulled tinned fish into the present — smoked salmon, tuna, anchovies, and trout, hand-packed and responsibly sourced, with packaging that made people who'd never bought a tin suddenly curious. The food backs it up; the smoked salmon in particular is genuinely good. It's the easiest, most crowd-friendly place to start.

Why it isn't on AmazonA small cannery built on responsible sourcing and its own supply chain isn't a commodity tin. The sourcing standard is the product.

No.2 · The Cleanest Conscience

Scout

Certified B Corp · 100% North American catch

Scout is a certified B Corp that sources entirely from North American waters — one-by-one hook-and-line albacore, rope-grown PEI mussels — and uses the imperfect, back-end parts of the catch to cut waste. It's the strongest sustainability story in the category, and the food is excellent. If you want to feel genuinely good about the tin and still eat well, this is the one.

Why it isn't on AmazonMSC-certified, gear-specific, traceable catch is slow and expensive to do right. That's a mission company, not a marketplace brand.

See it at Scout →
No.3 · The Deep-Provenance Pick

Wildfish Cannery

Klawock, Alaska · hand-packed since 1987

Wildfish has been hand-packing wild Alaskan seafood since 1987 — alderwood-smoked salmon and other wild Alaska catch, from community-based, responsibly managed fisheries. They call themselves a cannery with a brand rather than a brand with a cannery, and it shows. This is the provenance pick: you know it's wild, you know it's Alaska, you know who packed it.

Why it isn't on AmazonA small Alaskan cannery doing everything by hand from a remote community is the opposite of warehouse scale. The provenance is the entire value.

See it at Wildfish Cannery →
No.4 · The Conservation Standard

Patagonia Provisions

Spanish anchovies, mussels & mackerel · conservation-built supply chains

Patagonia's food arm builds its supply chains from scratch alongside conservation groups, so the Spanish white anchovies, smoked mussels, and mackerel come with about the most rigorous sourcing story in the aisle. It's the standard you'd expect from Patagonia, applied to fish. A reliable pick when sourcing integrity is the whole reason you're buying.

Why it isn't on AmazonBuilding fisheries with conservation partners from the ground up is mission work, not procurement. It doesn't reduce to a generic listing.

See it at Patagonia Provisions →
No.5 · The Doorway to Spanish Conservas

Donostia Foods

Curated premium Spanish conservas

Donostia is a small importer that curates premium Spanish conservas — the Basque-style sardines, mussels, and squid from family canneries you'd never find on your own. This is how most people get genuinely good Spanish tinned fish without a plane ticket. Treat it as a doorway into the deep end of conservas; once you've had the real thing, the grocery version is hard to go back to.

Why it isn't on AmazonThese are small family canneries an importer hand-selects — producers who never set up their own marketplace presence. The curation is the point.
