5 Regional Chip Makers Worth the Hunt

Big-bag chips are fried in cheap seed oils and engineered in a lab. Regional and small-batch chips are a different snack — kettle-cooked in clean oils, made from real or heirloom corn and potatoes, often by one shop in one town. The makers below ship the genuine article. Here's where to get chips worth the bag.

How this list works. Every maker here is small or regional, fries in clean oil from real ingredients, and earns its spot on merit — nobody pays to be listed. The chip aisle is a handful of giants and a lot of seed oil; these are the people doing it by hand, in small kettles, with real corn and potatoes.
No.1 · The Clean-Oil Cult Pick

MASA Chips

Austin, TX · tallow-fried tortilla chips

MASA makes tortilla chips fried in 100% grass-fed beef tallow with no seed oils — the cult favorite of anyone who's gone down the clean-oil rabbit hole. They're sturdy, rich, and genuinely different from a standard chip. A great first stop if you care what your chips are fried in.

Why it isn't on AmazonTallow-fried, seed-oil-free chips are a deliberate, more expensive process. The clean oil is the entire reason they exist.

No.2 · The Everyday Upgrade

Zack's Mighty

Hudson Valley, NY · organic, avocado oil

Zack's Mighty makes organic tortilla chips fried in avocado oil from regenerative corn — and engineered to be sturdy enough not to snap in the dip, which sounds minor until you've eaten them. Clean oil, organic corn, and a real obsession with the chip itself. A great everyday upgrade.

Why it isn't on AmazonOrganic, avocado-oil chips from regenerative corn are a deliberate sourcing-and-frying choice. The standards are what set them apart.

No.3 · The Shenandoah Kettle

Route 11 Potato Chips

Mount Jackson, VA · small-batch since 1992

Route 11 has been kettle-cooking small-batch potato chips in Virginia's Shenandoah Valley since 1992 — a genuine regional-heritage chip maker, hand-cooked in small kettles. The flavor and crunch of a real kettle chip from a small shop is a different thing than the mass version. A wonderful regional pick.

No.4 · Heirloom Blue Corn

Pinole Blue

Wichita, KS · Tarahumara heirloom corn

Pinole Blue makes chips and tortillas from Tarahumara heirloom blue corn, made to order, with a genuine indigenous-sourcing story behind it. Heirloom blue corn has a depth standard corn doesn't, and the mission is real. A great pick if you want a chip with actual provenance and purpose.

Why it isn't on AmazonHeirloom blue corn sourced from a specific indigenous tradition is tied to that seed and that community. The provenance is the point.

Lowcountry Kettle

Charleston, SC · handmade Carolina chips

Lowcountry Kettle makes handmade Carolina kettle chips with Southern flavors — a true small-batch regional maker out of Charleston. It's the kind of chip you find at a good Southern shop and then can't stop thinking about. A lovely regional pick with real character.
