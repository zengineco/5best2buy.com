Halal Beef & Lamb FAQ
What's the difference between 'halal' and 'zabiha'?

Halal means permissible under Islamic law. Zabiha (or dhabiha) is the specific method: a swift hand-cut to the throat by a Muslim who invokes God's name, with the animal alive and the blood drained. A lot of mass-market 'halal' meat is machine-slaughtered; the makers here hand-slaughter zabiha and say so.

Are IFANCA, HMS, ISA, and Halal Advocates real certifiers?

Yes. IFANCA (Islamic Food and Nutrition Council of America) and Islamic Services of America (ISA) are long-running US certifiers used across the food industry. Halal Monitoring Services (HMS) is a nonprofit tied to the Shariah Board of America known for strict hand-zabiha, non-stunned standards and unannounced inspections. Halal Advocates is a recognized certifier as well. Naming the body is the whole test — a 'halal' label with no certifier behind it tells you nothing.

Is halal beef the same as grass-fed or organic?

Not automatically — halal describes how the animal was slaughtered, not how it was raised. But several makers here stack both: Halal-N-Tayyib and Halal Pastures raise 100% grass-fed animals, and TruBeef is grass-finished. If you want grass-fed AND zabiha, check the product page, because plenty of certified halal beef is still grain-finished.

How does frozen halal meat ship without spoiling?

It goes out frozen, packed with dry ice or gel packs in an insulated box, usually early in the week so it isn't sitting over a weekend. It should arrive frozen or very cold to the touch; refrigerate or refreeze right away. Ordering a larger box actually helps, since a denser load of frozen meat holds temperature longer in transit.
