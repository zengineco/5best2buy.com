Red Boat is widely regarded as the best fish sauce in the US market. It's a US company (Milpitas, CA), founded by Cuong Pham, and it makes fish sauce the honest way — just two ingredients, black anchovy and Phu Quoc sea salt, barrel-aged about a year in Vietnam with no water, sugar, or additives. Straight Answers
Fish Sauce FAQ
What is fish sauce made of?

Just fermented fish — usually anchovies — and salt, aged for months to over a year. The best fish sauces have only those two ingredients and no added sugar, water, or hydrolyzed protein.

How do I use fish sauce?

A few drops or a splash adds savory depth to almost anything — pho and dipping sauces of course, but also marinades, stir-fries, dressings, even a Bolognese. It smells strong from the bottle but mellows into pure umami when cooked.

What is first-press or "nhi" fish sauce?

The first extraction from the fermenting barrels — the most flavorful, highest-protein grade, prized for finishing and dipping sauces. Lower grades are pressed later or diluted.

Does fish sauce go bad, and how do I store it?

It is fermented and very salty so it keeps a long time. Store it sealed in a cool dark cupboard, and it is fine at room temperature — the color may darken over time, which is normal.
