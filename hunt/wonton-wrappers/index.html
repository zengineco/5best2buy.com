Straight Answers
Wonton & Dumpling Wrapper FAQ
Where do I buy fresh wonton wrappers?

The refrigerated section of an Asian grocery is your best bet — they are fresh, local, and inexpensive. National brands like Twin Marquis and Wonton Food supply many stores, and frozen wrappers ship better than fresh if you must order.

What is the difference between wonton, dumpling, and egg roll wrappers?

Wonton wrappers are thin and usually square, often with egg; dumpling (gyoza/potsticker) wrappers are round and a bit thicker; egg roll and spring roll wrappers are larger — they are not interchangeable without changing the dish.

Can I freeze wonton wrappers?

Yes — keep them well wrapped so they don't dry out, and thaw in the fridge before use; separate any stuck sheets gently, and keep the stack covered with a damp towel while you work so they stay pliable.

Can I make my own wrappers?

Yes — a simple dough of flour, water, and sometimes egg, rolled very thin and cut into squares; homemade are more work but let you control thickness, and are the only option if no store nearby carries them.
