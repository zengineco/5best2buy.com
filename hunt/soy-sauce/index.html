Best Soy Sauce & Tamari Worth the Hunt

Real soy sauce is brewed slowly from soybeans, wheat, and koji over many months — worlds away from the fast, chemically-hydrolyzed stuff that fills most supermarket shelves. There's a small but real American craft-shoyu movement worth knowing — a handful of makers actually brewing soy sauce and tamari on U.S. soil, from whole soybeans, the long way. Straight Answers
Soy Sauce FAQ
What is the difference between soy sauce and tamari?

Tamari is made with little or no wheat, so it is usually gluten-free and tastes rounder and less sharp. Regular soy sauce includes wheat and is thinner and saltier-bright.

What does naturally brewed mean, and why does it matter?

Naturally brewed soy sauce is fermented with koji mold over months to years, building deep flavor. Cheap soy sauce is often chemically hydrolyzed in days and tastes flat and harsh. Check the label for the words naturally brewed.

Light soy sauce vs dark soy sauce?

Light or regular soy sauce is thinner, saltier, and used for general seasoning. Dark soy is thicker, aged longer often with a little molasses, less salty, and used for color and depth in braises. They are not interchangeable.

How do I store soy sauce?

It keeps a long time sealed in a cool dark place. Once opened, refrigerate it to preserve the fresh aroma and flavor over the following months.
