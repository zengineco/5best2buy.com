Straight Answers
Mexican Chocolate FAQ
What is Mexican chocolate?

Mexican chocolate is coarsely stone-ground cacao blended with sugar and cinnamon (canela), sometimes almond. It is deliberately grainy and rustic — made to be dissolved into hot chocolate or melted into mole, not eaten like a smooth candy bar.

How do I use Mexican chocolate in mole?

A tablet or disc is melted into the sauce near the end — it adds body, a little sweetness, and warm spice, not overt chocolate flavor. Use a small amount; it should round out the chiles, not dominate them.

Mexican chocolate vs. regular or baking chocolate?

Regular and baking chocolate is smooth and either sweet or pure. Mexican table chocolate is grainy and pre-spiced with cinnamon and sugar — you can substitute dark chocolate plus a pinch of cinnamon in a pinch, but it will not have the same rustic texture.

How do I store it?

Keep it cool, dry, and airtight; stone-ground chocolate is shelf-stable for many months. A whitish bloom is just cocoa butter and is harmless — it still melts and dissolves fine.
