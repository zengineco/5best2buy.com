5 Craft Drinking Chocolate Makers Worth the Hunt

Most "hot chocolate" in a store is a packet of sugar, powdered dairy, and a little cocoa. Real drinking chocolate is closer to a melted bar: actual ground cacao, thick and rich, the way it was drunk for centuries before it became a powder. The makers below are small bean-to-bar and stone-ground producers who care where their cacao comes from, and they all make a dedicated version meant to be drunk rather than eaten. Here's where to buy it.

Straight Answers
Drinking Chocolate FAQ
What is the difference between drinking chocolate and hot cocoa?

Drinking chocolate is made from real ground cacao — essentially melted chocolate thinned into a drink — so it is thick, rich, and intensely chocolatey. Hot cocoa mix is powdered cocoa (the defatted solids left after cocoa butter is pressed out) with sugar and often powdered milk, which makes a thinner, sweeter cup. The presence of cocoa butter is the key difference: it is what gives real drinking chocolate its body and melt.

How do I actually make it?

Heat milk (or water, or a mix) gently until steaming but not boiling, then whisk in the drinking chocolate off a hard boil until it fully melts and thickens — usually a couple of minutes. Use less liquid than you would for cocoa; it is meant to be sipped in a small cup, closer to espresso than a mug of hot chocolate. Whole milk gives the richest result, but it melts into water or oat milk too.

Does drinking chocolate have caffeine?

Yes, a little. Because it is made from whole cacao rather than mostly sugar, real drinking chocolate carries the naturally occurring caffeine and theobromine of the bean — a mild, gentle lift, well below a cup of coffee. Darker, higher-cacao versions have somewhat more than milkier ones. Traditional Mayan-style cups, being the most concentrated, sit at the higher end.

What is cacao tea?

Cacao tea is an infusion made from cacao husks — the papery shells removed from the bean during processing — steeped in hot water like a tea. It has a light, naturally chocolatey aroma without the richness or fat of actual chocolate, and it uses a part of the pod that would otherwise be discarded. It is a low-calorie, gently flavored way to enjoy the scent of cacao if you do not want a full cup of drinking chocolate.
