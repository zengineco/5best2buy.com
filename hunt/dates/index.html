Dates FAQ
What's the difference between Medjool and Deglet Noor dates?

Medjools are big, soft, and sticky with a deep caramel flavor — the ones you eat out of hand or stuff. Deglet Noor are smaller, firmer, and less sweet, better for chopping into baking, tagines, or salads because they hold their shape. Most 'date' recipes assume Medjool unless they say otherwise.

Why do farm-shipped dates taste so much better than grocery ones?

Good dates are tree-ripened and packed soft, then shipped fast so they stay that way. Grocery dates are often older imports that have dried out and hardened in storage and distribution, which kills the caramel texture. A hand-packed box from the grower arrives moist and fresh the way the fruit is supposed to be.

How do I store dates and how long do they keep?

Keep them in an airtight container; at room temperature they last a month or two, and in the fridge they'll hold for up to a year. If they dry out, steam them briefly or soak in warm water to bring back the soft texture. Sugar crystallizing on the surface is natural and harmless, not spoilage.

Are dates actually good for you, or just sugar?

They're high in natural sugar, so they're calorie-dense, but they come with real fiber, potassium, and antioxidants, and they don't spike blood sugar as hard as refined sugar because of the fiber. They work well as a whole-food sweetener — blended into a date paste, they replace sugar in a lot of baking.
