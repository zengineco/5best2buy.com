Best Duck Feed Worth the Hunt

Ducks are not chickens, and feeding them like chickens is how ducklings end up with crippled legs. Ducks need noticeably more niacin (vitamin B3), and growing ducklings especially — a deficiency shows up fast as leg and joint problems. The right feed is a waterfowl or all-flock formula with the niacin ducks need (or a good whole-grain feed supplemented with brewer's yeast). Get it right and you get robust birds and some of the richest eggs in the barnyard. Here's where to buy real duck feed.

Straight Answers
Duck Feed FAQ
Can ducks eat chicken feed?

Adult ducks can eat unmedicated chicken layer feed in a pinch, but ducks need more niacin (vitamin B3) than chickens. If you feed chicken feed, supplement with brewer's yeast — or better, use a waterfowl or all-flock feed formulated with the niacin ducks need.

What do ducklings need that chicks don't?

Ducklings need significantly more niacin than chicks. Niacin deficiency causes leg and joint problems that can cripple a growing duckling. Use a waterfowl or all-flock starter, or add brewer's yeast to a chick starter, and never feed medicated chick starter to ducklings.

What is the best feed for laying ducks?

A waterfowl layer or an all-flock layer feed with added niacin and enough calcium for eggshells. Ducks lay large, rich eggs and need the calcium and B-vitamins to keep it up — plus grit and free-choice oyster shell.

Do ducks need grit?

Yes, if they aren't free-ranging on ground where they can find their own. Grit sits in the gizzard and grinds food. Ducks that forage on soil and gravel often get enough naturally, but offering it free-choice is good insurance.
