Straight Answers
Masa & Corn Tortilla FAQ
What is masa harina, and how is it different from cornmeal?

Masa harina is corn that has been nixtamalized in cal (lime), then dried and finely ground. It makes real tortillas and carries that toasty, distinctive corn flavor. Plain cornmeal is not nixtamalized, so it will not form a proper masa dough and cannot stand in for it.

What is nixtamalization?

Nixtamalization is the ancient process of simmering corn in an alkaline lime solution and then letting it steep. It unlocks niacin, loosens the hulls, and develops the flavor and aroma while making the dough pliable. It is the difference between real tortillas and gritty ones.

Corn or flour tortillas for tacos?

Traditional Mexican street tacos are corn. Flour tortillas are a northern-Mexico and Tex-Mex thing and are better suited to burritos. For authentic tacos, warm corn tortillas and use those.

How do I store masa harina and fresh tortillas?

Store masa harina airtight in a cool, dry place and it keeps for months. Fresh masa should be refrigerated and used within a couple of days, or frozen. Fresh tortillas keep about a week in the fridge or freeze well, and reheat best on a dry comal.
