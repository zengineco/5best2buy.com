Straight Answers
Feeder Insect FAQ
What do tarantulas eat?

Live insects — appropriately sized crickets or dubia roaches are the staple, with prey no bigger than the tarantula's body length (smaller for slings). Most tarantulas eat once a week or less; remove uneaten prey so it doesn't stress or harm the spider.

Are dubia roaches better than crickets?

Dubia roaches are meatier, higher in protein, quiet, don't smell, and can't climb glass or escape easily — which makes them the preferred feeder for many keepers. Crickets are cheaper and more available, but noisier and shorter-lived. Many keepers use both for variety.

What feeder insects should I feed a bearded dragon or leopard gecko?

A rotation of dubia roaches, crickets, hornworms, and black soldier fly larvae (naturally calcium-rich) works well, dusted with a calcium supplement. Variety matters — no single feeder is nutritionally complete on its own.

How do I keep feeder insects alive?

Gut-load them — feed the insects a nutritious diet for 24 to 48 hours before feeding them to your pet, so the nutrition passes on. Keep them at the right temperature with ventilation and a little moisture, and order fresh from a real feeder farm rather than a stale pet-store cup.
