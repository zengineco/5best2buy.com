Straight Answers
Macadamia Nuts FAQ
Why are macadamia nuts so expensive?

Everything about them is slow and hard. Trees take seven to ten years before they produce, the shell is one of the hardest of any nut and needs serious equipment to crack, and almost all the US crop comes from a small area of Hawaii. Low supply, high labor, and premium land add up.

Are the big macadamia brands still Hawaiian-owned?

Many aren't. Mauna Loa, the best-known name, runs under Hawaiian Host, which was acquired by Hershey. The farms here are still independently, family-owned and grow their own nuts - which is the point if you want your money staying with Hawaiian growers.

Is all macadamia grown in Hawaii?

Most US macadamia is Hawaiian, though a little is now grown in California, and Australia and South Africa are big global producers. '100% Hawaii-grown' on the label matters because some cheaper products blend imported nuts. The single-farm sellers here grow what they sell.

How long do macadamia nuts stay fresh?

Their high oil content means they go rancid faster than harder nuts, especially once roasted. Keep them sealed and cool; the fridge buys you several months and the freezer up to a year. Raw macs last a bit longer than roasted, so buy roasted in amounts you'll actually eat.
