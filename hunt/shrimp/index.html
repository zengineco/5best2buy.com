Shrimp Worth the Hunt

Around 90% of the shrimp Americans eat is imported, farmed in overseas ponds, and often treated with antibiotics and sodium tripolyphosphate to bulk it up with water. Wild American shrimp — Gulf and South Atlantic browns, whites, and pinks — is a cleaner, sweeter animal that grew up in the ocean, not a pond. These independents catch it off US boats and ship it frozen, no imports, no farm.

Shrimp FAQ
Why does wild American shrimp cost more than the bag at the store?

Most cheap shrimp is farmed overseas, often treated with antibiotics, and soaked in sodium tripolyphosphate that pumps it full of water — so you're partly paying for water weight and getting a mushier, blander product. Wild American shrimp is caught in the ocean, quick-frozen without additives, and traceable to a US fishery. You're buying more actual shrimp and a cleaner one.

Is wild shrimp sustainable, or is trawling a problem?

US wild shrimp is regulated and the makers here use turtle excluder devices and bycatch reduction gear required in American fisheries. Wild American shrimp (Gulf and South Atlantic) is not endangered or overfished. The bigger sustainability concern is imported farmed shrimp, which is linked to mangrove destruction and poor practices abroad — buying US wild avoids that.

What does IQF mean, and why does it matter?

IQF is 'individually quick frozen' — each shrimp is frozen separately rather than in a solid block, so you can pour out exactly how many you need and the rest stay loose in the freezer. It also freezes fast enough to protect texture. Block-frozen shrimp (frozen together in a slab) keeps well too but you thaw the whole thing at once.

What's the difference between rock shrimp, royal reds, and regular Gulf shrimp?

Regular Gulf and Atlantic shrimp (browns, whites, pinks) are your classic sweet, firm shrimp. Rock shrimp are a deep-water shrimp with a hard shell and a firm, lobster-like taste, usually sold already split. Royal reds are a deep-water shrimp with a rich, almost scallop-like sweetness and softer texture — cook them gently and briefly. All are wild and American.
