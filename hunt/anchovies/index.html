Tinned Anchovies Worth the Hunt

The anchovy is the most misunderstood thing in the pantry — the flat, harsh ones from a dusty grocery tin gave the whole fish a bad name. Real anchovies, especially Cantabrian ones from northern Spain, are salt-cured for months and hand-packed in good olive oil: silky, deep, savory, barely fishy. They melt into a sauce, crown a pizza, or go straight on buttered bread. Once you've had the real thing, the cheap tin is unthinkable.

No.1 · The Chef's Cantabrian

Don Bocarte

Santoña, Spain · salt-cured 8+ months, hand-packed in EVOO

Don Bocarte is the anchovy chefs whisper about — Cantabrian anchovies from Santoña, packed in sea salt for at least eight months, then hand-filleted and laid in extra-virgin olive oil. They're firm, silky, and profoundly savory, the kind of anchovy served at serious restaurants. This is the top of the category, and a single tin will change what you think an anchovy is. Available in the U.S. through importers like Despaña.

Why buy it directEight months of salt-curing and hand-filleting is artisan work at tiny scale. That patience is the silky texture and deep flavor — everything a fast, commodity anchovy lacks.

No.2 · The Reliable Cantabrian

Ortiz

Northern Spain · daily-caught, salt-packed 6–12 months

Ortiz buys Cantabrian anchovies daily from the auctions along Spain's north coast and processes them the same day, curing them in salt for six to twelve months. The fillets have a firm bite and a deep, clean ocean flavor without being overly fishy, and the olive oil in the tin is genuinely good. They're the most widely trusted premium anchovy going — the reliable, always-excellent choice, perfect melted into a pizza sauce.

Why buy it directSame-day processing and long salt-curing are what separate a real Cantabrian anchovy from a harsh commodity one. Buying the genuine imported product is how you get that quality intact.

USA · sustainably-caught anchovies, clean-packed

Wild Planet is a California-based company built around sustainable fishing, and their anchovies are a clean, responsibly-sourced option that's easy to get across the U.S. They're a great everyday anchovy for cooking — melting into sauces, building umami — from a company that takes ocean stewardship seriously.
