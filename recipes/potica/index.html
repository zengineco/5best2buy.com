Potica, and Where to Source It

Potica (po-TEET-sa) is the Slovenian holiday roll: a yeast dough stretched paper-thin, spread with a honeyed walnut filling, then rolled into a tight spiral and baked until deep and gold. It is a dish where the ingredients are the recipe — commodity walnuts and supermarket honey make a commodity potica. So this is the real method, and next to every ingredient that matters, exactly where to buy the best version of it.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Proof the yeastStir the yeast into the warm milk with a pinch of sugar. Let it rest about 10 minutes, until it foams.
  2. Make the doughCombine the flour, sugar, and salt. Work in the yeast mixture, softened butter, egg yolks, whole egg, and lemon zest. Knead until smooth and elastic — 8 to 10 minutes by hand. Cover and let rise 1.5 to 2 hours, until doubled.
  3. Make the fillingWarm the milk or cream with the honey and butter until melted. Pour over the ground walnuts and stir. Mix in the sugar, egg yolks, cinnamon, vanilla, and rum, then gently fold in the whipped egg whites. The filling should be soft and spreadable, not stiff.
  4. Roll it thinOn a floured cloth, roll the risen dough into a large rectangle — as thin as you dare, nearly translucent. This is the make-or-break step; take your time.
  5. Spread and coilSpread the filling evenly to the edges. Roll up tightly from the long side into a snug log, then coil it into a greased tube or potica pan.
  6. Second riseCover and rest 30 to 45 minutes. Brush the top with a beaten-egg wash.
  7. BakeBake at 350°F (175°C) for 50 to 60 minutes, until deep golden and hollow-sounding. Cool completely before slicing — potica firms and improves as it rests.
