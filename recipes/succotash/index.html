Succotash, and Where to Source It

Fresh summer corn cut off the cob and just-picked lima beans make this dish; frozen works but flat sweetness shows. The one technique: cook the corn only a few minutes so it stays crisp and milky, adding it near the end rather than stewing it soft.

The Method
  1. Render the baconCook chopped bacon until crisp, lift it out, and leave the fat in the pan.
  2. Sweat the onionCook the diced onion in the bacon fat 4-5 minutes until soft.
  3. Add the limasStir in the cooked lima beans and a splash of water and warm through, 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add corn and tomatoAdd the corn kernels, their milk, and the tomato and cook just 4-5 minutes so the corn stays crisp.
  5. FinishSwirl in the butter, season with salt and pepper, and top with the reserved bacon.

Straight Answers
Can you make Succotash in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Succotash? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Succotash ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Succotash gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Succotash vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
