Chicken Fried Rice, and where to source it

Cook in batches and never crowd the wok. Pile everything in at once and it drops the heat, releases water and steams — you want each addition to sear and stay dry, which is what gives fried rice its wok-hei.

The Method
  1. Sear the chickenStir-fry the diced chicken in a hot wok until cooked, then remove.
  2. Scramble the eggsAdd a little oil and scramble the eggs, breaking into curds.
  3. Fry the riceAdd the cold rice and scallion whites, tossing until hot and loose.
  4. SeasonReturn the chicken, add peas and carrots and soy, stir-frying to combine.
  5. FinishOff the heat, toss in scallion greens and a few drops of sesame oil.

Straight Answers
Can you make Chicken Fried Rice in an air fryer? — Not really

Not really — loose batter spreads and needs a flat, hot surface. Keep this one on the stovetop or griddle.

Can you freeze Chicken Fried Rice? — Not ideal

You can, but the crisp coating softens. Freeze the cooked pieces, then re-crisp them in a hot oven or air fryer — never the microwave.

Can you make Chicken Fried Rice ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Chicken Fried Rice gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Chicken Fried Rice vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
