Chicken Piccata, and Where to Source It

Piccata is a pan-sauce dish, so the browned bits left in the skillet are the whole point; do not wipe the pan before you deglaze. Mount the sauce with cold butter off the heat so it stays glossy instead of breaking into oil.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Pound and dredgePound the cutlets to 1/4 inch, season, and dredge in flour; shake off the excess so the sauce does not turn pasty.
  2. SearSear in oil and 1 tbsp butter over medium-high, about 3 minutes a side, until golden and 160F inside. Set aside.
  3. Build the sauceDeglaze with wine and lemon juice, scraping up the fond, and reduce by half. Add capers and a splash of stock.
  4. Mount and serveOff the heat, whisk in the remaining cold butter until glossy. Return the cutlets to warm through, finish with parsley.
