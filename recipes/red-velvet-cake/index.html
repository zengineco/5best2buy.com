Red Velvet Cake, and Where to Source It

Red velvet is not chocolate cake dyed red; it is a mild cocoa cake whose tang comes from buttermilk and a splash of vinegar reacting with baking soda. That acid-base reaction is what gives the crumb its signature softness, so do not skip the vinegar even though it sounds odd.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Cream the baseBeat 1/2 cup butter with the sugar until light, 3 minutes, then add eggs one at a time.
  2. Add color and oilBeat in 1/2 cup oil, 2 tbsp red food coloring, and 1 tsp vanilla until uniform.
  3. Alternate dry and buttermilkAdd the sifted flour and cocoa in three parts alternating with the buttermilk, beginning and ending with flour.
  4. Activate the liftStir 1 tsp baking soda into 1 tbsp vinegar and fold it in quickly; it will fizz, then bake immediately.
  5. BakeDivide between two greased 9-inch pans and bake at 350F for 26-30 minutes until the center springs back.
  6. FrostBeat cream cheese, 1/2 cup butter, 4 cups powdered sugar, and 1 tsp vanilla; frost the cooled layers.
