Noodle Kugel, and Where to Source It

Kugel is a baked pudding of egg noodles held in a rich custard, so the ratio of egg and dairy to noodle decides whether it sets soft or dry. Slightly undercook the noodles because they keep drinking custard in the oven, and let it rest before cutting so the squares hold.

Straight Answers
Can you make Noodle Kugel in an air fryer? — Sometimes

You can, but a custard needs gentle, even heat and usually a water bath the basket can’t give, so the top tends to set before the middle. Bake it in ramekins that fit and watch closely, or use the oven for a foolproof set.

Can you freeze Noodle Kugel? — Not ideal

Not really — the cream, custard, or emulsion turns grainy or splits once thawed. This one is best kept fresh in the fridge.

Can you make Noodle Kugel ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Noodle Kugel gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in a gluten-free pasta or noodle (check the other labels too).

Is Noodle Kugel vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Boil noodlesBoil the noodles two minutes short of done and drain; they finish cooking in the oven.
  2. Make custardBeat eggs, softened cream cheese, sour cream, melted butter, and sugar until smooth.
  3. CombineFold noodles and raisins into the custard and pour into a buttered 9-by-13 dish.
  4. BakeBake at 350F until set and golden on top, about 60 minutes; rest 15 minutes before cutting.
