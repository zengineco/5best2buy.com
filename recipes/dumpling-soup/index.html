Dumpling Soup, and Where to Source It

This is comfort food that hinges on two things: a filling with enough fat to stay juicy and a broth clean enough to taste of ginger and not much else. Boil the dumplings in water first so the starch does not cloud your broth.

Straight Answers
Can you make Dumpling Soup in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Dumpling Soup? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Dumpling Soup ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Dumpling Soup gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Dumpling Soup vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. FillingMix pork, soy, ginger, and a splash of water; stir one direction until tacky.
  2. WrapPlace filling in each wrapper, wet the rim, and pleat shut.
  3. BrothSimmer bone broth with sliced ginger 10 minutes, then wilt the bok choy in it.
  4. BoilCook dumplings in a separate pot until they float, about 4 minutes.
  5. AssembleAdd dumplings to bowls, ladle broth over, and finish with sesame oil.
