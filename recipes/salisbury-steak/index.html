Straight Answers
Can you make Salisbury Steak in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Salisbury Steak? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Salisbury Steak ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Salisbury Steak gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in gluten-free versions of the wheat items (check the other labels too).

Is Salisbury Steak vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Mix pattiesCombine beef, breadcrumbs, one egg, grated garlic, salt, and pepper; form 4 oval patties without packing them tight.
  2. SearBrown patties in a hot skillet 3 minutes a side to build fond, then set them aside; they finish in the gravy.
  3. Brown mushroomsIn the same pan cook mushrooms and onion until deeply browned and the moisture cooks off, 8 minutes.
  4. Build gravyStir in 2 tbsp flour, cook 1 minute, then whisk in broth and simmer to a spoon-coating gravy.
  5. FinishNestle patties back in, cover, and simmer 10 minutes to 160F internal, spooning gravy over the top.
