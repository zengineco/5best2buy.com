Lemon Tart, and where to source it

A French lemon tart lives on real lemon juice and zest, so buy fruit heavy for its size. Cook the lemon custard on the stove to 170F before it goes in the shell, then bake it low so it sets silky and never curdles or cracks.

The Method
  1. Make the crustCut cold butter into the flour and sugar, bind with an egg, press into the pan, and chill 30 min.
  2. Blind-bakeLine with parchment and weights, bake at 375F for 18 min, pull the weights, and bake 8 min more until pale gold.
  3. Cook the custardWhisk eggs, sugar, lemon juice, and zest over low heat to 170F until it coats a spoon, then whisk in the butter off the heat.
  4. Bake to setStrain the custard into the warm shell and bake at 325F for 15 min until the center barely jiggles.
  5. ChillCool at room temp then refrigerate 2 hours until fully set before slicing.

Straight Answers
Can you make Lemon Tart in an air fryer? — Sometimes

You can, but a custard needs gentle, even heat and usually a water bath the basket can’t give, so the top tends to set before the middle. Bake it in ramekins that fit and watch closely, or use the oven for a foolproof set.

Can you freeze Lemon Tart? — Not ideal

Not really — the cream, custard, or emulsion turns grainy or splits once thawed. This one is best kept fresh in the fridge.

Can you make Lemon Tart ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Lemon Tart gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in a gluten-free flour blend (check the other labels too).

Is Lemon Tart vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.
