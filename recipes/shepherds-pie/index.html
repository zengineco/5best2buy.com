Shepherd's Pie, and Where to Source It

Grass-fed lamb and a gelatin-rich broth give the filling body without a wall of flour. The one technique: dry the mashed potatoes over the heat before you add butter, so the topping browns instead of weeping water.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Brown the meatSear lamb hard in a wide pan until well browned, 8 minutes, then spoon off excess fat.
  2. BraiseAdd onion and carrot, cook 5 minutes, stir in 2 tbsp flour, then the broth; simmer 12 minutes until thick. Fold in peas.
  3. MashBoil potatoes until fork-tender, drain, and dry them in the hot pot 1 minute before mashing with butter and a splash of milk.
  4. AssembleSpread the meat in a baking dish, top with mash, and rake the surface with a fork for browning ridges.
  5. BroilBake at 400F for 20 minutes, then broil 3-4 minutes until the peaks are deep brown. Rest 10 minutes.
