Egg Foo Young, and Where to Source It

Good pasture-raised eggs set into tall, custardy patties instead of flat rubber, and the gravy leans on a real broth so it tastes of something. The move that matters is frying in a deep pool of hot oil and basting so the edges lace and puff rather than lying flat.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Mix batterBeat eggs with soy, then fold in sprouts, scallion and the cooked pork or raw shrimp.
  2. Fry pattiesLadle into 1/2 inch of 350F oil, basting the tops, and fry until the edges are lacy and gold, about 2 minutes a side.
  3. DrainLift onto a rack so they stay puffed instead of going greasy.
  4. Make gravySimmer broth with soy and a splash of oyster sauce, then thicken with a cornstarch slurry until it naps a spoon.
  5. ServeSpoon gravy over the patties on rice. Sauce them at the table so the edges keep their crisp.
