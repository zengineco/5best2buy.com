English Toffee, and Where to Source It

English toffee is a hard-crack candy, so cook the butter and sugar to 300F for the clean snap; pull it early and it turns chewy. Stir constantly the whole way so the butter never separates out and the toffee stays smooth.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Toast the almondsToast and chop the almonds; set some aside for the top.
  2. Cook the toffeeMelt the butter and sugar together and cook, stirring constantly, to 300F hard-crack and a deep amber.
  3. SpreadPour the toffee onto a lined sheet and spread it thin before it sets.
  4. Top with chocolateScatter the chopped chocolate over the hot toffee, let it melt, then spread it smooth.
  5. FinishSprinkle the toasted almonds over the chocolate, chill until set, and break into pieces.
