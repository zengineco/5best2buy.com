Arroz Caldo, and Where to Source It

Arroz caldo is a congee, so a good rich chicken broth and plenty of fresh ginger are what make it taste like more than wet rice. The one move: toast the rice in the ginger-garlic fat before adding broth, and stir often so the starch releases into a creamy, thick porridge.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Bloom aromaticsSaute a heavy hand of minced ginger and garlic in oil until fragrant and just golden, about 2 minutes.
  2. Toast the riceAdd the rice and stir 1 minute to coat it in the ginger fat; this deepens the flavor of the porridge.
  3. SimmerAdd chicken and broth, then simmer 35 to 40 minutes, stirring often, until the rice breaks down and thickens. Add broth if it gets too stiff.
  4. FinishShred the chicken back in, season with fish sauce, and top with fried garlic, scallion, and a squeeze of citrus.
