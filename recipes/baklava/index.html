Baklava, and Where to Source It

Baklava lives or dies on the contrast between shatter-crisp pastry and syrup, which only works if one is hot when the other is cold. Pour room-temperature syrup over the baklava straight from the oven; matching temperatures makes it soggy instead of crisp.

The Method
  1. Make the syrupSimmer sugar, honey, 1 cup water, and the lemon juice for 10 minutes, then cool completely to room temperature.
  2. Layer the baseBrush a 9x13 pan and stack 8 phyllo sheets, brushing butter between each.
  3. BuildSpread a third of the nuts, add 4 buttered sheets, repeat twice, then finish with 8 buttered sheets on top.
  4. Cut and bakeCut into diamonds through all layers before baking, then bake at 325F for 45-50 minutes until deep golden and crisp throughout.
  5. SoakPour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava and let it sit uncovered several hours so it absorbs and crisps.

Straight Answers
Can you make Baklava in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Baklava? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Baklava ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Baklava gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in gluten-free versions of the wheat items (check the other labels too).

Is Baklava vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and honey. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy.
