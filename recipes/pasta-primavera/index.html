Pasta Primavera, and Where to Source It

The point is vegetables that still have snap, so you cook each one only to bright-tender and no further. A finishing olive oil and pasta water make the sauce; there is no cream hiding the vegetables here.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Prep the vegCut everything to bite size and group by cook time: firm asparagus and zucchini together, quick peas and tomatoes apart.
  2. Cook to snapSaute garlic in oil, add the firmer vegetables and cook 3 to 4 minutes until bright and just tender, then the quick ones for 1 minute.
  3. Boil the pastaBoil the pasta to al dente in salted water; reserve a cup of the water before draining.
  4. Toss togetherToss pasta with the vegetables, the raw olive oil, Parmesan, and enough pasta water to make a glossy sauce that clings. Taste for salt.
