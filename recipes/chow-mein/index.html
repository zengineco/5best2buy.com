Chicken Chow Mein, and Where to Source It

The dish lives on wok control, so a real brewed soy and toasted sesame oil carry a flavor that light noodles alone can't. The technique: work over the highest heat you have and keep everything moving, so the noodles pick up a little char instead of steaming into a clump.

Straight Answers
Can you make Chicken Chow Mein in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Chicken Chow Mein? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Chicken Chow Mein ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Velvet the chickenToss sliced thigh with a little soy, cornstarch and oil; this keeps it silky through the high heat.
  2. Parboil noodlesBoil the noodles just under done, drain hard, and toss with a drop of oil so they don't stick.
  3. Sear chickenIn a screaming wok, stir-fry the chicken until just cooked, then set aside.
  4. Fire the vegetablesStir-fry cabbage, carrot and garlic over high heat until crisp-tender, tossing constantly.
  5. Toss it allReturn chicken and noodles, add the soy, and toss hard over high heat until the noodles catch a little color. Finish with sesame oil and scallion.
