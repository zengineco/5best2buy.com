Currywurst, and Where to Source It

The sauce is the whole thing and it is not just ketchup: you cook down tomato with onion, then build in curry powder, paprika, and a little sweetness so it turns into a spiced, tangy gravy. The sausage gets sliced and coated hot, then a final dusting of curry powder on top so the spice hits raw and fragrant.

The Method
  1. Cook the sauceSoften the onion, add tomato, curry powder, paprika, and a little sugar and vinegar, and simmer into a spiced gravy.
  2. Griddle the wurstGriddle or pan-fry the bratwurst until browned and crisp all over.
  3. Slice and coatSlice the sausage into rounds and toss or pour the hot sauce over.
  4. Dust and serveDust with more curry powder and serve with fries or a roll.

Straight Answers
Can you make Currywurst in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Currywurst? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Currywurst ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Currywurst gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Currywurst vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.
