Strawberry Shortcake, and Where to Source It

This lives or dies on the berries, so buy strawberries in season and let them sit in sugar to draw out their juice. The biscuit is a real cut-butter biscuit, not sponge, so keep the butter cold and the dough barely mixed for tall flaky layers.

Can you make Strawberry Shortcake in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Macerate the berriesSlice the strawberries, toss with 1/4 cup sugar, and let them sit 30 min until they release a pool of syrup.
  2. Cut the doughRub the cold butter into the flour and 2 tbsp sugar until crumbly, stir in the buttermilk just until it comes together, and do not overwork it.
  3. BakePat to 3/4 inch, cut rounds, and bake at 425F for 13-15 min until risen and golden on top.
  4. Whip the creamBeat the cold cream with 2 tbsp sugar and the vanilla to soft peaks that just hold a shape.
  5. BuildSplit the warm biscuits, spoon on berries with their syrup, add cream, and cap with the top half.
