Glazed Donuts, and Where to Source It

These are yeast donuts, so the light airy inside comes from a proper proof, not baking powder. Fry at a steady 350F and glaze them while still warm so the glaze thins and sets in a clear shell.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Proof the yeastStir the yeast into milk warmed to about 110F with a pinch of sugar and wait 5-10 min until it foams.
  2. Mix and kneadMix in the flour, 1/4 cup sugar, butter, and eggs, then knead 8 min until smooth and elastic.
  3. First riseCover and let rise about 1 hour until doubled, then roll to 1/2 inch and cut rings.
  4. Second riseLet the cut donuts proof 30-45 min until puffy and light to the touch.
  5. Fry and glazeFry at 350F about 1 min per side until golden, then dip the warm donuts in a powdered-sugar glaze.
