Chicken Parmesan, and Where to Source It

The cutlet has to stay crisp under the sauce, so you sauce lightly and bake short. A cutlet pounded to even thickness cooks through before the crust burns, and a real Parmigiano-Reggiano rind in the sauce is what carries the whole plate.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Pound and breadPound cutlets to an even 1/2 inch. Dredge in flour, then egg, then crumbs mixed with a little Parmesan; press so the crust sticks.
  2. Fry crispFry in 1/4 inch of oil at 350F, about 3 minutes a side, until deep gold and 155F inside. Drain on a rack, not paper, so they stay crisp.
  3. Sauce and cookSimmer crushed tomatoes with a smashed garlic clove 20 minutes until thick enough to mound on a spoon.
  4. Top and bakeSpoon a thin layer of sauce over each cutlet, top with mozzarella and Parmesan, and bake at 425F for 8 to 10 minutes until the cheese bubbles. Basil at the table.
