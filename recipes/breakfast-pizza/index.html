Breakfast Pizza, and Where to Source It

A real pizza dough and a good sausage make this more than novelty. The one technique is the egg timing — you par-bake the crust with the toppings, then add the eggs partway so they finish with the yolks still soft.

Can you make Breakfast Pizza in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Heat the ovenPut a stone or steel in the oven and heat to 500F for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Brown the sausageCrumble and cook the sausage in a hot pan until browned and cooked through, then drain.
  3. Top and par-bakeStretch the dough, top with cheese and sausage, and bake 6 to 7 minutes until the crust is setting and the cheese melts.
  4. Add the eggsPull the pizza out, crack the eggs into wells in the cheese, and return to the oven 4 to 5 minutes until whites set and yolks are still soft.
  5. FinishScatter scallions and a pinch of salt over the top and slice.
