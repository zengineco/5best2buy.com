Doro Wat, and Where to Source It

The whole dish lives or dies on the onions, which you cook down dry for 30 to 45 minutes before any fat goes in until they turn to a jammy paste. Good berbere and real spiced butter carry the flavor, so buy both well. Rushing the onion base gives you a thin, harsh sauce.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Dry-cook onionsCook finely diced onions in a dry pot over medium-low, stirring, 30 to 45 min until collapsed and jammy. They should be a deep brown paste, not raw.
  2. Bloom berbereAdd niter kibbeh and berbere; cook 5 min until the fat runs red and the raw spice smell is gone.
  3. BraiseAdd chicken and a cup of water, cover, and simmer gently 60 to 90 min until the meat pulls from the bone.
  4. FinishStir in grated garlic and ginger, nestle in the scored hard-boiled eggs, and simmer uncovered 15 min to tighten the sauce.
