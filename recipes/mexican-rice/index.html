Mexican Rice, and Where to Source It

Restaurant arroz rojo is about two moves: toast the dry rice in oil until it smells nutty and turns chalky-white, then use the right liquid ratio so it steams dry instead of turning to mush. Skip the toast and you get sticky rice; overdo the liquid and it clumps.

The Method
  1. ToastFry the rinsed and dried rice in the oil over medium heat, stirring, until it turns chalky white and smells nutty, about 5 minutes.
  2. BlendPuree the tomato, onion, and garlic and add enough water to make 3 cups of liquid total.
  3. SimmerPour the liquid over the rice, bring to a boil, then cover and cook on low 18 minutes.
  4. RestOff the heat, let it sit covered 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.

Straight Answers
Can you make Mexican Rice in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Mexican Rice? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Mexican Rice ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Mexican Rice gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Mexican Rice vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.
