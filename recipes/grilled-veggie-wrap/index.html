Grilled Veggie Wrap, and Where to Source It

Without meat to lean on, the vegetables have to taste like something, so grill them hard for real char instead of steaming them soft. The one technique: get the grill or pan smoking hot and leave the vegetables undisturbed so they mark and caramelize rather than sweat.

The Method
  1. Prep the vegetablesSlice the zucchini into planks and the peppers and onion into wide strips, then toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill hardGrill or sear over high heat, undisturbed, until well charred and tender, about 3-4 minutes a side.
  3. Warm the tortillasWarm each tortilla 20 seconds so it folds without tearing.
  4. FillSpread hummus down the center, pile on the grilled vegetables, and crumble feta over the top.
  5. RollFold in the sides, roll tight, and cut in half on the bias; sear seam-side down for a crisp edge if you like.

Straight Answers
Can you make Grilled Veggie Wrap in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Grilled Veggie Wrap? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Grilled Veggie Wrap ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Grilled Veggie Wrap gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in a gluten-free flour blend (check the other labels too).

Is Grilled Veggie Wrap vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy.
