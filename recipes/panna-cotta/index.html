Panna Cotta, and Where to Source It

Panna cotta lives or dies on the dairy, since it is mostly cream, sugar, and vanilla, so use the best you can find. Use just enough gelatin for a trembling, barely-there set, and never boil the cream or you cook off the fresh flavor.

Part Two
The Method
  1. BloomSprinkle the gelatin over 3 tbsp cold water and let it sit 5 minutes until spongy.
  2. WarmHeat the cream, milk, sugar, and scraped vanilla to a bare steam (about 175F); do not let it boil.
  3. DissolveOff the heat, whisk in the bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved, then strain the mixture.
  4. SetPour into 6 ramekins and chill at least 4 hours until just set with a soft jiggle; top each with jam to serve.
