Eggplant Rollatini, and Where to Source It

Salt the eggplant slices and let them drain so they cook down without turning bitter or waterlogging the dish. Roast them until fully pliable before rolling; a plank that still has a raw core cracks and springs open in the sauce.

Straight Answers
Can you make Eggplant Rollatini in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Eggplant Rollatini? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Eggplant Rollatini ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Eggplant Rollatini gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Eggplant Rollatini vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Salt and drainSlice the eggplant lengthwise 1/4 inch thick, salt, and drain 30 minutes; pat dry to draw out the bitterness and water.
  2. Roast the planksBrush with oil and roast at 400F for 15 minutes until fully pliable and lightly browned.
  3. Fill and rollBeat ricotta with parmesan, the egg, and basil. Spread on each plank and roll up tight.
  4. Bake in sauceSet the rolls seam-down in tomato sauce, top with mozzarella, and bake at 375F for 20 minutes until bubbling.
