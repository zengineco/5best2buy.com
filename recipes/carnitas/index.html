Carnitas, and Where to Source It

Carnitas is pork confit, so the meat and the fat both matter: a well-marbled shoulder and real lard to cook it in. The technique is two-stage, a low slow braise in fat until fork-tender, then cranking the heat at the end to crisp the edges without drying the inside.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Start the confitPut pork chunks in a heavy pot with the lard, quartered onion, garlic, orange juice and spent halves, and a big pinch of salt. Add water to nearly cover.
  2. Braise lowSimmer gently over low heat, uncovered, 2.5 to 3 hours until the meat shreds with a fork and the water has cooked off, leaving the fat behind.
  3. Crisp itRaise the heat to medium-high and let the pork fry in its own rendered fat, stirring occasionally, 10 to 15 minutes until the edges brown and crisp.
  4. Shred and servePull the pork into rough shreds, keeping some crisp bits, and pile onto warm tortillas with onion, cilantro, and lime.
