Pozole Rojo, and Where to Source It

Pozole is a long-simmer dish where good pork and a real toasted-chile broth do the work; the guajillos and anchos have to be fresh enough to be pliable. The technique is a slow braise so the pork gives up its gelatin and the broth turns silky, then blending the chiles in for color and depth.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Braise the porkCover cubed pork with water, add onion and garlic, and simmer 90 minutes, skimming the foam, until the meat is tender.
  2. Build the chile brothStem and seed the chiles, toast 30 seconds, soak 15 minutes, then blend smooth with a cup of the soaking liquid and strain.
  3. CombineFry the strained chile puree in a little oil for 5 minutes, then stir it into the pork broth with the drained hominy.
  4. Finish the simmerSimmer everything together 45 minutes to an hour so the flavors marry. Serve with bowls of shredded cabbage, sliced radish, onion, oregano, and lime.
