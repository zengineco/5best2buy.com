Ragu Bolognese, and Where to Source It

Bolognese is barely a tomato sauce; it is meat, aromatics, wine, and milk cooked down for hours until it turns velvety. Brown the meat hard first for the deep flavor, and add the milk before the wine so it coats the meat.

The Method
  1. Build the soffrittoSweat the finely diced onion, carrot, and celery in butter and oil over low heat 10 minutes until soft and sweet, no color.
  2. Brown the meatRaise the heat and brown the meat hard, breaking it up, until the liquid cooks off and it starts to fry and stick, about 12 minutes.
  3. Milk then winePour in the milk and simmer until nearly gone, then the wine and reduce again. Add the tomatoes and rind.
  4. Simmer lowBarely simmer, partly covered, 2.5 to 3 hours, stirring now and then, until glossy and thick. Toss with tagliatelle and the starchy water.

Straight Answers
Can you make Ragu Bolognese in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Ragu Bolognese? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Ragu Bolognese ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Ragu Bolognese gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Ragu Bolognese vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
