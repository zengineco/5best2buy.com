Greek Salad, and Where to Source It

A real brined feta and good olives are the whole personality of this salad, so buy the good ones. The one technique: salt the tomatoes and let them weep, then use that juice in the dressing instead of throwing it out.

Straight Answers
Can you make Greek Salad in an air fryer? — Not needed

It’s a salad — nothing to air-fry once it’s tossed. You can crisp a topping in one, though: croutons, bacon, or chickpeas in a single layer while you build the rest.

Can you freeze Greek Salad? — Best fresh

Not really — the vegetables and dressing turn to mush once thawed. Salads are a make-fresh thing.

Can you make Greek Salad ahead of time? — Best fresh

Dress it just before serving — the greens wilt and brown once coated. Wash, cut, and chill the components ahead, then toss at the last minute.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Salt the tomatoesCut tomatoes into wedges, salt them, and let them sit 10 minutes. The released juice becomes part of the dressing instead of watering it down on the plate.
  2. Whisk the dressingWhisk olive oil, red wine vinegar, dried oregano, and the tomato juices together. Taste for salt and acid before it hits the bowl.
  3. Build chunkyCombine cucumber, onion, olives, and tomatoes in big rustic pieces. This is a knife-and-fork salad, so keep everything hearty.
  4. Feta lastLay the feta on top in a slab or big crumbles and pour the dressing over. Adding it last keeps it from smearing into paste.
