Bibimbap, and Where to Source It

The whole dish turns on the rice, so a good short-grain that stays chewy pays off, and a proper gochujang brings fermented depth no ketchup-based sub can. The technique that makes it bibimbap and not a salad: press the rice into a hot oiled pan so the bottom forms a golden crust before you build the bowl.

Straight Answers
Can you make Bibimbap in an air fryer? — Not needed

It’s a salad — nothing to air-fry once it’s tossed. You can crisp a topping in one, though: croutons, bacon, or chickpeas in a single layer while you build the rest.

Can you freeze Bibimbap? — Best fresh

Not really — the vegetables and dressing turn to mush once thawed. Salads are a make-fresh thing.

Can you make Bibimbap ahead of time? — Best fresh

Dress it just before serving — the greens wilt and brown once coated. Wash, cut, and chill the components ahead, then toss at the last minute.

Is Bibimbap gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Bibimbap vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Season the namulBlanch spinach and sprouts, saute carrots, and dress each pile separately with sesame oil, garlic and salt so they keep their own flavor.
  2. Marinate beefToss thin-sliced sirloin with soy, sesame oil and garlic, then sear hot and fast in a dry-hot pan.
  3. Crisp the ricePress warm rice into a hot oiled pan or stone bowl and leave it undisturbed to form a golden crust.
  4. BuildArrange the vegetables and beef in wedges over the rice, top with a runny fried egg, and add a spoon of thinned gochujang.
  5. MixAt the table, break the yolk and stir everything hard so the rice, egg and gochujang bind into one.
