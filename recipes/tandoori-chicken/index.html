Tandoori Chicken, and Where to Source It

The yogurt is not just flavor; its acid and enzymes tenderize the meat, so give it at least four hours and overnight is better. Slash the chicken to the bone so the marinade gets in, and roast as hot as your oven goes to mimic the char of a tandoor.

The Method
  1. SlashCut deep slashes to the bone in each piece so the marinade reaches the center.
  2. MarinateCoat in the spiced yogurt with garlic, ginger, and lemon. Chill at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  3. PreheatSet the oven to 500F with a rack in the top third and a foil-lined tray beneath.
  4. RoastRoast 25 to 30 minutes until charred at the edges and 175F at the bone.
  5. RestRest 5 minutes and finish with a squeeze of lemon.

Straight Answers
Can you make Tandoori Chicken in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Tandoori Chicken? — Not ideal

You can, but the crisp coating softens. Freeze the cooked pieces, then re-crisp them in a hot oven or air fryer — never the microwave.

Can you make Tandoori Chicken ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Tandoori Chicken gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Tandoori Chicken vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
