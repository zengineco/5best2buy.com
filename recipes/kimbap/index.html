Kimbap, and where to source it

Season the rice with sesame oil and salt, not vinegar. Kimbap is not sushi; the sesame-and-salt rice and a brush of sesame oil on the finished roll are exactly what separates it from a California roll.

The Method
  1. Season the riceToss warm cooked rice with sesame oil and salt and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Prep the fillingsFry and slice the egg, saute the carrot, blanch and season the spinach, and cook the beef.
  3. Lay the basePlace a seaweed sheet on a bamboo mat and spread a thin even layer of rice, leaving a bare top edge.
  4. Fill and rollLine the fillings across the near third, then roll tight with the mat, sealing the bare edge.
  5. Finish and sliceBrush the roll with sesame oil, sprinkle seeds, and slice into coins with a sharp wet knife.

Straight Answers
Can you make Kimbap in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Kimbap? — Not ideal

You can, but the crisp coating softens. Freeze the cooked pieces, then re-crisp them in a hot oven or air fryer — never the microwave.

Can you make Kimbap ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Kimbap gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Kimbap vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
