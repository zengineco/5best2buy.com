Soft-Boiled Eggs, and Where to Source It

The egg is the entire dish, so freshness and quality show. The one technique is exact timing from a rolling boil — six minutes gives a runny yolk, seven a jammy one — and an ice bath to stop the cooking dead.

The Method
  1. Boil the waterBring a pot of water to a full rolling boil over high heat.
  2. Lower the eggsGently lower cold-from-the-fridge eggs into the water with a spoon so they don't crack.
  3. Time itBoil exactly 6 minutes for a runny yolk or 7 for a jammy set, adjusting the heat to keep a steady boil.
  4. Ice bathMove the eggs straight into ice water for 1 minute to stop cooking and loosen the shell.
  5. ServeSet in egg cups and crack the top, or peel, halve, and season with salt. Serve with toast strips.

Straight Answers
Can you make Soft-Boiled Eggs in an air fryer? — Sometimes

Sometimes — if the pieces are small and cooked dry, an air fryer can handle it in a single layer; anything saucy or liquid needs a pan. When in doubt, the stovetop or oven is the safe call.

Can you freeze Soft-Boiled Eggs? — Yes

Yes — cool it, portion into airtight containers, and freeze; thaw in the fridge and reheat.

Can you make Soft-Boiled Eggs ahead of time? — Partly

The components hold — prep the parts ahead and finish it close to serving for the best texture.

Is Soft-Boiled Eggs gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Soft-Boiled Eggs vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.
