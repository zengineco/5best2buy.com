Chakalaka, and Where to Source It

Chakalaka is built on grated carrot and curry powder cooked into a tomato base, with baked beans stirred in at the end for body. Grate the carrot so it softens fast and melts into the sauce. Cook the curry powder in oil before the vegetables or it tastes raw and dusty.

Straight Answers
Can you make Chakalaka in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Chakalaka? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Chakalaka ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

The Method
  1. Bloom curryFry onion in oil until soft, add curry powder and chili, and cook 2 min until fragrant.
  2. Soften vegAdd diced pepper and grated carrot and cook 5 min until they start to slump.
  3. SimmerAdd chopped tomatoes and simmer 10 min until thick and jammy.
  4. Fold beansStir in the baked beans, warm through 5 min, and season; serve warm or at room temperature.
