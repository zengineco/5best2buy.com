Split Pea Soup, and Where to Source It

Split peas from a good source cook evenly and don't stay chalky in the center. The one thing that carries the flavor: a smoky ham hock or bacon simmered in from the start, so the smoke gets into the whole pot instead of sitting on top.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Render and softenIf using bacon, render it first, then cook the diced onion, carrot, and celery in the fat until soft, 8 minutes.
  2. Simmer the peasAdd the rinsed split peas, the broth, and the ham hock. Bring to a simmer and cook uncovered 60 minutes, stirring now and then so the bottom doesn't catch.
  3. Pull the hockWhen the peas have collapsed into a thick puree, lift out the hock, shred any meat off it, and stir the meat back in.
  4. SeasonSalt to taste at the end, since the ham already brought salt. Loosen with a splash of broth if it's thicker than you want.
