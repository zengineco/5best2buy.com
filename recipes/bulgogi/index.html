Bulgogi, and Where to Source It

Well-marbled beef sliced paper-thin is the whole point, so quality meat and a sharp knife matter more than any step. The technique: get the pan or grill screaming hot and cook in a single uncrowded layer so the sugar in the marinade caramelizes instead of stewing.

Straight Answers
Can you make Bulgogi in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Bulgogi? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Bulgogi ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Slice thinFirm the beef in the freezer 20 minutes, then slice against the grain as thin as you can.
  2. MarinateBlend soy, sesame oil, honey, grated pear, garlic and ginger; toss the beef and rest at least 30 minutes.
  3. Get it screaming hotHeat a heavy pan or grill until it smokes; the sugar needs the heat to caramelize.
  4. Sear in batchesCook in a single uncrowded layer for a minute or two per side until the edges char, then pull. Crowding steams it.
  5. ServePile into lettuce wraps or over rice, scatter scallion and sesame seeds.
