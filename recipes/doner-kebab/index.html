Döner Kebab, and Where to Source It

Real döner is a stacked rotisserie you can't build at home, so the honest home version packs seasoned lamb and beef into a loaf, roasts it, then thin-slices and re-crisps the shavings in a hot pan. Fat content is everything here: use meat that's at least 20% fat or the shavings turn to sawdust. The one move that matters is the double cook — roast the loaf to 160°F, chill it firm, then sear the slices.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the pasteBlitz onion, garlic, spices and salt into a paste, then knead into the lamb and beef for 3 full minutes until the mix turns sticky and tacky.
  2. Pack the loafPress the meat hard into a loaf pan with no air gaps — density is what lets you slice thin later.
  3. RoastBake at 325°F until the center hits 160°F, about 45 minutes, then chill at least 4 hours or overnight until firm.
  4. Shave and crispSlice the cold loaf as thin as you can, then sear the shavings in a dry hot pan until the edges brown and curl, 2-3 minutes.
  5. BuildPile into warm pita with raw onion, tomato and a swipe of yogurt.
