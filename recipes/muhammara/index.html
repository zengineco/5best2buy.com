Muhammara, and Where to Source It

Muhammara balances three loud flavors — sweet roasted peppers, tannic walnuts and sour-sweet pomegranate molasses — so taste as you go and adjust the molasses last. Toast the walnuts hard; raw ones taste flat and pasty in the blend. Char the peppers until the skins blacken so they slip off and leave you smoky flesh, not watery raw pepper.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Char the peppersRoast peppers over flame or at 450°F until the skins blacken, then steam covered 10 minutes and peel.
  2. Toast the walnutsToast walnuts in a dry pan until fragrant and lightly browned, 5 minutes, then cool.
  3. BlendPulse peppers, walnuts, breadcrumbs, Aleppo and olive oil into a coarse paste — leave some texture.
  4. BalanceStir in pomegranate molasses a spoon at a time, tasting until sweet, sour and smoky sit even.
  5. ServeSpread on a plate, drizzle with oil, and scoop with warm bread.
