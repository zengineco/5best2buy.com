Ash Reshteh, and Where to Source It

Ash reshteh is a hearty herb-and-legume soup where the reshteh noodles thicken the broth as they cook, so the finished bowl is nearly stew-thick. The garnishes aren't optional: fried mint (na'na dagh), crispy onions, and kashk (fermented whey) are what turn it from bean soup into ash. Cook the noodles right in the pot at the end so their starch does the thickening.

Straight Answers
Can you make Ash Reshteh in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Ash Reshteh? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Ash Reshteh ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Cook the legumesSimmer soaked beans and lentils with turmeric until tender, about 45 minutes, topping up water as needed.
  2. Add the greensStir in all the chopped herbs and greens and simmer 30 minutes until the soup turns deep green.
  3. Add noodlesBreak in the reshteh and cook 8-10 minutes, stirring so they don't clump, until the soup is thick.
  4. Make the garnishesFry sliced onion until dark and crisp; in another pan bloom dried mint in oil for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. ServeLadle into bowls and top with kashk or yogurt, fried mint, and crispy onions.
