Black Bean Soup, and Where to Source It

Cooking heirloom black beans from dry gives you a broth you cannot buy in a can, and a toasted dried chile deepens it past plain beans. The technique is blending only part of the pot, so you get a creamy base with whole beans still in it.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Sweat aromaticsCook diced onion and garlic in oil over medium heat until soft, about 5 minutes, then stir in cumin and oregano for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. SimmerAdd rinsed beans, the torn ancho, and 8 cups water. Simmer uncovered 90 minutes to 2 hours until beans are fully soft. Salt in the last 20 minutes.
  3. Blend part of itLadle 2 cups of beans and liquid into a blender, puree until smooth, and stir back in for a creamy body with whole beans intact.
  4. Adjust and serveLoosen with water to the thickness you like, check salt, and top bowls with onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
