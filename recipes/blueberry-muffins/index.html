Blueberry Muffins, and Where to Source It

Real butter and good eggs make these richer than the oil-based box mix, and fresh in-season blueberries beat frozen every time. The one technique is a hot-then-moderate oven — you start at a high temperature to spring the domes tall, then drop it to bake through without burning.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Heat the ovenHeat the oven to 425F and line a 12-cup muffin tin with papers.
  2. CreamBeat the softened butter with the sugar until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes, then beat in the eggs one at a time and 1 teaspoon vanilla.
  3. Alternate dry and wetWhisk the flour with 2 teaspoons baking powder and 1/2 teaspoon salt, then add to the butter in three parts, alternating with the milk, mixing just until combined.
  4. Fold the berriesToss the blueberries with a spoon of flour and fold them in gently so they do not bleed or sink.
  5. Bake hot then lowFill the cups to the top, sprinkle with sugar, and bake at 425F for 5 minutes, then drop to 375F and bake 15 to 18 minutes more, until a toothpick comes out clean.
