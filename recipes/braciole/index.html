Braciole, and Where to Source It

Pound the beef thin and even so it rolls tight and cooks through; a thick slice stays tough no matter how long it braises. Brown the rolls hard before they go in the sauce, then hold a bare simmer for two hours, because a boil turns the collagen rubbery instead of silky.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the fillingMix breadcrumbs, Parmesan, minced garlic, parsley, and olive oil into a moist filling. Season well.
  2. Roll and tiePound the beef to 1/4 inch, spread filling thin, roll tight, and tie with twine or secure with toothpicks.
  3. BrownBrown the rolls in oil on all sides, then remove; deglaze the pan with red wine, scraping the fond.
  4. BraiseAdd crushed tomatoes, return the rolls, cover, and simmer 2 to 2.5 hours until fork-tender. Snip the twine before serving.
