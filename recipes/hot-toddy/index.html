Hot Toddy, and Where to Source It

A toddy is a balancing act of hot water, honey, lemon, and whiskey, and the trap is boiling water that scalds off the aromatics. Heat the water hot but not boiling, dissolve the honey first, and add the whiskey last so the alcohol doesn't cook off.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Steep the waterHeat water hot but not boiling, around 190F, and steep the cinnamon, cloves, and ginger 3-4 min.
  2. Dissolve the honeyStir the honey into the hot spiced water until fully dissolved, then add the lemon juice.
  3. Add whiskey lastPour in the whiskey off the heat so the alcohol and aroma stay, and serve hot.
