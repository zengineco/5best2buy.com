Beef Chow Fun, and Where to Source It

Fresh wide rice noodles take on wok hei, the charred smokiness, when a screaming-hot pan is left alone long enough to blister them. The technique that decides it is heat and restraint: get the wok ripping, spread the noodles flat, and resist stirring so they scorch rather than clump and tear.

Straight Answers
Can you make Beef Chow Fun in an air fryer? — Sometimes

Sometimes — if the pieces are small and cooked dry, an air fryer can handle it in a single layer; anything saucy or liquid needs a pan. When in doubt, the stovetop or oven is the safe call.

Can you freeze Beef Chow Fun? — Yes

Yes — cool it, portion into airtight containers, and freeze; thaw in the fridge and reheat.

Can you make Beef Chow Fun ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it needs the chill time to set, so making it ahead is the whole point.

Is Beef Chow Fun gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Beef Chow Fun vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Prep noodlesIf refrigerated, microwave the noodles briefly and separate the strands by hand so they don't break in the wok.
  2. Sear beefVelvet the sliced steak in cornstarch, then sear in a smoking-hot wok 1 minute until browned and set it aside.
  3. Char noodlesAdd noodles to the dry-hot wok, spread flat, and leave them 30 seconds to blister before tossing.
  4. SeasonAdd regular and dark soy, tossing so every strand colors evenly without breaking them up.
  5. FinishFold the beef, sprouts and scallions back in, hit it with sesame oil off heat, and serve at once.
