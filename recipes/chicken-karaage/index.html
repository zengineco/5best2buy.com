Chicken Karaage, and Where to Source It

Thigh meat and a potato-starch coat are what set karaage apart: the starch fries into a lacy, glassy shell that stays crunchy while the dark meat stays juicy. Double-frying is the trick, resting the pieces between fries so the second dip drives out moisture and locks in the crackle.

Part Two
The Method
  1. MarinateCut chicken into 2-inch pieces and marinate in soy, sesame oil, and grated ginger and garlic for 20 minutes.
  2. DredgeRoll each piece in potato starch and let them sit 5 minutes so the coating hydrates into a paste-like shell.
  3. First fryFry at 325F for 90 seconds until pale and set, then lift out and rest 4 minutes.
  4. Second fryRaise the oil to 360F and fry again for 90 seconds until deep gold and crackly.
  5. Drain and serveRest on a rack, salt right away, and serve hot with lemon wedges.
