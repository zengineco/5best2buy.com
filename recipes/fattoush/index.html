Fattoush, and Where to Source It

Fattoush is a bright chopped salad whose signature is crisp fried pita and tart sumac. The one move that matters: toast the pita until fully crisp and add it at the last second so it stays crunchy, not soggy. Sumac and lemon carry the whole dressing, so do not skip either.

The Method
  1. Crisp the pitaTear the pita, toss with olive oil, and toast at 375F for 8 to 10 minutes until fully crisp; cool.
  2. Make the dressingWhisk lemon juice, olive oil, sumac, garlic, and salt into a tart dressing.
  3. ChopChop the romaine, cucumber, tomato, and radish the same size and pile in a wide bowl with the herbs.
  4. DressToss the vegetables with the dressing just before serving so they stay crisp.
  5. Add pita lastFold in the crisp pita at the last second and dust with more sumac so it does not go soggy.

Straight Answers
Can you make Fattoush in an air fryer? — Not needed

It’s a salad — nothing to air-fry once it’s tossed. You can crisp a topping in one, though: croutons, bacon, or chickpeas in a single layer while you build the rest.

Can you freeze Fattoush? — Best fresh

Not really — the vegetables and dressing turn to mush once thawed. Salads are a make-fresh thing.

Can you make Fattoush ahead of time? — Best fresh

Dress it just before serving — the greens wilt and brown once coated. Wash, cut, and chill the components ahead, then toss at the last minute.

Is Fattoush gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in gluten-free versions of the wheat items (check the other labels too).

Is Fattoush vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.
