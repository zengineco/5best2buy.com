Shakshuka, and Where to Source It

Eggs poached in a spiced tomato-and-pepper sauce, eaten straight from the pan with bread — breakfast, lunch, or a lazy dinner. It's cheap, it's one pan, and it lives or dies on two things: a real canned tomato and good eggs. Here's how, and where to buy them.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Build the baseSoften the sliced onion and red pepper in olive oil over medium-low heat until sweet and jammy — about 10 minutes, no rushing. Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, and a pinch of chile and toast for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Simmer the sauceAdd the tomatoes, crushing any whole ones by hand, season with salt, and simmer until it thickens and stops looking watery — about 10 minutes. A loose sauce makes for weepy eggs.
  3. Poach the eggsMake little wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each. Cover the pan and cook gently until the whites are just set but the yolks are still runny — 5 to 8 minutes. Watch it; carryover heat firms the yolks fast.
  4. FinishScatter with crumbled feta and chopped herbs and bring the whole pan to the table with a stack of bread. Scoop, don't plate.
