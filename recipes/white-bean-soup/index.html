Tuscan White Bean Soup, and Where to Source It

Dried cannellini from a good source cook up creamy with intact skins, worlds apart from mushy canned. The quiet trick: drop a parmesan rind into the pot from the start so it dissolves its savory depth into the broth over the long simmer.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Start the beansSoak the beans overnight, then drain. Cover with the broth plus water, add the garlic, herbs, and parmesan rind, and bring to a simmer.
  2. Simmer lowHold at a gentle simmer 60 to 75 minutes until the beans are creamy. Salt only in the last 15 minutes so the skins stay tender.
  3. Thicken naturallyScoop out a cup of beans, mash them, and stir them back in to thicken the broth without any cream.
  4. FinishPull the rind and herb stems. Ladle into bowls and finish each with a hard drizzle of the raw olive oil.
