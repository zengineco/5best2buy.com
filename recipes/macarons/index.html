French Macarons, and Where to Source It

Macarons live or die on two moves: macaronage, folding the batter until it ribbons and slowly settles, and resting the piped shells until a skin forms. Skip the skin and they crack; overfold and they spread flat with no feet.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Sift the drySift almond flour and powdered sugar twice; any lumps show as bumps on the shells.
  2. Whip the meringueBeat whites with cream of tartar to soft peaks, add sugar, and whip to stiff, glossy peaks that hold a straight point.
  3. MacaronageFold the dry into the meringue until the batter flows like slow lava and a ribbon sinks back in about 10 seconds.
  4. Pipe and restPipe 1.5-inch rounds, rap the tray, and rest 30-60 min until the tops feel dry and no longer tacky to a light touch.
  5. Bake and fillBake at 300F for 14-16 min until the feet set and the shells lift clean; cool, then sandwich with ganache.
