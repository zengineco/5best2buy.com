Meatball Sub, and Where to Source It

Meatballs stay tender when the panko-and-milk soak keeps them from tightening up, and simmering them in the sauce is what marries the two. Toast the roll first so it does not turn to paste under the sauce, then broil to melt the cheese.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Soak the pankoSoften panko in milk 5 minutes so the meatballs stay tender.
  2. Form and brownMix meat, panko, egg, and garlic, roll into balls, and brown all over in a hot pan.
  3. Simmer in sauceDrop the meatballs into marinara and simmer 20 minutes until cooked through at 160F.
  4. Build and broilToast the rolls, fill with meatballs and sauce, top with provolone, and broil until bubbling.
