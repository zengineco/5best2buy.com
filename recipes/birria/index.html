Birria, and Where to Source It

Birria is a chile braise, so a blend of good dried chiles and a well-marbled cut build the brick-red consomme. The one technique: toast and blend the chiles, then braise low for hours so the meat falls apart and the fat rises for dipping.

The Method
  1. Build the chile pasteToast the dried chiles until fragrant, soak them soft, then blend with garlic and spices into a smooth paste.
  2. Braise lowCoat the beef in the chile paste, cover with broth, and braise low for three hours until it shreds.
  3. Separate the fatShred the meat and skim the red fat off the top of the consomme for dipping the tortillas.
  4. Dip and fryDip each tortilla in the fat, griddle with cheese and meat, and fold into a crisp taco.
  5. Serve with consommeServe the tacos alongside a bowl of hot consomme topped with onion and cilantro.

Straight Answers
Can you make Birria in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Birria? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Birria ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Birria gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Birria vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
