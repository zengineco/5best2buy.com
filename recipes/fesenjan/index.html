Fesenjan, and Where to Source It

Fesenjan hinges on toasting and grinding walnuts, then cooking them long enough that they break down and release their oil, thickening the stew and turning it deep brown. The balance of sweet and sour from pomegranate molasses is the whole dish — taste and adjust with a little sugar or more molasses near the end. Stir often once the walnuts go in, because the oil can scorch on the bottom.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Grind the walnutsToast walnuts in a dry pan until fragrant, cool, then grind to a coarse paste in a food processor.
  2. Build the baseFry onion until soft, add turmeric, then brown the chicken pieces and set them aside.
  3. Cook the walnutsAdd ground walnuts and water, simmer on low 45 minutes stirring often, until the oil separates and the sauce darkens.
  4. Add molassesStir in pomegranate molasses and sugar, return the chicken, and simmer 45-60 minutes more until thick and mahogany.
  5. Balance and serveTaste and adjust sweet-sour, then serve over basmati rice with a drizzle of the surfaced oil.
