Bruschetta, and Where to Source It

The name means the toast, not the topping, so a crusty sturdy loaf that chars without falling apart is the foundation. The move most people skip: rub the raw garlic clove across the hot toasted surface, which grates just enough garlic into the rough crust.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Dress the tomatoDice tomatoes, salt them, and let sit 10 min in a strainer so they shed water; toss with olive oil and torn basil.
  2. Grill the breadGrill or broil thick slices until charred at the edges and crisp outside but still chewy within, about 2 min a side.
  3. Rub with garlicWhile hot, rub a halved raw garlic clove firmly across the rough toasted surface; the crust grates it in.
  4. Top and serveSpoon the drained tomato over each toast, drizzle olive oil, and finish with flaky salt; serve at once before it softens.
