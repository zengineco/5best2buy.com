Spaetzle, and Where to Source It

Spaetzle lives on eggs and flour, so both should be good since there is nowhere for them to hide. The batter should ribbon off the spoon in a slow stream; too stiff and it clogs the press, too loose and it dissolves.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Mix batterWhisk flour, eggs, milk, salt, and nutmeg to a thick batter that ribbons slowly off the whisk; rest 10 minutes.
  2. PressPush the batter through a spaetzle press or colander into salted boiling water.
  3. BoilThey are done about 30 seconds after they float to the surface; lift out and drain.
  4. BrownToss the drained noodles in browned butter over medium-high until the edges crisp and catch color.
