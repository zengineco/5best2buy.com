Southern Comeback Sauce, and where to source it

Grate the onion in raw on the small holes of a box grater. You want its sharp juice carried all through the sauce, not minced pieces sitting in lumps — the grated pulp disappears and leaves only the bite.

Straight Answers
Can you freeze Southern Comeback Sauce? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Southern Comeback Sauce ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it keeps in the fridge for several days; stir or shake it before serving.

Is Southern Comeback Sauce gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Southern Comeback Sauce vegan? — No

No — it contains seafood, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. GrateGrate the garlic and onion fine so they melt into the sauce as pulp and juice, not pieces.
  2. WhiskWhisk everything together until smooth and one even color.
  3. RestChill at least an hour — the raw onion and garlic soften and spread through the whole bowl.
