Pavlova, and Where to Source It

A pavlova lives or dies on the meringue: it must be crisp outside and soft as marshmallow within. Whip the whites to stiff glossy peaks and bake low and slow, then cool it in the turned-off oven so it does not crack or weep.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Whip the whitesBeat whites with cream of tartar to soft peaks, then add sugar a spoon at a time and whip to stiff glossy peaks that hold when you lift the beater.
  2. Fold in cornstarchSift in the cornstarch and a splash of vinegar and fold gently to keep the volume.
  3. ShapeMound the meringue into an 8-inch disk on parchment, building the sides higher than the center to hold the topping.
  4. Bake lowBake at 250F for 75 min until the shell is dry and lifts cleanly, then turn off the oven and cool it inside with the door ajar.
  5. TopJust before serving, pile on whipped cream and the fresh fruit so the shell stays crisp.
