Fried Catfish, and Where to Source It

Fresh farm-raised catfish has clean, mild flesh that fries up sweet; a stale fillet turns muddy. The one technique: soak the fillets in buttermilk, then coat in cornmeal not flour, because cornmeal is what gives a catfish fry its signature gritty crunch.

The Method
  1. SoakSoak the fillets in buttermilk with a few dashes of hot sauce for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Season the mealMix the cornmeal with the Cajun seasoning and 1 tsp salt in a shallow dish.
  3. DredgeLift each fillet, let it drip, and press into the cornmeal on both sides to coat fully.
  4. FryFry in 350F oil, 4-5 minutes total, until the crust is deep golden and the fish flakes at 145F.
  5. DrainRest on a rack, salt right away, and serve with lemon and more hot sauce.

Straight Answers
Can you make Fried Catfish in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Fried Catfish? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Fried Catfish ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Fried Catfish gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Fried Catfish vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy.
