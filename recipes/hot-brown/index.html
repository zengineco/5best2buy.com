Kentucky Hot Brown, and Where to Source It

This Louisville hotel classic lives or dies on the Mornay, so real milk and a good grating cheese matter more than anything else. The one technique: cook the flour-butter roux two full minutes before the milk, then melt the cheese off the heat so the sauce stays glossy instead of grainy.

Can you make Kentucky Hot Brown in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Crisp the baconCook bacon until crisp, drain, and set aside for the topping.
  2. Build the rouxMelt butter, whisk in 4 tbsp flour, and cook 2 full minutes until it smells nutty and turns pale gold.
  3. Make the MornayWhisk in the milk, simmer 5 minutes until it coats a spoon, then pull off the heat and stir in most of the parmesan until glossy.
  4. AssembleSet a slice of thick toast in each gratin dish, pile on turkey, lay a tomato slice, and flood with Mornay; dust with the rest of the cheese.
  5. BroilBroil 4-5 minutes until the sauce bubbles and browns in spots, then cross two bacon strips over each and serve at once.
