Refried Beans, and Where to Source It

Heirloom pintos cooked from dry taste nothing like canned, and real lard gives the frying step its flavor and body. The technique is patience twice over: cook the beans until fully soft, then fry the mash long enough that it tightens and pulls from the pan.

Straight Answers
Can you make Refried Beans in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Refried Beans? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Refried Beans ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Simmer the beansCover rinsed beans with 2 inches of water, add half an onion and a clove of garlic, and simmer 90 minutes to 2 hours until completely soft. Salt only in the last 20 minutes.
  2. Render the aromaticsMelt the lard in a heavy skillet over medium heat and cook diced onion until translucent, about 4 minutes.
  3. Mash and fryAdd beans with a ladle of their liquid and mash with a potato masher or wooden spoon. Fry, stirring, 8 to 10 minutes until thick enough to pull cleanly from the pan.
  4. FinishLoosen with a splash of bean liquid if they tighten too far, then top with crumbled queso fresco.
