Oyakodon, and Where to Source It

Oyakodon means parent-and-child, chicken and egg, and the whole thing hinges on pulling the egg while it is still soft and glossy. Beat the eggs only loosely, pour them in two waves, and cut the heat while the top is nearly runny, because carryover finishes them into a silky curd.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Build the brothIn a small skillet simmer dashi, soy, and mirin with sliced onion until the onion softens.
  2. Cook the chickenAdd bite-sized chicken and poach in the broth until just cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  3. First eggPour in two-thirds of the loosely beaten egg and let it set halfway without stirring.
  4. Second eggAdd the rest of the egg, cover, and cook 30 seconds until barely set but still glossy, then kill the heat.
  5. ServeSlide the whole thing over a bowl of hot rice and top with scallion.
