Rugelach, and Where to Source It

Rugelach is a cream cheese dough, so the cream cheese and butter carry the flavor and the flake. The one thing that matters is keeping the dough cold at every stage; warm dough leaks filling and bakes greasy instead of shattering-flaky.

Can you make Rugelach in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. DoughPulse flour, cold cream cheese, cold butter, and 1/2 tsp salt until it clumps, then divide into 4 disks and chill 2 hours.
  2. RollRoll one cold disk into a 9-inch circle on a floured surface, keeping the rest chilled.
  3. FillSpread jam thinly, scatter walnuts and cinnamon sugar, then cut into 8 wedges.
  4. ShapeRoll each wedge from the wide edge to the point and curve into a crescent. Chill the shaped cookies 20 minutes.
  5. BakeBrush with egg wash, sprinkle with cinnamon sugar, and bake at 350F for 22-25 minutes until deep golden.
