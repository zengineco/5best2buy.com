Quesadilla, and Where to Source It

A quesadilla is cheese and tortilla, so both have to be good: real nixtamal corn tortillas and a cheese that actually strings, like Oaxaca. The one thing to get right is heat management on a dry pan so the tortilla toasts while the cheese melts through.

Straight Answers
Can you make Quesadilla in an air fryer? — Sometimes

Sometimes — if the pieces are small and cooked dry, an air fryer can handle it in a single layer; anything saucy or liquid needs a pan. When in doubt, the stovetop or oven is the safe call.

Can you freeze Quesadilla? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Quesadilla ahead of time? — Partly

The components hold — prep the parts ahead and finish it close to serving for the best texture.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Heat the comalSet a dry cast-iron comal or skillet over medium heat until a drop of water skitters and evaporates, about 3 minutes. No oil.
  2. Warm the tortillaLay a tortilla down for 20 seconds to soften, then flip it and pile shredded cheese on one half.
  3. Fold and toastFold the bare half over the cheese and press gently. Cook 2 to 3 minutes per side until the tortilla is spotted brown and the cheese has fully melted.
  4. Open and dressOpen at the fold, tuck in onion, cilantro, and a few drops of hot sauce, then close and eat while it strings.
