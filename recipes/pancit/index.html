Pancit, and Where to Source It

Pancit lives or dies on the noodles absorbing seasoned broth instead of plain water, so a real bone broth and naturally brewed soy do the heavy lifting. The one move: soak the bihon just until pliable, then finish cooking them in the broth so they drink up flavor instead of turning to mush.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Soak noodlesCover bihon in warm water 5 to 8 minutes until just pliable, then drain. They should still have a raw bite.
  2. Cook aromatics and chickenStir-fry garlic and sliced chicken over high heat until the chicken is cooked through, about 4 minutes, then add carrot and cabbage.
  3. Simmer noodles in brothPour in broth and soy, add noodles, and toss constantly. Cook until the noodles absorb nearly all the liquid, 4 to 6 minutes.
  4. FinishFold in scallions off the heat and squeeze citrus over the top. Serve with more citrus wedges on the side.
