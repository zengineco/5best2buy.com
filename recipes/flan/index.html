Flan, and Where to Source It

Flan is a custard set only by eggs, so the whole game is baking it gently in a water bath until it just sets without curdling into scrambled eggs. Pull it while the center still jiggles like loose jelly; it firms as it chills overnight.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the caramelMelt 3/4 cup sugar in a dry pan over medium until it turns deep amber, then pour into a 9-inch round pan and swirl to coat the bottom.
  2. Blend the custardWarm both milks with the orange strip and vanilla, discard the peel, then whisk into the eggs, yolks, and remaining sugar and strain out any lumps.
  3. Set the water bathPour the custard over the caramel, set the pan in a larger roasting pan, and add hot water halfway up the sides.
  4. Bake gentlyBake at 350F for 50-60 minutes until the center still jiggles as one soft mass; a set-solid center means it is overbaked.
  5. Chill and unmoldCool, then refrigerate at least 4 hours or overnight; run a knife around the edge and invert onto a rimmed plate to release the caramel.
