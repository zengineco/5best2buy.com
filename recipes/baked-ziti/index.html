Baked Ziti, and Where to Source It

Undercook the ziti hard, because it keeps cooking in the oven and absorbs sauce, so pasta that was al dente in the pot ends up mush in the dish. Reserve some sauce to coat the top layer or the exposed pasta dries out and burns.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the sauceSoften the onion and garlic in oil, add the crushed tomatoes, and simmer 25 minutes. Stir in basil.
  2. Undercook the zitiBoil the ziti four minutes short of al dente and drain; it firms and softens again in the oven.
  3. CombineToss the pasta with most of the sauce and the ricotta-parmesan mix, folding in half the mozzarella.
  4. Layer and bakeSpread in a dish, top with reserved sauce and the rest of the mozzarella, and bake at 400F for 25 minutes until browned and bubbling.
