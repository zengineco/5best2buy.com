Juicy Lucy, and Where to Source It

The cheese lives inside the patty, so a fresh fatty grind and a real melting cheese are everything. The one technique: seal the two patty halves completely around the cheese with no gaps, or the molten core blows out into the pan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Form the shellsDivide beef into 8 thin patties; pile folded cheese in the center of four of them, keeping a wide bare border.
  2. SealLay the second patty on top and pinch the edges tight all the way around so no cheese can escape; there can be no gaps.
  3. Griddle low and slowCook on a medium griddle 5-6 minutes a side; lower heat than a normal burger so the cheese melts before the beef overcooks.
  4. VentPoke one small hole in the top just before serving to release steam so it does not burst on the first bite.
  5. Build and restToast the buns, stack with onion and pickle, and let it rest 2 minutes so the molten cheese does not scald.
