Sole Meunière, and Where to Source It

There is nowhere to hide in sole meunière, so the fish has to be fresh and the butter has to be watched. The whole dish turns on the brown butter: you take it just to the point the milk solids smell nutty and go hazelnut-brown, then cut it with lemon before it burns.

Part Two
The Method
  1. DredgePat the fillets dry, season, and dredge lightly in flour, shaking off the excess.
  2. Fry the fishFry in foaming butter over medium-high heat about 2 minutes a side until golden; the thin fillets cook fast.
  3. PlateMove the fish to warm plates so it does not overcook in the pan.
  4. Brown the butterAdd fresh butter to the pan and cook until it smells nutty and turns hazelnut-brown, then kill the heat and add lemon juice.
  5. FinishPour the foaming lemon-butter over the fish and scatter parsley.
