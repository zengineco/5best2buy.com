Sundubu Jjigae, and Where to Source It

The dish rests on soft, barely-set sundubu tofu that you spoon straight into the pot so it stays in creamy clouds. The technique that makes it is blooming gochugaru in oil first to build a red chili base, which turns the broth glossy and deeply spicy rather than just watery and hot.

Can you make Sundubu Jjigae in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Bloom the chiliHeat sesame oil in a pot, add gochugaru and garlic, and stir 1 minute over low heat until the oil turns bright red. Don't let it burn or the broth goes bitter.
  2. Build brothPour in the stock and soy sauce, bring to a boil, and simmer 5 minutes to meld.
  3. Add tofuSpoon in the soft tofu in large chunks and simmer 5 minutes; don't stir hard or it breaks up completely.
  4. Crack the eggSlide a raw egg into the center, scatter scallion, and serve immediately while the broth is still furiously bubbling so the egg poaches at the table.
