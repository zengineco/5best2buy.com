Japchae, and Where to Source It

Sweet-potato glass noodles have a bounce that wheat noodles can't imitate, and a real brewed soy plus toasted sesame oil is what carries the whole dish. The technique: cook each vegetable separately over high heat so nothing steams, then fold everything into the seasoned noodles at the end.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Boil noodlesCook the glass noodles until translucent and springy, drain, and cut a few times with scissors so they toss evenly.
  2. Cook in batchesOver high heat, stir-fry onion, carrot, mushrooms and spinach separately so each keeps color and bite.
  3. Sear beefMarinate beef strips in soy, sesame oil and garlic, then sear hot and fast.
  4. Season noodlesToss the warm noodles with soy, sesame oil and a little sugar until glossy and evenly coated.
  5. CombineFold the beef and all the vegetables into the noodles, finish with toasted sesame seeds, and serve warm or at room temperature.
