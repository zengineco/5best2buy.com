Neapolitan Pizza, and Where to Source It

Great pizza is barely a recipe — it's four or five ingredients and a very hot oven. Which means there's nowhere to hide: the flour, the tomatoes, and the mozzarella are the pizza. Use 00 flour instead of all-purpose, real San Marzano instead of mystery tomatoes, and fresh mozzarella instead of the shrink-wrapped brick, and a home pizza goes from fine to unforgettable — before you've learned a single trick. Here's the method, and exactly where to buy the three things that matter.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the doughDissolve the pinch of yeast in the water, then mix in the 00 flour and salt. Knead until smooth and elastic. Bulk rise about 2 hours, divide into 4 balls, and — this is the secret — cold-ferment them in the fridge 24 to 72 hours. Time does the flavor.
  2. Make the sauceCrush the San Marzano tomatoes by hand, add a pinch of salt, and stop. Neapolitan sauce is raw; it cooks on the pizza. Resist the urge to add anything else.
  3. Preheat hardPut a pizza steel or stone on a high rack and heat the oven as hot as it goes for at least 45 minutes. (A dedicated pizza oven at 800–900°F is the dream, but a steel under the broiler gets you shockingly close.)
  4. Stretch by handBring a dough ball to room temperature, then stretch it to about 12 inches with your hands, leaving a puffy rim. Never a rolling pin — it presses out the air that becomes the crust.
  5. Top lightA thin layer of sauce, torn mozzarella scattered (not blanketed), a drizzle of olive oil. Overloading is the most common home mistake — less is more.
  6. Bake hot and fast60–90 seconds in a pizza oven, or 5–8 minutes on a steel under the broiler, until the rim is puffed and leopard-spotted and the cheese is just melted.
  7. FinishStraight out of the oven, add fresh basil and a last drizzle of olive oil. Eat it immediately — pizza waits for no one.
