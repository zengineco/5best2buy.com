Kung Pao Chicken, and Where to Source It

Whole dried chiles bloomed in oil are the soul here, so buy fragrant ones that still smell smoky, not dusty and old. The one move: toast the dried chiles in oil just until they darken and perfume the pan, but pull the heat before they blacken and turn bitter.

Part Two
The Method
  1. VelvetToss the diced chicken with a little soy and cornstarch and let it sit 15 minutes so it seals and stays tender.
  2. Bloom chilesHeat oil, add the whole dried chiles and Sichuan pepper, and toast just until they darken and go fragrant. Pull the pan back if they threaten to blacken.
  3. SearAdd the chicken in one layer and let it catch color before tossing, then stir-fry until nearly cooked through.
  4. SauceAdd garlic, ginger, and the soy-vinegar-sugar sauce, tossing until glossy. Kill the heat and fold in the nuts and scallion so they stay crunchy.
