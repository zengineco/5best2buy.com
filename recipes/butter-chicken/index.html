Butter Chicken, and Where to Source It

The gravy lives or dies on a good garam masala and real cream, not on how much butter you dump in at the end. The one move: char the marinated chicken hard under the broiler before it goes into the sauce, so you get smoke and browning the simmer can never build on its own.

Part Two
The Method
  1. MarinateToss chicken with yogurt, half the garam masala, salt, and a spoon of ginger-garlic paste. Rest at least 30 minutes so the acid can work into the meat.
  2. CharBroil the chicken on a rack 6 inches from the element until the edges blacken, about 8 minutes. You want real char, not just cooked-through.
  3. Build gravyCook onion soft in butter, add ginger-garlic and remaining garam masala for a minute, then blended tomatoes. Simmer 20 minutes until it darkens and the raw tomato edge is gone.
  4. FinishAdd the charred chicken, then stir in cream and cold butter off the heat. Never let it boil after the cream goes in or the gravy will break.
