Tres Leches Cake, and Where to Source It

The cake has to be a lean, airy sponge with almost no fat so it can drink up the milk mixture without turning to mush. Poke it all over while still warm and pour the milk on slowly; a dense buttery cake will just repel the soak and sit in a puddle.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Whip the eggsBeat the yolks with 3/4 cup sugar until pale and thick, then whip the whites with the rest to stiff peaks.
  2. FoldFold the flour into the yolks, then fold in the whites in two additions to keep the batter airy.
  3. BakeSpread into a greased 9x13 pan and bake at 350F for 25-30 minutes until the top springs back.
  4. SoakWhisk the condensed milk, evaporated milk, and 1/2 cup cream; poke the warm cake all over and pour the mixture on slowly until absorbed.
  5. Chill and topRefrigerate at least 3 hours, then whip the remaining cream with 2 tbsp sugar, spread over the top, and finish with the fresh berries before serving.
