Pad Thai, and Where to Source It

The sauce is tamarind, fish sauce, and sugar in balance, so a clean fermented fish sauce keeps it savory instead of just salty. The one move: soak the noodles in warm water until pliable but still firm, then finish the cook in the wok so they don't turn to mush.

The Method
  1. SauceWhisk tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm or brown sugar into a balance that tastes equally sour, salty, and sweet before it hits the wok.
  2. SoakSoak rice noodles in warm water until bendy but still firm, about 10 minutes, then drain. They finish cooking in the sauce.
  3. EggStir-fry aromatics, push aside, and scramble the eggs in the cleared space before folding them into the noodles.
  4. FryAdd drained noodles and sauce, tossing constantly over high heat until the liquid is absorbed. Kill the heat, fold in sprouts and scallion, finish with peanuts and lime.

Straight Answers
Can you make Pad Thai in an air fryer? — Not really

Not really — loose batter spreads and needs a flat, hot surface. Keep this one on the stovetop or griddle.

Can you freeze Pad Thai? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Pad Thai ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it needs the chill time to set, so making it ahead is the whole point.

Is Pad Thai gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Pad Thai vegan? — No

No — it contains seafood, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
