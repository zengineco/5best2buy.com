Coxinha, and Where to Source It

The dough is cooked, not raw: a thick chicken-broth-and-flour paste that you knead until smooth. Cook the dough until it pulls from the pan like choux, or the coxinha will crack and leak in the fryer.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the fillingPoach and shred the chicken, saute with onion and garlic, then fold in cream cheese and cool.
  2. Cook the doughBring the reserved broth with milk to a boil, add flour all at once, and stir hard until it forms a smooth ball that leaves the pan.
  3. Shape teardropsFlatten a piece of dough, add filling, and pinch closed into the signature teardrop.
  4. Bread and fryCoat in egg and breadcrumbs, then fry at 350F for 3 to 4 minutes until deep golden.
