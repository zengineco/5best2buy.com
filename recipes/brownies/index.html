Fudgy Brownies, and Where to Source It

Melting a chopped chocolate bar into the butter, rather than leaning on cocoa alone, is what makes the crumb fudgy instead of cakey. Beat the eggs and sugar hard for a full 2 minutes to get that shiny, papery crackle on top.

Straight Answers
Can you make Fudgy Brownies in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Fudgy Brownies? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Fudgy Brownies ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Fudgy Brownies gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in a gluten-free flour blend (check the other labels too).

Is Fudgy Brownies vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.

Part Two
The Method
  1. MeltGently melt the chocolate and butter together over a double boiler or in short microwave bursts, then let cool slightly.
  2. WhipBeat eggs and sugar on high for 2 minutes until thick and pale; this builds the crackly top.
  3. FoldStir the cooled chocolate into the eggs, then fold in flour, cocoa, and 1/2 tsp salt just until combined.
  4. BakePour into a lined 8-inch square pan and bake at 350F for 28-32 minutes, until a toothpick comes out with a few moist crumbs, not wet batter.
