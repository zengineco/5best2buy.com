Lumpia, and Where to Source Them

Lumpiang Shanghai is mostly seasoned ground pork and a paper-thin wrapper, so a good pork blend with some fat and a fresh wrapper are what separate crisp from greasy. The one move: roll them tight and thin, no thicker than a finger, so the pork cooks through before the wrapper burns.

Straight Answers
Can you make Lumpia in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Lumpia? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Lumpia ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Lumpia gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Lumpia vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Mix fillingCombine pork, soy, minced carrot, onion, garlic, and scallion. Fry a pinch to taste and adjust salt before you roll the whole batch.
  2. Roll tightLay a wrapper as a diamond, spoon a thin log of filling near the bottom, fold sides in, and roll tight. Seal the edge with a dab of water.
  3. FryHeat oil to 350F and fry in batches 3 to 4 minutes until deep gold and the filling reads 160F. Crowding drops the oil and makes them soggy.
  4. Drain and dipRest on a rack, not paper towels, so steam escapes and they stay crisp. Serve hot with cane vinegar cut with minced garlic and a crack of pepper.
