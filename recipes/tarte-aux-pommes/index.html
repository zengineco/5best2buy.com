Tarte aux Pommes, and Where to Source It

Spread a layer of thick apple compote under the sliced apples so the tart stays moist without going wet. A brush of warmed apricot jam the moment it leaves the oven seals the fruit and gives it the glossy sheen.

The Method
  1. Make compoteCook some of the apples with a little water and sugar to a thick, smooth paste, then cool.
  2. Fill the baseSpread the compote over the pastry base.
  3. Fan the slicesLay the thin apple slices in tight overlapping rings over the compote.
  4. Bake and glazeBrush with butter, bake at 375F until golden, then brush warm apricot jam over the hot tart.

Straight Answers
Can you make Tarte aux Pommes in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Tarte aux Pommes? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Tarte aux Pommes ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Tarte aux Pommes gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in gluten-free versions of the wheat items (check the other labels too).

Is Tarte aux Pommes vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy.
