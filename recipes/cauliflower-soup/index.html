Cauliflower Soup, and Where to Source It

Roast the cauliflower until the tips go deep brown before it goes in the pot; that char is the whole flavor and boiling it raw gives you bland cream. Blended long enough, it turns as silky as any cream soup with barely any dairy.

Straight Answers
Can you make Cauliflower Soup in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Cauliflower Soup? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Cauliflower Soup ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. RoastToss cauliflower florets and garlic cloves in olive oil and roast at 450F for 25 minutes until browned at the tips.
  2. Sweat the baseSoften the diced onion in butter over medium heat until translucent, 6 minutes.
  3. SimmerAdd the roasted cauliflower and garlic with the broth and simmer 10 minutes.
  4. Blend and finishPuree until completely smooth, stir in the parmesan and cream off the heat, and season with salt and pepper.
