Feijoada, and Where to Source It

Feijoada is about layering cuts of pork and smoke into a pot of black beans until everything melts together. The key is time on low heat: the beans must break down enough to thicken their own liquid into that glossy black gravy, so don't rush it or add cornstarch.

Can you make Feijoada in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Render and sweatCrisp the bacon, then sweat onion and garlic in the fat until soft.
  2. Build the potAdd soaked beans, pork shoulder, bay, and water to cover by two inches; bring to a boil then drop to a low simmer.
  3. Long simmerCook covered 2.5 to 3 hours, adding sausage in the last hour, until beans break down and broth turns thick and black.
  4. Season and serveShred the pork back in, salt to taste, and serve over rice with orange slices and greens.
