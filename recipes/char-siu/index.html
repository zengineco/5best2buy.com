Char Siu (Chinese BBQ Pork), and Where to Source It

Well-raised pork shoulder has the fat marbling that keeps char siu moist through a hot roast, and real honey does the caramel work that corn syrup fakes. The key technique is basting in layers: three coats of glaze under the broiler build the sticky, blistered edge.

Part Two
The Method
  1. MarinateWhisk soy, half the honey, five-spice, hoisin, garlic, ginger and wine; coat the pork strips and refrigerate at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.
  2. RoastRoast at 425F on a rack over water, turning once, about 25 minutes until the edges darken.
  3. Glaze in coatsThin the remaining honey with a spoon of marinade and brush on; broil 2 to 3 minutes, then repeat twice for a blistered lacquer.
  4. Rest and sliceRest 10 minutes so the juices settle, then slice across the grain into thin planks. Serve over rice or in noodle soup.
