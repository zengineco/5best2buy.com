Marinara Sauce, and Where to Source It

Marinara is proof that two ingredients bought well beat a jar of anything: a great canned tomato and good olive oil. No sugar, no twenty ingredients — just a clove of garlic and a little patience. Here’s how, and where to buy the two that matter.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Warm the garlicGently warm the olive oil with smashed garlic until fragrant and pale gold — not browned.
  2. Add the tomatoesCrush the tomatoes in by hand, add a pinch of salt, and let it come to a low simmer.
  3. Simmer downSimmer gently 30–40 minutes, stirring now and then, until it thickens and the oil turns orange at the edges — the sign it’s done.
  4. FinishTear in basil, add a raw thread of olive oil, and toss with pasta, saving a little pasta water to loosen.
