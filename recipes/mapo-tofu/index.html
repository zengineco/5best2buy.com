Mapo Tofu, and Where to Source It

This dish rides on fermented chili bean paste and chili crisp, so a good, funky, deeply red one is the difference between real mapo and chili gravy. The one move: slide the soft tofu in gently and push it around with the back of a spoon, never a spatula, so the cubes stay whole.

The Method
  1. Warm tofuSlip the cubed tofu into gently salted hot water while you cook; it firms slightly and stays intact when it joins the sauce.
  2. Fry porkBrown the ground pork hard in oil until crispy, then add garlic, ginger, and chili crisp and fry until the oil runs red and fragrant.
  3. Build sauceAdd a little broth and soy, bring to a simmer, then slide in the drained tofu and nudge it gently to coat without breaking the cubes.
  4. FinishThicken with a cornstarch slurry in two additions until it clings. Off the heat, dust with toasted ground Sichuan pepper and top with scallion.

Straight Answers
Can you make Mapo Tofu in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Mapo Tofu? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Mapo Tofu ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Is Mapo Tofu gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Mapo Tofu vegan? — No

No — it contains meat, so it isn’t vegetarian or vegan.
