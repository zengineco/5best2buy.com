Spanakopita, and Where to Source It

Spanakopita rewards good feta and a filling that is not wet, or the bottom phyllo goes soggy. The one move that matters: wring the cooked spinach bone-dry in a towel before it meets the cheese. Brush every phyllo sheet with butter for the shatter you want.

The Method
  1. Cook the spinachWilt the spinach, cool it, then wring it dry in a clean towel until no water drips.
  2. Mix the fillingCombine dry spinach, crumbled feta, dill, scallions, and beaten eggs; taste before salting since feta is salty.
  3. Layer the baseBrush a phyllo sheet with butter, lay it in the pan, and repeat for half the stack, letting edges overhang.
  4. Fill and topSpread the filling, fold the overhang, then layer the rest of the buttered phyllo on top and tuck the edges.
  5. Score and bakeScore the top into pieces and bake at 375F for 45 minutes until deep golden and crisp. Rest 15 minutes before cutting.

Straight Answers
Can you make Spanakopita in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Spanakopita? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Spanakopita ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Spanakopita gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in gluten-free versions of the wheat items (check the other labels too).

Is Spanakopita vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.
