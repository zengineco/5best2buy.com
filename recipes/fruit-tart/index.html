Fruit Tart, and Where to Source It

A fruit tart is three things done right: a crisp blind-baked shell, a thick vanilla pastry cream, and fruit arranged tight. Cook the pastry cream until it bubbles hard for a full minute so the starch loses its raw taste and the filling holds a clean slice.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the crustCut the butter into the flour and sugar, bind with a yolk, press into the tart pan, and chill 30 min.
  2. Blind-bakeLine with parchment and weights, bake at 375F for 18 min, pull the weights, and bake 8 min more until dry and golden.
  3. Cook the creamWhisk sugar, cornstarch, and yolks, temper in hot milk, and cook until it bubbles hard for one full minute and turns thick.
  4. Chill the creamOff the heat stir in the vanilla, press plastic to the surface, and chill until cold and set.
  5. Fill and topSpread the cream in the cooled shell, arrange the fruit tight on top, and brush warmed jam over the fruit to glaze.
