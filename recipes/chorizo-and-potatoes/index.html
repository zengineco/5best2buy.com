Chorizo and Potatoes, and Where to Source It

This one-pan dish is built on the fat the chorizo gives up, so use a real cured Spanish chorizo that renders red and smoky. The technique that matters is browning the potatoes hard in that fat, undisturbed, so they crisp instead of steam. A splash of vinegar at the end cuts the richness.

Can you make Chorizo and Potatoes in an air fryer? — Sometimes

Sometimes — if the pieces are small and cooked dry, an air fryer can handle it in a single layer; anything saucy or liquid needs a pan. When in doubt, the stovetop or oven is the safe call.

Can you freeze Chorizo and Potatoes? — Yes

Yes — cool it, portion into airtight containers, and freeze; thaw in the fridge and reheat.

Can you make Chorizo and Potatoes ahead of time? — Partly

The components hold — prep the parts ahead and finish it close to serving for the best texture.

Is Chorizo and Potatoes gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Chorizo and Potatoes vegan? — No

Part Two
The Method
  1. CutCut potatoes into 3/4-inch chunks and slice the chorizo into coins.
  2. Render the chorizoCook the chorizo in a little oil over medium until it renders its red fat and crisps, then set aside.
  3. Brown the potatoesCook the potatoes in the chorizo fat over medium-high, undisturbed, until a deep crust forms, about 12 minutes.
  4. Add aromaticsStir in onion, garlic, and paprika and cook 8 minutes more until the potatoes are tender through.
  5. FinishReturn the chorizo, add the vinegar, and toss; taste for salt and serve hot.
