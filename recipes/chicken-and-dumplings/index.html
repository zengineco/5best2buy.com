A real chicken and a gelatin-rich broth give the pot the body that instant stock never will. The one technique: once the dumplings go in, keep the lid on and the pot at a gentle simmer for 15 minutes without peeking, so they steam through instead of falling apart.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Simmer the chickenCover the chicken with the broth plus water and the aromatics; simmer 45 minutes until the meat pulls easily.
  2. Pull the meatLift out the chicken, pull the meat, and return it to the strained, seasoned broth kept at a low simmer.
  3. Mix the dumplingsCut the cold butter into the flour with 2 tsp baking powder and 1 tsp salt, then stir in the milk to a shaggy dough.
  4. Drop and steamDrop tablespoons of dough onto the simmering broth, cover tight, and cook 15 minutes without lifting the lid.
  5. ServeCheck a dumpling is cooked through, then ladle into bowls with plenty of broth and cracked pepper.
