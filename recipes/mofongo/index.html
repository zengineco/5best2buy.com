Mofongo, and Where to Source It

Mofongo uses green, starchy plantains, not sweet ripe ones; they must be fried first, then mashed while hot. Pound the fried plantains with garlic and pork cracklings in a pilón while still warm so it binds into a dome, not a paste.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Fry the plantainsFry 1-inch chunks of green plantain in oil at 325F for 5 to 6 minutes until tender but not browned.
  2. Crisp the porkFry the pork belly or bacon until crackling and crisp.
  3. Mash in the pilonPound garlic with oil and salt, then add hot plantains and pork and mash into a coarse, cohesive mix.
  4. Shape the domePack the mash into a bowl and turn out into a dome; serve with broth or a garlic-tomato sauce.
