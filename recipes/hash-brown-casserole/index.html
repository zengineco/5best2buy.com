Hash Brown Casserole, and Where to Source It

Thaw the shredded potatoes fully and press out the water before mixing. The ice crystals melt as it bakes, and if you skip this the casserole weeps into a watery, separated bake instead of a cohesive one.

Straight Answers
Can you make Hash Brown Casserole in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Hash Brown Casserole? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Hash Brown Casserole ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Dry the potatoesPress the thawed shreds in towels to squeeze out the water.
  2. MixStir the butter, sour cream, onion, and most of the cheese through the potatoes.
  3. Pack the dishSpread it into a buttered baking dish without pressing hard.
  4. Top and bakeScatter the rest of the cheese and bake at 350F about 45 min until bubbling.
