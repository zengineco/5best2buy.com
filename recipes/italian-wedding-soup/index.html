Italian Wedding Soup, and Where to Source It

Keep the meatballs small and drop them straight into the simmering broth so they poach tender and season the liquid. Cook the pasta separately or it drinks up the broth and swells overnight into a solid mass.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Mix the meatballsCombine the pork with egg, breadcrumbs, parmesan, minced garlic, and salt, then roll into 3/4-inch balls.
  2. Cook the pastaBoil the acini di pepe separately until just tender, drain, and hold it aside.
  3. Poach the meatballsBring the broth to a gentle simmer and drop the meatballs in; poach 10 minutes until cooked through.
  4. FinishStir in the chopped escarole and cook 3 minutes until wilted, then add the pasta and serve with parmesan.
