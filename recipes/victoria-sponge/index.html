Victoria Sponge, and Where to Source It

A Victoria sponge is a plain cake, so weigh the eggs and match butter, sugar, and flour to that weight for a reliable crumb. Cream the butter and sugar until pale and fluffy, which is the only air the cake gets before the flour goes in.

Part Two
The Method
  1. CreamBeat butter and sugar 4 to 5 minutes until pale and fluffy, scraping down the bowl.
  2. Add eggsBeat in eggs one at a time, adding a spoon of flour with the last to keep the batter from splitting.
  3. Fold and bakeFold in the remaining flour, divide between two tins, and bake at 350F for 25 minutes until a skewer comes out clean.
  4. FillCool completely, spread jam on one layer and whipped cream over it, and sandwich the second on top.
