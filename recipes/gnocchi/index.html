Potato Gnocchi, and Where to Source It

Gnocchi go gummy the second you add too much flour, so a dry, starchy baked potato that needs less flour is the whole game. The rule: rice the potatoes while hot, add just enough flour to barely bind, and stop kneading the instant it comes together.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Bake and riceBake the potatoes at 400F until soft, about 50 min, then rice them hot straight onto the counter and let the steam escape.
  2. Make the doughScatter flour, the yolk, and parmesan over the potato and fold gently until it just comes together; do not knead like bread.
  3. ShapeRoll ropes as thick as a thumb, cut into pillows, and press each over a fork tines to ridge them so sauce grabs on.
  4. Boil brieflyDrop into salted boiling water and pull them out 30 seconds after they float, then finish in brown butter and sage.
