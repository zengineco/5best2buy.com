Orange Chicken, and Where to Source It

Fresh oranges give juice and zest oil that bottled concentrate can't touch, and a genuine brewed soy balances the sweetness so the sauce reads savory. The technique to nail: reduce the sauce to a syrup before the chicken goes in, then toss off heat so it clings without steaming the crust soft.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Prep chickenCut thighs into chunks, toss with 1 tbsp soy and 1 tsp cornstarch, and rest 15 minutes.
  2. Zest and juiceZest both oranges into the sauce, then juice them; you want about half a cup of juice.
  3. Coat and fryDredge in flour-cornstarch, then fry at 360F until golden and crisp. Drain on a rack.
  4. ReduceSimmer orange juice, zest, soy, sugar, vinegar, ginger and garlic until it coats a spoon, then slack a cornstarch slurry in to gloss it.
  5. Toss off heatKill the heat, add the chicken, and toss quickly so it lacquers evenly. Finish with scallion and serve over rice.
