Biscotti, and Where to Source It

Biscotti are meant to be hard and dry so they hold up to coffee, and the twice-baking is what gets them there. The second bake at low heat drives off moisture, turning a soft log into a crisp cookie.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the doughBeat eggs and sugar, add vanilla, anise and zest, then work in the flour to a stiff, sticky dough.
  2. Add almondsFold in the toasted whole almonds until evenly spread.
  3. First bakeShape into two flat logs, bake at 350F for 25-30 min until set and lightly golden, then cool 10 min.
  4. SliceCut the warm logs on a diagonal into 1/2-inch slices with a serrated knife.
  5. Second bakeLay slices cut-side down and bake at 300F for 15-20 min, flipping once, until dry and crisp; they firm more as they cool.
