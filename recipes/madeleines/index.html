Madeleines, and Where to Source It

These little sponge cakes reward brown butter and a rested, chilled batter. The cold batter hitting a hot pan is what gives madeleines their trademark bump; skip the rest and they bake flat.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Brown the butterMelt butter over medium until it foams, smells nutty, and the solids turn amber; cool slightly.
  2. Whip eggs and sugarBeat eggs, sugar and lemon zest 3-4 min until pale, thick and tripled in volume.
  3. FoldGently fold in the flour, then the brown butter, keeping the batter airy.
  4. Chill the batterCover and refrigerate at least 1 hour; the cold shock is what forms the hump.
  5. BakeSpoon into a buttered shell pan and bake at 375F for 10-12 min until the humps rise and the edges brown; eat the same day.
