Gazpacho, and Where to Source It

Gazpacho is raw, so it lives or dies on August tomatoes and good olive oil; out of season it just tastes thin. The one technique that matters is emulsifying the oil in slowly at the end so the soup turns silky and orange instead of watery. Chill it hard before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Soak the breadTear the bread and soak it in a splash of water so it blends smooth.
  2. Blend the baseBlend tomatoes, cucumber, pepper, garlic, soaked bread, and vinegar until fully smooth, about 2 minutes.
  3. EmulsifyWith the blender running, pour in the olive oil in a thin stream until the soup turns pale orange and creamy.
  4. StrainPass through a sieve for a silky texture, pressing the solids to get all the liquid.
  5. ChillRefrigerate at least 2 hours until cold; taste and adjust salt and vinegar before serving.
