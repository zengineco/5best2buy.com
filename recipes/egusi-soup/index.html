Egusi Soup, and Where to Source It

Ground egusi seeds are the whole point: they cook into soft curds that give the soup its grainy body, so how you add them matters. Frying the ground seeds into the hot oil base, then letting them set into lumps, is the traditional method. Stir them in as a slurry and you get a smooth soup instead of the textured one it should be.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Cook the meatSimmer beef or goat with onion and salt 40 min until tender; reserve the broth.
  2. Fry the baseBloom ground chiles and crayfish in hot oil 3 min, then add reserved broth to loosen.
  3. Set the egusiStir the ground seeds into a paste with a little water, drop it in spoonfuls, and simmer 15 min without stirring so it sets into lumps.
  4. FinishFold in the greens and cooked meat, cook 5 min more, and adjust salt and heat.
