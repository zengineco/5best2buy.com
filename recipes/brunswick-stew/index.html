Brunswick Stew, and Where to Source It

This is a smoky, tomato-thick stew that rewards real pulled pork and a gelatin-rich broth over anything from a can of soup. The one technique: cook it low and long enough that the potatoes break down and thicken the pot on their own, so you never need flour.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Poach the chickenSimmer chicken thighs in the broth 30 minutes to 175F, then pull the meat and return the bones to the pot for 15 more minutes.
  2. Build the potStrain out the bones, add tomatoes, diced potato, onion, and lima beans, and bring to a low simmer.
  3. Simmer lowAdd the pulled pork and shredded chicken and cook uncovered 45-60 minutes, stirring often, until the potatoes fall apart and the stew thickens.
  4. Add corn and seasonStir in corn and a splash of cider vinegar and a spoon of hot sauce; cook 10 more minutes.
  5. FinishSeason hard with salt and black pepper; it should be thick enough to stand a spoon in.
