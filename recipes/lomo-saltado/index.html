Lomo Saltado, and Where to Source It

Lomo saltado is a wok stir fry with a Peruvian accent, so a good sirloin seared hard and a naturally brewed soy give it the smoky depth called sabor. The one move: cook in small batches over the highest heat you have, because a crowded pan steams the beef gray instead of searing it.

Straight Answers
Can you make Lomo Saltado in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Can you freeze Lomo Saltado? — Not ideal

You can, but the crisp coating softens. Freeze the cooked pieces, then re-crisp them in a hot oven or air fryer — never the microwave.

Can you make Lomo Saltado ahead of time? — Prep ahead

Best fried to order — the crunch fades if it sits. Do the breading or shaping ahead and cook it fresh when you serve.

Is Lomo Saltado gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in tamari instead of soy sauce (check the other labels too).

Is Lomo Saltado vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Fry the potatoesFry thick-cut potatoes at 350F until golden and crisp, then hold warm. Homemade fries beat frozen here.
  2. Sear the beefGet the wok smoking hot, sear the beef in two batches for 60 seconds each so it browns instead of steaming. Set aside.
  3. Stir-fry aromaticsAdd onion, tomato, and aji amarillo; toss 2 minutes so they soften but keep their edges.
  4. Deglaze and combineReturn the beef, splash in vinegar and soy, and scrape the pan. Fold in the fries at the very end and serve over rice.
