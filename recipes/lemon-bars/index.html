Lemon Bars, and Where to Source It

Fresh-squeezed lemon juice is the whole reason to make these; bottled juice tastes flat and dull. Par-bake the crust so the bottom stays crisp, and pull the bars while the filling still has a slight wobble so the curd sets soft, not rubbery.

Part Two
The Method
  1. CrustMix flour, 1/2 cup sugar, and butter into a crumbly dough, press into a lined 9-inch pan, and bake at 350F for 18-20 minutes until pale gold.
  2. CurdWhisk eggs, 1 cup sugar, 3 tbsp flour, lemon juice, and zest until smooth.
  3. BakePour the curd over the warm crust and bake at 350F for 20-22 minutes, until the center barely jiggles when nudged.
  4. FinishCool completely, then chill before cutting; dust with powdered sugar just before serving.
