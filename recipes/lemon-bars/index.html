Lemon Bars, and Where to Source It

Fresh-squeezed lemon juice is the whole reason to make these; bottled juice tastes flat and dull. Par-bake the crust so the bottom stays crisp, and pull the bars while the filling still has a slight wobble so the curd sets soft, not rubbery.

The Method
  1. CrustMix flour, 1/2 cup sugar, and butter into a crumbly dough, press into a lined 9-inch pan, and bake at 350F for 18-20 minutes until pale gold.
  2. CurdWhisk eggs, 1 cup sugar, 3 tbsp flour, lemon juice, and zest until smooth.
  3. BakePour the curd over the warm crust and bake at 350F for 20-22 minutes, until the center barely jiggles when nudged.
  4. FinishCool completely, then chill before cutting; dust with powdered sugar just before serving.

Straight Answers
Can you make Lemon Bars in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — bake it in a small pan or dish that fits the basket, at about 25°F below the recipe temperature. Air fryers run hot and fast, so check it early; no flipping needed.

Can you freeze Lemon Bars? — Yes

Yes — freeze it well-wrapped, then reheat or toast straight from frozen. Frost or glaze after thawing.

Can you make Lemon Bars ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — bake it a day or two ahead; it keeps well-wrapped at room temperature and freezes cleanly. Add any frosting or glaze close to serving.

Is Lemon Bars gluten-free? — Not as written

Not as written — it contains gluten. Make it gluten-free: swap in a gluten-free flour blend (check the other labels too).

Is Lemon Bars vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy and egg. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy, and a flax or aquafaba egg for the egg.
