Calzone, and Where to Source It

A calzone is a sealed pocket, so the ricotta has to be well-drained or the crust steams into a soggy mess. Bake it hot and cut a vent, and the filling sets instead of bursting the seam.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make the doughKnead flour, water, yeast, and salt into a smooth dough and let it rise until doubled, about 90 minutes. Divide in two.
  2. Drain the ricottaLet the ricotta drain in cheesecloth, then mix with mozzarella, garlic, basil, and a little Parmesan. It should mound, not run.
  3. Fill and sealStretch each dough to a 10-inch round, mound filling on half, fold over, and crimp the edge tight. Cut a small vent on top.
  4. Bake hotBake at 500F on a hot stone or steel 12 to 15 minutes until deep gold and firm. Rest 5 minutes, serve with warm sauce for dipping.
