Thanksgiving Dinner, and Where to Source It

Thanksgiving is the one meal a year where people actually cook from scratch — and the one meal most worth sourcing well, because every dish is simple enough that the ingredients carry it. A heritage turkey needs almost nothing done to it. Real cranberries make a sauce that buries the can. Fresh pecans make the pie. This is the whole dinner, dish by dish, with every ingredient linked to a small American maker who does it right. Build your feast from real food.

The Battle Plan
So Nothing Burns
  1. 3+ weeks outOrder the heritage turkey — the single most time-sensitive thing on the table. The best farms sell out early.
  2. 1 week outOrder everything shelf-stable and frozen: pecans, flour, maple, spices, sugar, salt, cider, and frozen cranberries if fresh aren't in season yet.
  3. 2–3 days outMake the cranberry sauce and the pie doughs — both keep beautifully and clear your oven and your head for the big day.
  4. Thanksgiving morningRoast the bird low and even; build the gravy from the drippings and bone broth; bake the sides around the turkey's rest. Rest the bird a full 30 minutes before carving — this is non-negotiable.
  5. ServeEverything to the table at once, cider poured, board already half-demolished. You sourced it right; it'll taste like it.
