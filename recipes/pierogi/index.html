Pierogi, and Where to Source It

A pierogi is dough plus filling, so both need to be right: a soft, rollable dough and a filling seasoned harder than you think it needs. Boil them just until they float, then finish in butter with onions for the browned edges that make them.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Make doughKnead flour, egg, warm water, and butter into a soft dough; rest covered 30 minutes.
  2. FillRoll thin, cut rounds, and fill with the mashed potato-cheese-onion mix; seal edges tight with no air pockets.
  3. BoilBoil in batches; they are done about a minute after they float, roughly 3 minutes total.
  4. FryFinish the boiled pierogi in butter with onions until the edges brown and crisp.
