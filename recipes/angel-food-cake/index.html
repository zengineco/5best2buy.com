Angel Food Cake, and Where to Source It

There is no butter or leavening here, so the whole rise comes from air beaten into a dozen egg whites. Whip them to stiff peaks and fold in the flour by hand, then bake in an ungreased tube pan and cool it upside down so it does not collapse.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Whip the whitesBeat the room-temperature whites with cream of tartar to soft peaks, then add 1 cup sugar a spoon at a time and whip to stiff glossy peaks.
  2. Fold the flourSift the flour with the remaining sugar and fold it into the whites in three additions, cutting through gently so you keep the air.
  3. Fill the panSpoon into an ungreased 10-inch tube pan and run a knife through the batter to pop large air pockets.
  4. BakeBake at 350F for 38-42 min until the top springs back and a skewer comes out clean.
  5. Cool invertedInvert the pan onto its feet or a bottle and cool completely upside down before releasing, or the cake sinks.
