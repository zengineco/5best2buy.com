Samosas, and Where to Source Them

A samosa is judged on its shell, and a stiff dough fried low and slow is what makes it shatter-crisp and blistered instead of soft and greasy. The one move: fry them at a moderate 325F, not screaming hot, so the shell cooks through and crisps evenly rather than browning before the inside sets.

Part Two
The Method
  1. DoughRub ghee into flour and salt, then bind with just enough water for a stiff dough. Rest it 30 minutes covered so it rolls without cracking.
  2. FillingToast cumin, add aromatics and spices, then fold in mashed potato and peas. Cool it fully; hot filling steams and softens the shell.
  3. ShapeRoll dough thin, cut into half-circles, form cones, fill, and seal the top edge with a wet finger. A tight seal keeps oil out.
  4. FryFry at 325F, moderate not hot, for 8-10 minutes until deep golden and blistered. Low and slow is what makes the shell shatter.
