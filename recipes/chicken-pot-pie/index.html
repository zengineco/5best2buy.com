Straight Answers
Can you make Chicken Pot Pie in an air fryer? — Yes, with an insert

Not on its own — an air fryer can’t hold a pool of liquid. You can simmer it in an oven-safe dish or a silicone insert made to fit the basket, but the broth won’t reduce or brown the way it does on the stove, and that insert comes out scorching, so use oven mitts. For real depth, a pot on the stove wins.

Can you freeze Chicken Pot Pie? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Chicken Pot Pie ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it holds well and often tastes better the next day. Make it ahead and reheat gently before serving.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Poach the chickenSimmer thighs in the broth 18-20 minutes to 165F internal, then pull the meat and reserve the broth.
  2. Build the rouxMelt 4 tbsp butter, whisk in 4 tbsp flour, and cook 2 full minutes over medium until it smells nutty and turns pale gold.
  3. Make the gravyWhisk in the reserved broth and milk off the heat, then simmer 5 minutes until it coats a spoon. Fold in chicken and vegetables.
  4. Fill and topLine a pie dish with rolled dough, add the cooled filling, lay the top crust, crimp, and cut vents.
  5. BakeBake at 400F for 40-45 minutes until the crust is deep golden and the filling bubbles through the vents. Rest 15 minutes before slicing.
