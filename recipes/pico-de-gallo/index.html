Pico de Gallo, and Where to Source It

Everything comes down to draining the diced tomato so the salsa is chunky, not watery, and salting it early to pull that liquid out. Use ripe tomatoes and fresh lime, since there's nowhere for a bad ingredient to hide. Let it sit 15 minutes so the raw onion mellows and the flavors come together.

Straight Answers
Can you freeze Pico de Gallo? — Yes

Yes — it freezes well. Cool it fully, portion into airtight containers, and it keeps a few months; thaw in the fridge and reheat gently.

Can you make Pico de Gallo ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it keeps in the fridge for several days; stir or shake it before serving.

Is Pico de Gallo gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Pico de Gallo vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.

Part Two
The Method
  1. DiceSeed and dice the tomatoes, then mince the onion, jalapeño, and cilantro fine.
  2. Salt and drainToss diced tomato with salt in a colander and let it drain 5 minutes to shed water.
  3. CombineMix everything in a bowl with fresh lime juice and taste for salt and acid.
  4. RestLet it sit 15 minutes so the onion softens and the flavors marry before serving.
