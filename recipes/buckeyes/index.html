Buckeyes, and Where to Source It

A buckeye is peanut butter and chocolate, full stop, so both have to be good. The trick is chilling the peanut butter balls until firm before dipping, and leaving the toothpick hole on top uncovered so it looks like the nut it is named for.

Straight Answers
Can you make Buckeyes in an air fryer? — Not needed

No cooking needed here — this one comes together without heat, so the air fryer sits it out.

Can you freeze Buckeyes? — Yes

Yes — cool it, portion into airtight containers, and freeze; thaw in the fridge and reheat.

Can you make Buckeyes ahead of time? — Yes

Yes — it needs the chill time to set, so making it ahead is the whole point.

Is Buckeyes gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Buckeyes vegan? — Vegetarian, not vegan

It’s vegetarian but not vegan as written — it has dairy. Make it vegan: use plant milk, oil, or a vegan cheese for the dairy.

Part Two
The Method
  1. MixBeat peanut butter, butter, vanilla, and a pinch of salt, then work in the powdered sugar until stiff.
  2. RollRoll into 1-inch balls and set on a lined pan.
  3. ChillFreeze the balls 30 minutes until firm; soft centers fall apart in the chocolate.
  4. DipMelt the chocolate gently, then dip each ball on a toothpick, leaving a bare circle on top.
  5. SetSmooth over the toothpick hole and chill until the chocolate is firm, about 20 minutes.
