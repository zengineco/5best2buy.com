Menudo, and Where to Source It

Menudo takes time and cannot be rushed: the tripe needs hours of gentle simmering to go from rubbery to tender, so plan for a long cook. Clean the tripe well and build the broth on rehydrated guajillo and ancho chiles for that deep red color and warmth.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Clean and boilScrub the tripe, cut into 1-inch pieces, and simmer in fresh water with onion for 3 hours.
  2. Chile brothToast and soak the chiles, blend smooth, and strain into the pot.
  3. SimmerAdd the hominy and spices and simmer another 1.5 to 2 hours until the tripe is fully tender.
  4. GarnishServe with diced onion, oregano, lime, and warm tortillas.
