Mango Sticky Rice, and Where to Source It

The rice has to be true glutinous (sticky) rice, soaked at least 30 minutes and steamed, not boiled — regular rice will never get that chewy pull. The warm rice is dressed in a salted coconut cream so the dish reads sweet-salty, not just sweet.

The Method
  1. Soak the riceSoak glutinous rice at least 30 minutes, ideally a few hours.
  2. SteamSteam the drained rice over simmering water 20 to 25 minutes until translucent and tender.
  3. Make the creamWarm coconut milk with sugar and a pinch of salt until dissolved; do not boil.
  4. Dress and serveFold most of the cream into the hot rice and rest 15 minutes; serve with sliced mango, extra cream, and sesame.

Straight Answers
Can you make Mango Sticky Rice in an air fryer? — Sometimes

Sometimes — if the pieces are small and cooked dry, an air fryer can handle it in a single layer; anything saucy or liquid needs a pan. When in doubt, the stovetop or oven is the safe call.

Can you freeze Mango Sticky Rice? — Yes

Yes — cool it, portion into airtight containers, and freeze; thaw in the fridge and reheat.

Can you make Mango Sticky Rice ahead of time? — Partly

The components hold — prep the parts ahead and finish it close to serving for the best texture.

Is Mango Sticky Rice gluten-free? — Yes

Yes — nothing in this recipe as written contains gluten. As always, glance at the labels on any packaged items (broth, sauces, chocolate), since brands vary.

Is Mango Sticky Rice vegan? — Yes

Yes — no meat, seafood, dairy, egg, or honey in it as written.
