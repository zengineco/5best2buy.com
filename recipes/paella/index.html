Paella, and Where to Source It

Paella rewards the right rice and a restrained hand: bomba drinks up three times its volume of stock without turning to mush. The one move that matters is the socarrat, the toasted crust on the bottom, which only forms if you stop stirring once the rice goes in. Spread the rice thin, one grain deep, and let the pan do the work.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Build the sofritoBrown the chorizo in olive oil, then cook onion, pepper, tomato, and paprika down to a dark jammy paste, about 15 minutes.
  2. Bloom the saffronCrush the saffron threads and steep them in 4 cups of warm stock so the color pulls fully.
  3. Add the riceStir the rice into the sofrito for 1 minute, then pour in the saffron stock and spread the rice flat and even.
  4. Do not stirSimmer 18 minutes without stirring so the bottom can toast; lay the shrimp on top for the last 6 minutes.
  5. Make the socarratWhen the liquid is gone, raise the heat 2 minutes until you hear crackling and smell toasted rice; rest 5 minutes off heat before serving.
