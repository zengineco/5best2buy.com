Mujadara, and Where to Source It

Mujadara is plain on paper and unforgettable in practice, and the whole thing rides on the onions: fry them low and slow until genuinely dark brown and crisp, not merely golden, so their bitter-sweet edge flavors the whole pot. Cook the lentils until just tender before the rice goes in, or one component ends up mush while the other stays hard. Some of the onion oil goes back into the rice, which is the quiet trick.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Fry the onionsSlice onions thin and fry in the oil over medium-low, stirring, 20-25 minutes until dark brown and crisp; drain on paper.
  2. Cook the lentilsSimmer lentils in salted water until just tender, about 20 minutes, then drain.
  3. Build the potBloom cumin in the reserved onion oil, add rinsed rice and lentils, cover with water by an inch, and season well.
  4. SteamSimmer covered on low until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, about 18 minutes, then rest off heat 10 minutes.
  5. Crown itFluff with a fork and pile the crisp fried onions on top.
