Oatmeal Cookies, and Where to Source It

Old-fashioned rolled oats, not quick oats, give the cookies their chew and texture; quick oats dissolve into a cakey crumb. Chilling the dough before baking stops the cookies from spreading thin and keeps the centers thick and chewy.

Part Two
The Method
  1. CreamBeat the butter with the brown sugar and 1/2 cup white sugar until fluffy, 3 minutes, then beat in the eggs and 1 tsp vanilla.
  2. MixWhisk the flour, cinnamon, 1 tsp baking soda, and 1/2 tsp salt, mix into the butter, then stir in the oats and raisins.
  3. ChillRefrigerate the dough 30 minutes so the cookies bake up thick instead of spreading thin.
  4. BakeScoop onto lined sheets and bake at 350F for 10-12 minutes until the edges are golden but the centers still look soft.
  5. CoolRest on the sheet 5 minutes to set the chewy centers before transferring to a rack.
