Bouillabaisse, and Where to Source It

Bouillabaisse is really two dishes: a rich fish broth you build from the bones and heads, and the firm fish poached in it at the end. The broth is everything, so it needs saffron, fennel, and enough fish frames to gel; the fillets go in only for the last few minutes so they do not overcook.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Build the brothSweat fennel, onion, and leek, add tomatoes, fish frames, saffron, and water, and simmer 30 minutes.
  2. StrainPress the broth through a strainer to extract everything, then return it to the pot.
  3. Poach the fishBring the broth to a gentle simmer and add fish by thickness, firmest first, cooking just until opaque, about 6 minutes.
  4. Add shellfishDrop mussels and shrimp in for the final 3 minutes until the shells open.
  5. Serve with rouilleLadle over toasted bread spread with garlicky saffron rouille.
