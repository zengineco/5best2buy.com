Chicken Caesar Wrap, and Where to Source It

A Caesar wrap is only as good as its dressing and its parmesan, so grate real Parmigiano and build a dressing with anchovy and garlic instead of a bottled pour. The one technique: dress the romaine right before you roll so it stays crisp and the tortilla does not turn to paste.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Grill the chickenSeason the breasts and grill or sear over medium-high 5-6 minutes a side to 165F, then rest and slice.
  2. Make the dressingWhisk the egg yolk, mashed anchovy, grated garlic, and lemon juice, then stream in olive oil to a loose mayo and fold in half the parmesan.
  3. Dress the romaineToss chopped romaine with the dressing just until coated so it stays crisp.
  4. Warm and fillWarm each tortilla 20 seconds, lay it flat, and pile chicken, romaine, and the rest of the parmesan down the center.
  5. Roll tightFold in the sides, roll away from you into a tight cylinder, and cut in half on the bias.
