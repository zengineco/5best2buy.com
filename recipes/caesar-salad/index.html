Caesar Salad, and Where to Source It

Real anchovies and aged parmesan are what make a Caesar taste like a Caesar, not just garlic mayo. The one technique: emulsify the dressing slowly so the oil and egg build into something creamy that clings to every leaf.

Can you make Caesar Salad in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Toast croutonsToss torn bread with olive oil and salt, then bake at 375F until golden and crunchy, about 12 minutes. Homemade croutons stay crisp instead of going stale-soft.
  2. Mash the baseMash anchovies and garlic to a paste with a pinch of salt. The paste is what spreads that savory hit through the whole bowl.
  3. EmulsifyWhisk in egg yolk and lemon, then drizzle olive oil slowly while whisking until it thickens and turns creamy. Going slow is what keeps it from breaking.
  4. Toss and finishToss chilled romaine with dressing until every leaf is coated, then fold in parm and croutons. Shave more parmesan over the top.
