Cinnamon Rolls, and Where to Source It

Real butter and a good, fresh ground cinnamon are what separate a great roll from a gummy one, so buy the best of both. The one technique is the two rises — you let the dough double once after kneading and again after shaping, which is what gives the rolls their pillowy pull.

Can you make Cinnamon Rolls in an air fryer? — Yes

Yes — this is exactly what an air fryer does well. Work in a single layer so the air can move, and shake or flip halfway for even crisping. It runs a touch faster than the oven, so check early.

Part Two
The Method
  1. Bloom and mixWarm the milk to 110F, stir in the yeast and a spoon of sugar, and wait 5 minutes until foamy. Mix with the flour, remaining sugar, an egg, softened butter, and 1 teaspoon salt into a soft dough.
  2. First riseKnead 8 minutes until smooth and elastic, then cover and let rise in a warm spot until doubled, about 1 hour.
  3. Fill and rollRoll the dough into a large rectangle, spread with softened butter, and scatter the cinnamon mixed with sugar. Roll up tight from the long side and cut into 12 rolls.
  4. Second riseArrange in a buttered dish, cover, and let rise again until puffy, about 45 minutes.
  5. Bake and glazeBake at 350F for 22 to 26 minutes until golden and set in the center. Cool 10 minutes, then spread with a glaze of powdered sugar, cream cheese, and a splash of milk.
