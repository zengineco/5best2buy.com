Amazon and the big grocery chains sell what's easy to ship at scale. That quietly leaves out almost everything good: the small makers, the regional stuff, the things a chain dropped when it "centralized suppliers," the products that exist only because one person cared more about the thing than the margin. This is a map back to all of it — starting with food. Pick an aisle.

Every maker we feature clears four bars: small or independent, genuinely hard to get on the big platforms, actually good, and we're honest about why they're hard to find. No one pays for a spot — the list is never for sale. What we're up against isn't a store, it's consolidation: the slow squeeze that buys up the good small brands or pushes them off the shelf. We point you back to them.
